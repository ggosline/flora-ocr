# *Synclisia*

**Authority**: Benth., in Benth. & Hook. f., Gen. plant. **1**: 36 (1862).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Twining lianas**. Leaves simple; **petiole straight**, slightly
thickened at base and apex; blade entire-margined. Inflorescences axillary;
male flowers often solitary; female flowers solitary or in 3-flowered
racemes. Male flowers: pedicel slender; **sepals 9**, the 6 outer
bracteoliform, the **3 inner linear-lanceolate, fused into a cylindrical
tube and free at apex**; petals 6, small, suborbicular, glabrous; stamens
6 to 9, fused at base, anthers dehiscing subobliquely. Female flowers:
sepals and petals like the male; carpels 7 to 20. Fruits with
obovoid-ellipsoidal mericarps, gibbous at apex, **densely
ferruginous-pilose**; endocarp crustaceous. Seeds horseshoe-shaped;
cotyledons unequal.

A mainly central African genus of **2 species**, both in Gabon.
