# *Tiliacora*

**Authority**: Colebr., Trans. Linn. Soc. London **18**: 53 (1822).
**Synonyms**:
- *Glossophis* Pierre, Bull. Soc. Linn. Paris n.s. **1**: 82–85 (1898),
  *pro parte*, excl. *G. jollyana* Pierre.
- *Sebicea* Pierre ex Diels in Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 59 (1910).

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Robust lianas**. Leaves simple, petiolate; blade entire-margined.
Inflorescences axillary or **cauline**, in pseudoracemes (rarely panicles),
composed of few-flowered cymules; male sometimes reduced to a capituliform
axillary cyme. Male flowers: **sepals 6 to 12**, the outer bracteiform and
of decreasing size, the inner valvate and much larger, subfleshy; petals 3
to 6, oblong to obovate or unguiculate and obcordate above; stamens 3 to
9, with broad and flattened or cylindrical filaments, free or ± fused,
anthers introrse, bilocular, dehiscing longitudinally. Female flowers:
sepals and petals like the male; staminodes absent; **carpels 6 to 30**,
inserted on a gynophore; stigmas often curved. Fruits with
ovoid-ellipsoidal mericarps, curved or not, smooth or verrucose, bearing
the often rudimentary stigma; mesocarp fleshy; endocarp coriaceous,
crustaceous, sulcate; condyle septiform. Seeds with little or no ruminate
endosperm.

A Palaeotropical genus of about **18 species**, 3 in Asia and ±15 in
Africa, of which **3 in Gabon**.
