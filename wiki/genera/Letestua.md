# *Letestua* Lecomte

**Authority**: Lecomte, *Notul. Syst.* **4**: 5 (1920)  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

The genus is placed in the Mimusopoideae by its double calyx of **3 + 3
sepals**, but in the Gabon treatment it stands apart from the other African
members of that group by having **12-18 petals**, each with **2 dorsal
appendages**, **12-18 stamens**, an ovary with about **18 locules**, and
above all by the **absence of staminodes**. The seed is flattened and marked
by a long narrow ventral scar.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 41-43  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment regards the genus as effectively monotypic in practice.
Lecomte's second species, *L. floribunda*, is reduced here to synonymy because
the floral differences were considered too slight and based on inadequate
material.

## Notes

- The treatment says the genus appears confined to **Gabon and the Mayombe**
  in the state of knowledge available in 1961.
- The wood is described as **extremely hard**, reflected in the epithet
  *durissima* and in vernacular names glossed as "stone wood".
