# *Chasmanthera*

**Authority**: Hochst., Flora **27(1)**: 21 (1844).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Lianas with **verrucose rhytidome and pendent branches**. Leaves simple;
blade with palmate venation. Inflorescences axillary, **pendent**, the
**male in panicles of 3- to 5-flowered cymules** with persistent filiform
bracts, the **female racemose**. Male flowers: sepals 6; petals 6; **stamens
6, fused**, thecae dehiscing longitudinally. Female flowers: sepals and
petals ± like the male; staminodes 6; **carpels 3**; style short, bilobed
at apex. Fruits with 3 subglobose to broadly ellipsoidal mericarps;
**endocarp with 3 apical teeth**. Seeds with ruminate endosperm.

A **monospecific** genus of tropical Africa.

**Author**: Breteler F.J.
**Pages**: 38–40
**Source**: `sources/Menispermaceae_vol60_liteparse`

Notable: Breteler formally synonymizes *C. welwitschii* Troupin under *C.
dependens*. Troupin himself had expressed doubts about its validity in his
1962 monograph and his subsequent identifications at Paris suggest he
accepted only one species in the genus.

Bibliography cited: Troupin (1956, 1962).

## Notes

- One of the most widespread Menispermaceae in tropical Africa: from
  Sierra Leone east to Ethiopia/Somalia and south to Angola/Zambia.
