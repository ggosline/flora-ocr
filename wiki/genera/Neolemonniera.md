# *Neolemonniera* Heine

**Authority**: Heine  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

The flowers are close to those of [[Lecomtedoxa]]: **5-merous**, with **5
corolla lobes**, each bearing **2 appendages**, **5 stamens**, **5
staminodes**, and a **5-locular** ovary. The fruits are unknown. The genus is
most distinctive vegetatively: the leaves are **stipulate**, arranged in
spaced **pseudo-whorls**, and conspicuously **striated** by parallel sclereid
bundles beneath the epidermis.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 73-74  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment explains the nomenclatural replacement of *Le-Monniera* by
*Neolemonniera* because the former had already been used for a fungus.

## Notes

- The source says three species were then known in the genus: one Ivorian, one
  shared by Cameroon and Ivory Coast, and one Gabonese.
- The floral appendages are said to look less like true dorsal appendages than
  in most Mimusopoideae, and more like lobing of the corolla segments.
