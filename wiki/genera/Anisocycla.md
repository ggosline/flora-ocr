# *Anisocycla*

**Authority**: Baill., Bull. Soc. Linn. Paris **135**: 1078 (1893).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Erect shrubs** (one of only two non-climbing genera in Gabonese
Menispermaceae, with [[Penianthus]]). Leaves simple; blade cartaceous,
papyraceous or membranaceous. Inflorescences in corymbiform panicles or
in cymes. Male flowers sessile or shortly pedicellate; **sepals 9 to 24**,
the outer bracteiform, the **3 inner much larger than the rest**; petals
3 to 6, small; **stamens 9 to 18, with filaments fused into a synandrium**,
anthers subsessile, dehiscing transversely. Female flowers: sepals like
the male; petals 3; staminodes 3 or absent; carpels 3 to 6. Fruits with
ovoid or subglobose mericarps. Seeds exalbuminous; cotyledons thick and
curved.

A tropical African genus of **6 species**, three in Madagascar; on the
continent ranging from Cameroon to Angola southward and to Zimbabwe and
Mozambique south-eastward. **One species in Gabon.**

## Notes

- *Anisocycla jollyana* is the **smallest non-climbing** Menispermaceae in
  Gabon (the only other erect species in the family is the much larger
  [[Penianthus_longifolius]], 3–4.5 m tall).
