# *Zeyherella* Pierre ex Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Authority**: Pierre ex Aubrev. & Pellegr.  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Small trees with stipulate leaves that are ferruginous or grey-tomentose below
and have numerous close lateral veins. Flowers are fasciculate on old wood and
may be female. Sepals **5**. Corolla usually reddish, with **5 oblong lobes**
and a short tube. Stamens **5**, inserted at the level of lobe fusion; free
filaments separate the genus from [[Englerophytum]]. Staminodes are absent or
rudimentary. Fruits are one-seeded, and the seed has a narrow linear scar on
part of the ventral face.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 98-104  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment says two species occur in Gabon, with a third riparian Congo
species, [[Zeyherella_longepedicellata]], probably expected but not yet
collected in Gabon.

## Notes

- The source rejects the reduction of *Zeyherella* into `Bequaertiodendron`.
- A probable additional Gabonese river species is discussed, but not accepted
  on current material.
