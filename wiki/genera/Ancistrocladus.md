# *Ancistrocladus* Wall.

**Family**: [[Ancistrocladaceae]]

Numer. List : 1052 (1829), nom. cons.

The sole genus of the family [[Ancistrocladaceae]], with about 16 species in
the evergreen forests of tropical Africa, southern India and Sri Lanka, and
Southeast Asia (through Hainan to Sumatra). Four species are recorded from
Gabon.

## Diagnosis

Juvenile leaves grouped in terminal rosettes; adult leaves differentiated into
those arranged along the main stems and those at the tips of the branchlets,
sometimes themselves grouped in terminal rosettes. Secondary nervation
brochidodromous (pinnate, with the secondary nerves forming loops); tertiary
nervation irregularly areolate, often with **glands or large craters at the
junctions of groups of nervules**, visible on both faces of the leaf but
especially on the upper.

Inflorescences subsessile to pedunculate; bracts subtending the axes and
pedicels often with one or several abaxial glands. Flowers actinomorphic or
with unequal sepals; pedicels absent or — more usually — present and articulate
at the apex; sepals imbricate, equal or with the 3 outer larger, sometimes
with 1 to several abaxial glands; petals with imbricate, convolute, or
intermediate aestivation; stamens arranged in one or two whorls, the filaments
equal or of two lengths; anther thecae usually separated by a well-developed
connective.

Fruits with the accrescent sepals subequal and coriaceous to chartaceous, or
unequal, papery and aliform. Seeds with a hard endosperm.

## Key to the species in Gabon

Translated from Gereau & Walters (2022). Couplet leads → species.

- **1.** Leaves at the tips of the branchlets very narrowly obovate-elliptic
  to narrowly obovate-elliptic, L/W 3.5–15.5; adult leaves with usually
  oblong large craters (0.5–)2–4 × 0.5–1 mm; bracts at the start of anthesis
  narrowly ovate-elliptic to narrowly obovate-elliptic and prolonged at the
  base; at the end of anthesis becoming cochleariform to claviform by
  enlargement of the abaxial gland and incurving of the apex
  → [[Ancistrocladus_letestui|*A. letestui*]]
- **1′.** Leaves at the tips of the branchlets elliptic to narrowly
  obovate-elliptic, obovate, or broadly elliptic, L/W 2–4.8; adult leaves with
  large circular to ovate craters 0.2–1 mm in diameter; bracts at the start of
  anthesis narrowly ovate-elliptic to ovate or triangular, not prolonged at
  the base, not becoming cochleariform to claviform at the end of anthesis
  → **2**
  - **2.** Adult leaves with circular to ovate craters 0.3–1 mm in diameter;
    sepals **unequal** in flower (3 larger 3.5–5.1 × 1.4–2.5 mm, 2 smaller
    3.2–4.2 × 2–2.9 mm); petals oblong-ovate, ±8.1 × 3.2 mm; sepals papery
    and very unequal in fruit (3 larger 28–42 × 13–16 mm, 2 smaller 5–16 ×
    4–6 mm)
    → [[Ancistrocladus_guineensis|*A. guineensis*]]
  - **2′.** Adult leaves with circular craters 0.2–0.5 mm in diameter; sepals
    subequal in flower, 4–6 × 2–3 mm; petals suborbicular to obovate, 2–4 ×
    2–3.5 mm; sepals thickened-chartaceous or coriaceous to woody, subequal
    to unequal in fruit, 5–10 × 3–8 mm
    → **3**
    - **3.** Adult leaves subcoriaceous to coriaceous, those at the tips of
      the branchlets truncate to subacute and sometimes abruptly acuminate at
      apex; peduncle relatively robust; petals obovate, ±4 × 3–3.5 mm; styles
      3; fruit turbinate, 8–15 × 10–20 mm (excluding wings)
      → [[Ancistrocladus_congolensis|*A. congolensis*]]
    - **3′.** Adult leaves chartaceous to subcoriaceous, those at the tips of
      the branchlets usually acute to acuminate at apex (sometimes obtuse to
      rounded); peduncle slender; petals suborbicular, 2–3 × 2–3 mm;
      **style 1**; fruit patelliform, 4–5 × 7–8 mm (excluding wings)
      → [[Ancistrocladus_ealaensis|*A. ealaensis*]]

## Treatments

### Vol 60 (2022) — Gereau R.E. & Walters G.M.

Source: `sources/Ancistrocladaceae_vol60_liteparse` · pp. 2–7 of the family
treatment.

The first comprehensive Gabonese treatment in the Margraf series. Treats all
four species; provides a key, full descriptions, distributions, and ecology;
illustrates *A. congolensis* and *A. ealaensis* together (Planche 1, Figure 1)
and *A. guineensis* with the classic Trevithick / Hutchinson, Dalziel & Keay
plate (Planche 2). No plate is provided for *A. letestui*.
