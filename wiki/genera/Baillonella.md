# *Baillonella* Pierre

**Authority**: Pierre, *Notes bot. Sapot.*: 13 (1890)  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

This is another mimusopoid genus with a double calyx of **4 + 4 sepals**, but
it is set apart by the seed, whose broad scar covers almost the whole ventral
face, by the presence of **stipules**, and by the thin seed coat. Each of the
**8 corolla lobes** bears **2 dorsal appendages**; **8 developed staminodes**
alternate with the **8 stamens**; the ovary has **8 locules**.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 55-61  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment calls the genus monotypic in the Cameroon-Gabon forest block,
but also discusses a southern form treated as
[[Baillonella_toxisperma|*B. toxisperma*]] var. *obovata* from Loango and the
lower Kouilou.

## Notes

- The account contains an extended nomenclatural discussion defending the name
  *toxisperma* against later attempts to replace it with `djave`.
- The genus is centred on the famous **moabi**, one of the largest emergent
  trees in African humid forest.
