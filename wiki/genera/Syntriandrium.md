# *Syntriandrium*

**Authority**: Engl., Bot. Jahrb. Syst. **26**: 412, tab. 24, 25 (1899).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Suffrutescent lianas**. Leaves **heteromorphic, simple to lobed or 2- or
3-foliolate** (unique among Gabonese Menispermaceae). Inflorescences
axillary; **male in panicles, female in racemes**. Male flowers: sepals 6,
free, the outer small; petals 3, free, small; **stamens 3, with completely
fused filaments**, anthers dehiscing transversely. Female flowers: sepals
like the male; petals 6; staminodes absent; **carpels 3**. Fruits with
subellipsoidal mericarps; endocarp cartaceous, straight. Seeds albuminous.

A **monospecific** genus of tropical Africa.

## Notes

- The diagnostic vegetative feature is the **heteromorphic, sometimes
  trifoliolate leaves** — unique in Gabonese Menispermaceae, where leaves
  are otherwise simple.
