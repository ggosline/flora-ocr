# *Hua* Pierre

**Authority**: Pierre, drawing by Delpy in herb. Pierre, based on Klaine 2859b, distributed in 1904 or earlier; *Ann. Mus. Congo Belge, Bot., sér. 5* **1**: 288 (1906)  
**Family**: [[Huaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Shrub, glabrous or only sparsely hairy, with leaves bearing small circular
glands on the lower surface and an impressed midrib above. Flowers are
plurifasciculate; the calyx consists of 4 or 5 free sepals, each with a
marginal row of glands on the inner face; the petals are peltate and
long-clawed; filaments are free, anthers are muticous and glabrous, and the
ovary has a single basal ovule with simple hairs. The fruit is capsular and
opens by 5 valves; the seed is whitish-pubescent.

The genus is monotypic and occurs in Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, and
the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

## Treatments

### Vol 38 (2009)

**Author in treatment**: Yves Azizet Issembe  
**Pages**: 30-33  
**Source**: `sources/Huaceae_vol38_liteparse`

The treatment presents *Hua* as the dehiscent-fruited counterpart to
[[Afrostyrax]], distinguished by its free sepals, peltate petals, and single
basal ovule. Only one species is recognised.

## Notes

Although monotypic, *Hua* is structurally distinctive within Huaceae and is a
good example of how strongly the source relies on reproductive characters to
separate small central-African woody genera.
