# *Cissampelos*

**Authority**: L., Sp. pl. **2**: 1031 (1753).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Twining herbs**. Leaves simple; **blade peltate**. Inflorescences
axillary, pendent, the female with much larger bracts than the male. Male
flowers: sepals 4, free; petals 4, fused into a cup; stamens 4, fused.
Female flowers: with **1 sepal and 1 opposite petal**; **single carpel**.
Fruits with horseshoe-shaped endocarp.

A pantropical genus of **20 to 25 species**, of which 13 in Africa,
mainly in eastern, south-eastern and southern Africa. **One species in
Gabon.**

Notes: The pantropical *C. pareira* L. has been reported from Gabon in
the literature but no herbarium voucher has been found to confirm its
presence. It differs from *C. owariensis* by its much shorter male
inflorescence (≤ 4 cm), but the two are often confused.

Bibliography cited: Troupin (1956, 1962).

## Notes

- *Cissampelos*, like [[Perichasma]], is characterised by the **single
  carpel** in the female flower — unusual in Menispermaceae, where 3 or
  more carpels are typical.
