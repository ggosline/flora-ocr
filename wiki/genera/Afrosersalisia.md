# *Afrosersalisia* A. Chev.

**Authority**: A. Chev.  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

The corolla has **5 oblong lobes**. Stamens have filaments inserted at the
corolla throat and are as long as or nearly as long as the corolla lobes. The
ovary is **5-locular**. Fruits are one-seeded, with a seed marked by a **very
broad ventral scar** and lacking endosperm. A key feature in the treatment is
that the slightly fused sepals persist as a **small cupule** at the base of
the fruit.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 109-112  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment says two species were then recognized in West and Central Africa,
with [[Afrosersalisia_afzelii]] the large humid-forest tree ranging from Sierra
Leone to Gabon.

## Notes

- The source treats this genus as close to [[Synsepalum]] and [[Pachystela]],
  but kept apart by the scale-like staminodes and the cupulate persistent
  calyx at the fruit base.
