# *Omphalocarpum* P. Beauv.

**Authority**: P. Beauv.  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

The genus is one of the most distinctive African Sapotaceae in the treatment.
Leaves are erect and clustered at the ends of branchlets, but the key feature
is the **cauliflorous inflorescences** and especially the **large woody fruits
borne on the trunk**. Flowers may be hermaphrodite or female. The corolla has
**5-7 lobes**, stamens are grouped in **bundles of 3-6** opposite the lobes,
and the ovary has **5 to very many locules**. Fruits are woody and
subglobose, with **5-30 seeds** that have a linear ventral scar.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 79-84  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment argues that many previously described species probably collapse
into synonymy because some were based only on seeds, which are often too
similar across the genus. For the forest flora from Ivory Coast to Gabon, the
author keeps only five species, of which three occur in Gabon.

## Notes

- The source says at least some older names based only on seeds are doubtful.
- The Gabon flora is reduced here to **3 species**, including the local
  [[Omphalocarpum_le-testui]].
- A fruit-only taxon, `O. ogouense`, is mentioned but not accepted as one of
  the well-defined Gabonese species in the treatment.
