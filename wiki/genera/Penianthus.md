# *Penianthus*

**Authority**: Miers, Ann. Mag. Nat. Hist., ser. 3, **13**: 124 (1864).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Erect shrubs** (rare habit in the family), glabrous in the vegetative
parts. Leaves simple; petiole thickened at the ends; blade entire-margined.
Inflorescences in axillary, ramiflorous or cauliflorous capitula. Male
flowers: sepals 6 to 10, thick, in 2 or 3 cycles of (2)3 or 4, the median
the largest, the inner barely distinct from the petals or reduced; petals
6 to 8, unequal; **stamens 5 to 9, free or nearly so**, anther 2-locular;
gynoecium rudimentary. Female flowers: sepals 6; petals absent or 6, very
small; staminodes absent or 6; carpels 3; stigmas spreading. Fruits:
mericarps with coriaceous exocarp; mesocarp pulpy; endocarp fibrous-cartaceous.
Seeds exalbuminous.

A Guineo-Congolian genus of **4 species**, one in Gabon.

## Notes

- One of only **two non-climbing** genera of Menispermaceae in Gabon (the
  other is the much smaller [[Anisocycla]], 30–70 cm).
- The **largest non-climbing Menispermaceae** in Gabon, with very hard
  wood occasionally used for timber.
