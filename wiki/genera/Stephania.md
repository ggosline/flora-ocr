# *Stephania*

**Authority**: Lour., Fl. cochinch. **2**: 608 (1790).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Lianas with sometimes succulent stems**. Leaves simple; **blade peltate**.
Inflorescences: **male in panicles** with very numerous flowers, **female
unknown** but infructescences in panicles. Male flowers: sepals 6,
**subequal**, free; petals 3, free; **stamens 3, entirely fused into a
discoid, stipitate, peltate synandrium**, thecae dehiscing transversely.
Female flowers unknown. Fruits with smooth mericarps; endocarp compressed,
with echinulate ridges.

A genus of about **30 species** of the Old World tropics and subtropics,
mostly in Asia and Australia; **5 species in Africa**, one in Gabon.

## Notes

- Two recent collections from Moyen-Ogooué and Woleu-Ntem (peltate leaves,
  male flowers) show some affinity with *Stephania* but have markedly
  unequal sepals (vs. subequal in *Stephania*). Their identity remains
  unresolved and may represent an additional, undescribed taxon.
