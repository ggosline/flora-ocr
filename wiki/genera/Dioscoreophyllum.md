# *Dioscoreophyllum*

**Authority**: Engl., Pflanzenw. Ost-Afrikas, **C**: 181 (1895).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Twining herbs**. Leaves simple; blade **3(5)-lobed, deeply cordate at
base**. Inflorescences in **axillary racemes**. Flowers solitary; sepals
6, free, glabrous; **petals absent**. Male flowers: **stamens 3 or 6**,
fused into a **± cylindrical or ± hemispherical synandrium**, sessile or
stipitate, anthers ± ovoid with parallel, longitudinally dehiscent
thecae. Female flowers: staminodes absent; carpels 3. Fruits with
glabrous mericarps.

A tropical African genus of **two species**, both present in Gabon.

## Notes

- *D. volkensii* is the **"West African serendipity berry"** or **Guinea
  potato** — its rhizome is eaten cooked like a potato and its fruits are
  edible.
- Note the **stamen number discrepancy** in *D. gossweileri*: Exell's
  original protologue described 6 fused stamens, but Gabonese (and DRC,
  RC) collections all have only 3. Breteler suspects Exell was mistaken;
  examination of the type (Gossweiler 7639) is needed.
