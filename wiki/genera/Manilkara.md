# *Manilkara* Adans.

**Authority**: Adans., *Fam. pl.* **2**: 166 (1763), *nom. cons.*  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

The genus is immediately recognisable in the Gabon treatment by its floral
architecture: a double calyx of **3 + 3 sepals**, a corolla of **6 petals**
each bearing **2 dorsal appendages**, **6 stamens**, and **6 staminodes**.
The ovary usually has about **12 locules**, more rarely about **6** as in
[[Manilkara_le_testui]]. Fruits, when known, are small ellipsoid berries with
1-2 seeds; the seeds are marked by a narrow basiventral scar.

The treatment notes that African humid-forest *Manilkara* can be grouped by
leaf architecture into species with many fine parallel lateral veins and those
with larger blades and more spaced venation. It also stresses that pedicel
form and floral details are often more reliable than foliage alone.

## Key to species

1. Leaves very large, **25-30 cm** long, long-petiolate; ovary with about
   **6** locules; species known from a single specimen  
   -> [[Manilkara_le_testui|*M. le-testui*]]
1. Leaves smaller; ovary with about **10-13** locules  
   1. Small leaves **5-9 cm** long, lateral veins very faint; species of the
      small-leaved complex  
      1. Fine lateral venation and nervules deeply impressed in the thick leaf
         blade  
         -> [[Manilkara_microphylla|*M. microphylla*]]
      1. Lateral venation not deeply impressed  
         -> [[Manilkara_welwitschii|*M. welwitschii*]]
   1. Leaves broader or larger

         often yellow or red  
         -> [[Manilkara_lacera|*M. lacera*]]
      1. Inland primary-forest tree with larger flowers and stout pedicels  
         -> [[Manilkara_fouilloyana|*M. fouilloyana*]]

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 27-40  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

This opening genus block of the Sapotaceae monograph mixes clear floral
diagnosis with repeated warnings that foliage alone can be deceptive. Several
species are still imperfectly known, and the author explicitly treats the
coastal and inland forms around [[Manilkara_lacera]] as a difficult continuum.

## Notes

- The OCR alternates between `Manikara` and `Manilkara`; the accepted page name
  here follows the conserved spelling **Manilkara**.
- The treatment repeatedly contrasts coastal ecotypes with inland forest forms,
  especially around [[Manilkara_lacera]].
- Figure coverage is partial: only [[Manilkara_le_testui]],
  [[Manilkara_fouilloyana]], and [[Manilkara_microphylla]] are represented on
  the extracted plates used in this first chunk.
