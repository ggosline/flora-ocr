# *Tridesmostemon* Engl.

**Authority**: Engl.  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Flowers are fasciculate in the **leaf axils**, not on the trunk. There are **5
free imbricate sepals**, **5 petals** united at the base, and **10 or 15
stamens** with filaments united into **5 thick, hairy phalanges** shorter than
the petals. The staminodes are hairy and **3-toothed**. The ovary has **10
locules**, and the fruits are spheroidal with about **10 seeds**.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 86-89  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment explicitly rejects *Nzidora* A. Chev. as distinct from
*Tridesmostemon*, arguing that the number of stamens per phalanx is not
generic. It also notes a fruit-only Gabonese tree as *Tridesmostemon
congoense*, but the fully treated Gabonese species page here is
[[Tridesmostemon_omphalocarpoides]].

## Notes

- The genus is separated from [[Omphalocarpum]] chiefly by **axillary flowers**
  and **stamens in phalanges**.
- The treatment says closely related Congolese species had been described, but
  keeps the Gabonese material here under [[Tridesmostemon_omphalocarpoides]].
