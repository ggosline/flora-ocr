# *Lecomtedoxa* Pierre ex Dubard

**Authority**: Pierre ex Dubard  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

The genus is defined in the treatment by **5 imbricate sepals**, a corolla
whose **5 lobes** each bear **2 dorsal appendages**, the presence of
**staminodes**, and especially by the **dry dehiscent one-seeded fruits**.
The single seed is elongated and flattened, with a **linear scar running the
full length of the ventral face**.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 62-72  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The account treats *Lecomtedoxa* as a distinctively Gabonese, probably
littoral lineage of hard-wood Sapotaceae. It also explains why Chevalier's
genus *Walkeria* is not maintained: the flower structure of its type species
matches *Lecomtedoxa* closely enough that Aubreville preferred to unite them
provisionally pending better herbarium material.

## Notes

- The treatment says the genus is **typically Gabonese** and probably limited
  to a **littoral range**.
- Several species are explicitly called **imperfectly known** or are based
  only on type material.
- The wood is repeatedly described as **very hard** across the genus.
