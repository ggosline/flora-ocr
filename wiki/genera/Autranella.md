# *Autranella* A. Chev. ex Aubr. & Pellegr.

**Authority**: A. Chev. ex Aubr. & Pellegr.  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

The treatment regards the genus as unmistakable from both seed and flower.
The seed is **large, heavy, and very hard**, with a **subrectangular
basiventral scar** occupying about half of the ventral face. Flowers have a
double calyx of **4 + 4 sepals**, a tubular corolla with **8 lobes**, each
bearing **2 dorsal appendages**, **8 stamens**, short staminodes on the rim of
the tube, and an **8-locular** ovary.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 44-48  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The genus account combines generic diagnosis with an extended discussion of
species limits. Aubreville considered the genus well defined, but thought the
material still too sparse to decide confidently whether more than one species
should be maintained in Central Africa.

## Notes

- The source explicitly says that if multiple species are eventually accepted,
  the application of the name *Autranella congolensis* may need re-examination.
- The commercial timber name **mukulungu** is attached to the Gabonese trees
  discussed here.
