# *Leptoterantha*

**Authority**: Louis ex Troupin, Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **19**:
426, t. 29 (1949).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Twining lianas, completely glabrous**. Leaves simple, petiolate; blade
entire-margined, with **numerous, ± parallel lateral nerves**.
Inflorescences in axillary panicles. Flowers: sepals 6, free, similar in
both sexes, the outer very small. Male flowers: **stamens 6** with fused
filaments and longitudinally dehiscent anthers. Female flowers: staminodes
6, very small; carpels 3; stigmas oblique, 3-fid. Fruits with ellipsoidal
mericarps; endocarp smooth.

A **monospecific** genus of central Africa.

## Notes

- The diagnostic feature is the **dense, ± parallel tertiary venation
  intermingled with the principal lateral nerves** — unique among Gabonese
  Menispermaceae.
