# *Afrostyrax* Perkins & Gilg

**Authority**: Perkins & Gilg, in Perkins, *Bot. Jahrb. Syst.* **43**: 216 (1909)  
**Family**: [[Huaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Shrubs to trees with stellate hairs or scales on the vegetative and floral
parts. The petiole is cylindrical when fresh and grooved or ribbed when dry,
and the leaf blade bears small glandular pits along the marginal vein. Flowers
are solitary to fasciculate; the sepals are fused and tear irregularly into
2-5 lobes that carry 1 or 2 glandular spots on the inner face. Petals are 5,
shortly clawed, staminal filaments are united at the base, the anthers are
apiculate, and the ovary bears 5 or 6 ovules with stellate hairs. Fruits are
indehiscent, usually ovoid, and the seeds are glabrous.

The genus comprises 3 species in humid tropical Africa, from Ghana eastward and
southward to the Democratic Republic of the Congo; all 3 occur in Gabon.

## Treatments

### Vol 38 (2009)

**Author in treatment**: Yves Azizet Issembe  
**Pages**: 26-31  
**Source**: `sources/Huaceae_vol38_liteparse`

The treatment covers the whole genus for Gabon and separates the species by
leaf indumentum, lateral-vein count, revolute versus flat margins, and anther
and filament morphology. All three species share the alliacous bark-and-seed
use noted repeatedly in the species accounts.

## Notes

The genus is morphologically cohesive but easy to distinguish from [[Hua]] in
the source treatment: *Afrostyrax* is consistently stellate-hairy, has fused
sepals, non-peltate petals, apiculate anthers, and an indehiscent fruit.
