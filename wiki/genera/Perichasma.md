# *Perichasma*

**Authority**: Miers, Ann. Mag. Nat. Hist., ser. 3, **18**: 22 (1866).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Lianas**. Leaves simple; **blade peltate**. Inflorescences in axillary
panicles or on second-year, defoliated branches. Flowers: sepals
imbricate; petals 3, alike. Male flowers: sepals 6; **stamens 6, fused,
with anthers in a horizontal ring** dehiscing transversely; pistillode
absent. Female flowers: sepals 3; staminodes absent; **single carpel**.
Fruits horseshoe-shaped when dry. Seeds albuminous, with cylindrical,
curved embryo.

A genus of **2 species**: one endemic to Cameroon and one (*P. laetificata*)
more widely distributed in tropical Africa.

  carpel** in the female flower — atypical for Menispermaceae.
