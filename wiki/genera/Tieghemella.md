# *Tieghemella* Pierre

**Authority**: Pierre, *Notes bot. Sapot.*: 18 (1890)  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

The genus comprises very large trees with a double calyx of **4 + 4 sepals**,
an **8-lobed** corolla whose lobes each bear **2 dorsal appendages**, **8
stamens**, **8 staminodes**, and an **8-locular** ovary. In the treatment it
is placed next to [[Baillonella]], but separated by its **very thick, hard
seed coat**, the absence of stipules, and a different leaf venation pattern.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 49-54  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment recognizes two close species in the genus overall: the West
African *T. heckelii* and the Gabon-Cameroon [[Tieghemella_africana|*T.
africana*]]. They are said to differ clearly by flowers and seeds but less
obviously by leaves.

## Notes

- The source repeatedly treats [[Tieghemella_africana|*T. africana*]] as one
  of the great emergent trees of Gabonese primary forest.
- The commercial timber name **douka** is tied to the Gabonese species.
