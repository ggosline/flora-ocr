# *Tulestea* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Authority**: Aubrev. & Pellegr.  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Shrubs with fasciculate axillary flowers. Sepals are free or nearly so. The
corolla has a **very short tube** and **oblong lobes**. Stamens have **very
short filaments** inserted about the level of corolla-lobe fusion; the anthers
are extrorse, longer than the filaments, somewhat acute-apiculate, and hairy
internally. Staminodes are reduced to scales. The ovary is hairy and
**5-locular**.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 105-108  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment recognizes three Gabonese species and suggests a probable fourth
still poorly known.

## Notes

- The source treats [[Afrosersalisia]] as the nearest genus, but separates it
  by its partially fused sepals and long-filamented stamens.
- All three accepted species are Gabonese end-of-branch or axillary-flowering
  shrubs rather than large canopy trees.
