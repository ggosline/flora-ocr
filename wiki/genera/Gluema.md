# *Gluema* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Authority**: Aubrev. & Pellegr.  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Placed in the Bumelioid group by the treatment, the genus has a **simple
5-merous calyx**, a corolla with **5 lobes** each carrying **2 dorsal
appendages**, **5 extrorse stamens** opposite the petals, and **5 staminodes**
which are also opposite the stamens and at least in bud are **coalescent**
around the style. The fruit is dehiscent, one-seeded, and the seed has a
narrowly oblong ventral scar.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 75-78  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment distinguishes the genus readily from [[Lecomtedoxa]] despite the
shared dehiscent one-seeded fruit, chiefly by the position and fusion of the
staminodes.

## Notes

- The source explicitly keeps the Gabonese material in the same species as the
  Ivorian one despite minor differences.
- The latex is said to be used as **glue** in Ivory Coast, matching the genus
  name.
