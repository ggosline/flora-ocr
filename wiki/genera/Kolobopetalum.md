# *Kolobopetalum*

**Authority**: Engl., Bot. Jahrb. Syst. **26**: 410 (1899).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**(Sub-)woody lianas**, rarely twining herbs, **completely glabrous**.
Leaves simple; petiole curved and/or twisted at base; blade subcoriaceous,
margin entire or sometimes with a few sparse, faint teeth. Inflorescences
**subracemose or in ample, 1- to 3-times branched panicles**. Flowers:
sepals 6; petals 6, ± truncate at apex. Male flowers: **stamens (3)6**,
thecae dehiscing semi-circularly and apically. Female flowers: staminodes
6; carpels 3. Fruits with ellipsoidal, ± smooth mericarps; mesocarp
fleshy, sticky; **endocarp spiny**. Seeds subreniform, albuminous.

A tropical African genus of **3 or 4 species**, all present in Gabon.

## Notes

- *Kolobopetalum synsepalum* was described very recently (Breteler 2021)
  on the basis of its unique fused inner male sepals.
- A fourth, undescribed entity (*K.* spec. nov.) is known from a single
  sterile collection from Moyen-Ogooué; placement in *Kolobopetalum* (vs.
  *Rhigiocarya*) is provisional pending fertile material.
