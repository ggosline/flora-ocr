# *Tacca* J.R.Forst. & G.Forst.

**Authority**: J.R.Forst. & G.Forst., *Char. gen. pl.*: 35 (1775)  
**Family**: [[Taccaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Perennial herbs whose young parts are hairy but become glabrescent, with a
solid rhizome and cataphylls at the base of the petiole and flowering scape.
Leaves are arranged in a rosette or more loosely spaced; petioles are ribbed
and canaliculate distally; blades are herbaceous to papery and glossy above.
The flowering scape is ribbed, solid or rarely hollow, and bears 4-12
involucral bracts; flowers have 6-ribbed pedicels, a usually dark perianth,
short filaments, a 6-ribbed obpyramidal ovary, and often petaloid, reflexed,
obcordate style lobes. The fleshy fruit dehisces irregularly and is packed with
many ribbed seeds.

The genus comprises 12 species with the same broad distribution as the family.
In Gabon only one species is recognised.

## Treatments

### Vol 38 (2009)

**Author in treatment**: Marc S.M. Sosef  
**Pages**: 60-63  
**Source**: `sources/Taccaceae_vol38_liteparse`

The Gabon treatment is monographic at the national scale and focuses entirely
on [[Tacca_leontopetaloides|*Tacca leontopetaloides*]], a conspicuous geophyte
of open and disturbed habitats. The account is useful both morphologically and
ethnobotanically, especially for the rhizome-derived starch.

## Notes

The source emphasises the remarkable vegetative variability of the dissected
leaf lobes and the importance of the long filiform floral bracts in field
recognition.
