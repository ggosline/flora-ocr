# *Triclisia*

**Authority**: Benth., in Benth. & Hook. f., Gen. pl. **1**: 39 (1862).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Twining lianas**. Leaves simple; petiole often thickened at base and
apex, straight or geniculate at apex. Inflorescences cauliflorous,
ramiflorous or axillary, in cymules or in small panicles, multi-flowered.
Male flowers: **sepals 9 to 18**, free, **densely hairy outside**, the
outer bracteoliform and small, the median larger, the **3 inner valvate
and ± coriaceous**; petals sometimes absent, 3 to 6; stamens 6, free or
slightly fused at base, anthers introrse, with often produced connective;
**pistillode a tuft of hairs**. Female flowers: sepals like the male;
petals absent or up to 3; staminodes absent; **carpels 10 to 40**,
pubescent. Fruits with ovoid to ellipsoidal mericarps, slightly flattened,
stipitate, hairy to glabrous; mesocarp fleshy-pulpy; **endocarp with marked
condyle**.

A tropical African genus of about **16 species**, of which **5 in Gabon**
(the **largest Menispermaceae genus** in the country).

## Treatments

### Vol 60 (2022)

**Author**: Breteler F.J.
**Pages**: 81–88
**Source**: `sources/Menispermaceae_vol60_liteparse`

Includes the description of **two new species** (*T. gabonensis* with
Jongkind, type from Haut-Ogooué; and *T. megacarpa*, type from Ngounié).
Of the five Gabonese species, **three are endemic** (*T. gabonensis*, *T.
hypochrysea*) or near-endemic, and *T. megacarpa* has its type locality in
Gabon.

Bibliography cited: Jongkind (2017); Troupin (1962).

## Notes

- Breteler reverts the Troupin (1962) lumping of *T. dictyophylla* and *T.
  gilletii*, accepting *T. dictyophylla* in a wider sense and segregating
  the West/Central African material with large beakless fruits as the new
  *T. megacarpa*.
