# *Tetracera* L.

**Family**: [[Dilleniaceae]]

Sp. pl. **1**: 533 (1753).

A pantropical genus of about 50 species, of which approximately 12 occur in
continental Africa. **Five species are recognised in Gabon** (in the present
treatment), with two further species cited as possibly to be sought
([[#Species to be sought in Gabon]]).

## Diagnosis

Lianas, often very large, more rarely trees, shrubs or suffrutices; indument
of simple or fasciculate hairs. Stipules absent.

**Leaves**: petiole **winged or canaliculate** (a key character at
section level); blade with secondary nerves either reaching the margin and
ending in a marginal tooth (craspedodromous venation) or joined to one
another in arches more or less remote from the margin (brochidodromous
venation).

**Inflorescences**: in ± branched cymes, few- to many-flowered, solitary
or grouped in panicles; flowers bisexual (often andromonoecious in South
America).

**Flowers**: pedicel articulate; sepals (3)4 or 5(13), subequal or with the
outer smaller, little or not accrescent; petals (2)3 to 5(6), obcordate to
elliptic, glabrous, often quickly caducous; stamens numerous (50–200), with
filaments unequal to equal, in several ranks, free or very rarely ± united
in fascicles, the connective inflated-capitate; anthers with 2 divergent
thecae, extrorse or rarely introrse, yellow to white, dehiscing by 2 slits;
carpels 1–5(6), free, 1-locular, with 2–12 ascending ovules; styles generally
curved in bud, with the stigma ± oblique, subcapitate, sometimes ± emarginate.

**Fruits**: apocarpic, with 1–5 follicles, each with 1–5 seeds.

**Seeds**: surrounded by a **red lacerate aril**.

## Sections in Gabon

The genus is divided into two sections recognised in this treatment:

- **Section *Tetracera*** — pantropical, with often many-flowered
  inflorescences and petals rounded at the apex. In Gabon:
  [[Tetracera_alnifolia|*T. alnifolia*]], [[Tetracera_breteleri|*T. breteleri*]],
  [[Tetracera_podotricha|*T. podotricha*]].
- **Section *Akara* Kubitzki** — exclusively palaeotropical, with
  pauciflorous inflorescences and **petals bilobed at the apex**. In Gabon:
  [[Tetracera_poggei|*T. poggei*]], [[Tetracera_rosiflora|*T. rosiflora*]].

## Key to the species in Gabon

Translated from Niangadouma, Lachenaud & Sosef (2022). Two species
non-Gabonese but cited in the key are marked **(NG)** = not yet known from
Gabon.

- **1.** Inflorescence shorter than the subtending leaf or barely exceeding
  it, with few flowers (1–15); flower with 4 petals 9–23 mm long, **bilobed
  or emarginate at apex**, and 4 sepals ± erect and appressed against the
  carpels in fruit; pedicel 6–30 mm; fruit with carpels 10–20 mm long, erect
  or sometimes oblique; petiole ≤5(–10) mm, canaliculate (sect. *Akara*)
  → **2**
  - **2.** Suffrutex ≤1 m tall, with erect stems; leaf blade pubescent on
    both faces (NG)
    → ***T. masuiana* De Wild. & T.Durand**
  - **2′.** Liana or scandent shrub of several metres; leaf blade glabrous
    above except sometimes on the midrib
    → **3**
    - **3.** Sepals 5–10 mm long, with short appressed pubescence within;
      flower bud 4–6 mm in diameter; leaf blade attenuate at base and ±
      decurrent on the petiole, with rather slender secondary nerves (less
      prominent than the midrib); tertiary venation reticulate; pedicel
      slender, ± flexuose, glabrous or very sparsely pubescent
      → [[Tetracera_poggei|*T. poggei*]]
    - **3′.** Sepals 10–15 mm long, densely tomentose-silky within;
      flower bud (6–)8–12 mm in diameter; leaf blade rounded to obtuse or
      barely acute at base, with strong secondary nerves almost as prominent
      as the midrib; tertiary venation scalariform; pedicel robust, straight,
      with ± dense pubescence
      → [[Tetracera_rosiflora|*T. rosiflora*]]
- **1′.** Inflorescence generally exceeding the subtending leaf considerably,
  many-flowered; flower with 2 or 3 petals 4–7 mm long, **entire or nearly
  so**, and generally 5–8 sepals (if only 4, then reflexed from anthesis);
  pedicel absent or up to 7(–10) mm; fruit (unknown in *T. breteleri*) with
  carpels 5–10 mm long, spreading or oblique; petiole generally longer,
  ± winged (sect. *Tetracera*)
  → **4**
  - **4.** Leaf blade discolorous, the lower surface entirely covered with
    a beige felt of woolly hairs (mixed with sparser long hairs — see Figure
    3D), and with the margin entire; sepals densely pubescent externally
    → [[Tetracera_breteleri|*T. breteleri*]]
  - **4′.** Leaf blade green on both faces, with hairs (if present) not
    felted and not covering the lower surface; sepals glabrous or very
    sparsely pubescent externally
    → **5**
    - **5.** Flower with (5)6 to 8 sepals **densely silky on the inner
      face**, very unequal — the 2 or 3 outer much smaller; leaf blade very
      scabrid on both faces (NG)
      → ***T. potatoria* Afzel. ex G.Don**
    - **5′.** Flower with 4 or 5(6) sepals glabrous on the inner face,
      little unequal or sometimes one outer much smaller; leaf blade
      smooth, or sometimes slightly scabrid
      → **6**
      - **6.** Sepals **4(5), already reflexed at anthesis**, 3–5 mm long;
        petiole generally long-villous; leaf blade with secondary nerves
        often deeply impressed above; fruit with follicles 5–7(–8) ×
        3–5(–6) mm
        → [[Tetracera_podotricha|*T. podotricha*]]
      - **6′.** Sepals **5(6), ± erect and remaining appressed against the
        carpels in fruit**, 5–8 mm long; petiole glabrous or with short
        appressed hairs; leaf blade with secondary nerves flat or barely
        impressed above; fruit with follicles 7–10 × 5–7 mm
        → [[Tetracera_alnifolia|*T. alnifolia* subsp. *alnifolia*]]

## Species to be sought in Gabon

Two widely distributed Central African species are not currently known from
Gabon but are likely to occur and are included in the key above:

- ***T. masuiana* De Wild. & T.Durand** — widespread in Central Africa
  (and also occurring in West and East Africa). A suffrutex with pubescent
  leaves; should be sought, particularly in eastern Gabon.
- ***T. potatoria* Afzel. ex G.Don** — widespread in Central, West and East
  Africa. *T. stuhlmanniana* Gilg is a synonym. The Gabonese material
  formerly cited under *T. stuhlmanniana* in Sosef et al. (2006) actually
  belongs to [[Tetracera_podotricha|*T. podotricha*]].

Two Gabonese fruiting collections (*Schoenmaker 13* and *van Valkenburg 2666*)
fit *T. alnifolia* in some characters but differ in their dense stellate hairs;
their status awaits flowering material.

## Uses

Many species of *Tetracera* contain flavonoids with medicinal properties,
notably anticancer, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory and antiviral, and may
have neuroprotective and cardio-protective effects (Kubitzki 1968, 1970). In
Africa, *Tetracera* species are best known for their abundant and potable
sap, hence their name **"water lianas"** (*lianes à eau*); they also have
medicinal uses (Bouquet 1969).
