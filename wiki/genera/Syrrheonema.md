# *Syrrheonema*

**Authority**: Miers, Ann. Mag. Nat. Hist., ser. 3, **13**: 124 (1864).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Lianas**. Leaves simple. Inflorescences in **fascicles of glomerules or
in capitula**, the male with 1 to 5 flowers, the female with usually fewer
flowers. Male flowers: **sepals 9 to 18, imbricate**, the outer strongly
tomentellous outside; petals 3; stamens 3 to 6, coalescent. Female flowers:
sepals like the male; petals 6, very small; staminodes 6; carpels 3 to 5,
long-pubescent; styles glabrous. Fruits with subglobose, hairy mericarps.
Seeds curved.

An African genus of **3 species**: two in central Africa (one in Gabon)
and one in Upper Guinea.
