# *Tinospora*

**Authority**: Miers, Ann. Mag. Nat. Hist., ser. 2, **7**: 35 (1851).
**Synonym**: *Hyalosepalum* Troupin, Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **19**:
430 (1949).

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Twining herbs or lianas**. Leaves simple; blade entire-margined, with
**pinnate venation**. Inflorescences: **male in (2)3(4)-flowered cymules
arranged in simple pseudoracemes**, sometimes with a few branches at base;
**female in racemes of solitary flowers**. Male flowers: sepals 6, in 2
ranks, the inner much larger than the outer; petals 6, fleshy, with rolled
margins; **stamens 3, with fused filaments**, thecae dehiscing
longitudinally. Female flowers: sepals and petals like the male; carpels
3; stigma sessile. Fruits: mericarps with pulpy exocarp and verrucose
endocarp. Seeds with fleshy, ruminate albumen.

A Palaeotropical genus of about **33 species**, of which 24 in Asia and 9
in Africa (2 in Madagascar). **One species in Gabon.**

## Notes

- *Tinospora penninervifolia* was originally described by Troupin in his
  segregate genus *Hyalosepalum*, later transferred to *Tinospora* by the
  same author.
- Fruits of *T. penninervifolia* remain unknown.
