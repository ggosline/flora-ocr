# *Beirnaertia*

**Authority**: Louis ex Troupin, Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **19**:
419, t. 18 (1949).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Lianas or sometimes shrubs. Leaves simple; petiole often geniculate
distally; blade entire-margined. Inflorescences in **ample panicles of
agglomerated cymules**. Male flowers: **sepals 9, imbricate**, the 3
outer membranous, the **6 inner subfleshy**, in 2 whorls; petals 3 to 6,
membranous; **stamens 3, free**, with subfleshy filament, anthers
dehiscing transversely. Female flowers: sepals and petals like the male;
staminodes 3; **carpels 3**. Fruits with stipitate, glabrous, smooth
mericarps; mesocarp pulpy; endocarp woody, slightly flattened, rugose;
condyle septiform. Seeds exalbuminous.

A **monospecific** genus of central tropical Africa.
