# *Englerophytum* Krause

**Authority**: Krause  
**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]]

## Diagnosis

The treatment calls this genus remarkable among African Sapotaceae because the
anthers, borne on **very short filaments**, are fused at the level of the
corolla-lobe commissures into a **collar-like ring** that seems to extend the
corolla tube. In bud the anthers form a **conical hood** above the ovary.
Flowers are **5-merous**, there are **5 stamens**, **no staminodes**, and the
ovary is **5-locular**. Fruits are one-seeded.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: 90-94  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

The treatment places several previously segregated genera in synonymy under
*Englerophytum* and then recognizes three Gabonese entities, two of them very
imperfectly known.

## Notes

- The source explicitly says [[Englerophytum_kouloungense]] is known only from
  a **sterile specimen**.
- [[Englerophytum_le-testui]] is treated as known chiefly from **fruit**.
- The genus is contrasted with [[Zeyherella]] mainly by the fused, extremely
  short filaments and the broader seed scar.
