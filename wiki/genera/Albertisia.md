# *Albertisia*

**Authority**: Becc., Malesia **1**: 161 (1877).
**Synonym**: *Epinetrum* Hiern, Cat. afr. pl. **1**: 21 (1896).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

Lianas with hairy to glabrous branches. Leaves simple, entire, petiolate.
Inflorescences axillary; **male in cymules of 1 to 4 flowers**, sessile or
pedunculate; **female with usually solitary flowers**. Male flowers: pedicel
short or absent; **sepals 9 to 12**, the 3 to 9 outer imbricate, the **3
inner fused into a narrowly ovoid pseudocorolla**; petals to 6, or absent,
minute; **stamens 15 to 30, in a stipitate conical synandrium** with sessile
anthers dehiscing transversely. Female flowers: carpels 4 to 12, hairy;
styles elongate. Fruits: mericarps ovoid-ellipsoidal, hairy; mesocarp
fleshy; endocarp coriaceous to woody; **condyle absent**. Seeds exalbuminous.

A Palaeotropical genus of about **18 species**, of which 13 in tropical
Africa and 5 in SE Asia. **Three or possibly four species in Gabon**, all
endemic or near-endemic.

## Treatments

### Vol 60 (2022)

**Author**: Breteler F.J.
**Pages**: 31–35
**Source**: `sources/Menispermaceae_vol60_liteparse`

Breteler describes **two new species** (*A. badia* and *A. mouilaensis*),
both endemic to Gabon, and recognizes a fourth, sterile collection from
Ogooué-Lolo as a possible additional new species. All four are restricted
to Gabonese provinces, making this a remarkable concentration of
*Albertisia* diversity. *A. badia* is named for its **chestnut-brown
indumentum**.

Bibliography cited: Forman (1975); Kessler (1993); Troupin (1962).

## Notes

- Gabon hosts an exceptional concentration of *Albertisia* diversity: of
  the four species recorded here, all are restricted to the country (or
  to Gabon plus very restricted neighbours).
- The diagnostic floral feature of the genus is the **pseudocorolla** of
  3 fused inner sepals — a synapomorphy with the **conical, stipitate
  synandrium** of 15–30 stamens.
