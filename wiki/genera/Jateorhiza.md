# *Jateorhiza*

**Authority**: Miers, in Hook., Niger Fl.: 212 (1849).
**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]]

## Diagnosis

**Twining herbs**. Leaves long-petiolate; blade entire or lobed.
Inflorescences axillary, with solitary flowers; **males in panicles, females
in racemes**. Sepals 6, free; petals 6, ± concave, ± enveloping the
stamens or staminodes. Male flowers: **stamens 6** with partially fused
filaments, anthers dehiscing transversely and apically. Female flowers:
**staminodes 6**; carpels 3, free, with **glandular hairs**, with dilated
stigmas. Fruits with hairy mericarps; endocarp ± smooth. Seeds curved,
with ruminate endosperm.

A tropical African genus of **two species**, one in Gabon and the other
in south-eastern Africa.
