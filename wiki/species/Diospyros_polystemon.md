# *Diospyros polystemon* Gurke

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

28.Diospyros polystemon Gürke

Bot.Jahrb.43:21O (1909).-F.WHITE,FWTA ed.2,2:IO,tab.202 A,1 (1963).

Shrub or small tree reaching 10-15 m in height, with bole slightly fluted at the base. Field observations on this species are still very scanty. Young twigs puberulent, dark brown-blackish in dried material.

Leaves with puberulent petiole 5-10 mm long; blade lanceolate to oblong-elliptic, 10-18 x 4-7 cm, with cuneate or slightly decurrent base, margin recurved beneath in dried material, and long narrow-triangular acumen often 2 cm long; midrib impressed above; 6-10 pairs of lateral veins fading toward the margin, with veinlets forming a loose network prominent on both surfaces; blade coriaceous, finely strigillose then rapidly glabrous except at the base of the midrib beneath; often one or a few small glands present well above the base near the margin in the lower part of the blade.

Male flowers grouped 2-8 in loose axillary cymes whose axes reach 1 cm; pedicels 3-5 mm long, finely pubescent, with caducous bracteole at the base. Calyx greenish-brown with cream-colored margin, glabrous outside and appressed-pubescent within, cup-shaped, about 3-4 mm long, prolonged downward by a false pedicel about 1 mm long to the articulation point, with (4-)5-6 imbricate hemi-orbicular lobes 2-3 mm long, ciliolate on the margin; corolla yellowish or ochre, very fragrant, rotate and divided almost to the base into (4-)5(-6) oval lobes 6-7 x 4 mm with obtuse tip; 45-120 stamens, inserted partly on the corolla tube and partly on the receptacle, of different lengths, 2-6 mm, with the inner ones shortest, filaments 1-5 mm, more or less bearing long hirsute hairs, and very short, about 1 mm, acute lanceolate anthers that are more or less pubescent; pistillode rudimentary, massive, broadly conical at the summit, 2 mm high.

Female flowers similar to the male but somewhat larger, with stout pubescent pedicels up to 5 mm long, bearing caducous bracts and bracteoles and articulated directly below the cupular part of the calyx, which is slightly accrescent; numerous small staminodes 2 mm long attached to the corolla tube, ciliate-pubescent; ovary paraboloid, 4 x 3 mm, glabrous, with about 8-10 locules, apparently directly surmounted by a conical stigmatic mass of 8-10 fleshy, more or less deltate stigmas interlocked with one another.

Fruits subglobose, about 2 cm, glabrous, surrounded at the base by the calyx reaching 1.5 cm in diameter and bearing 4-5 hemi-orbicular lobes with ciliolate margin; seeds 6-8, perhaps 10-12 mm long.

**Syntypes :**Zenker 167I et 2993, Cameroun (iso- ?).

This species is known only from Cameroon in evergreen humid dense forest, and from the Republic of the Congo, the DRC, and Angola.
