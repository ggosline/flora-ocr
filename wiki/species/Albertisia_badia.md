# *Albertisia badia* Breteler, **spec. nov.**

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Albertisia]]

Described as new in this Vol 60 treatment.

## Diagnosis (Latin protologue)

> *Albertisiae porcatae* Breteler similis, ab ea differt indumento castaneo-
> brunneo et foliis multo angustioribus pari uno tantum venarum laterialium
> basalium dominantium.

(*"Similar to [[Albertisia_porcata|A. porcata]] but differing in the chestnut-
brown indumentum and in much narrower leaves with only a single dominant pair
of basal lateral nerves."*)

## Type

**Gabon**, Ogooué-Lolo, Monts Birougou, région de Ramsar, village Maranda I,
12°38′8″E, 1°44′7″S, 6 Dec. 2017, *N. Texier, E. Akouangou, R. Niangadouma,
J. de Dieu Kaparidi, D. Bikassa & F. Mawango 1463* (**holo-: MO**; iso-: LBV).

## Description

Liana; stems, petioles and fruits with **brown** pubescence. **Leaves**:
petiole 1.5–2.5 cm, geniculate near the apex; blade oblong-elliptic,
**9–11 × 4–4.5 cm**, obtuse to subcordate at base, rounded to obtuse and
apiculate over ± 2 mm at apex, with 3–6 pairs of lateral nerves of which
**one basal pair longer than half the blade**, with brown pubescence on
the midrib of both faces and on the laterals beneath.

**Female flower** (inferred from the fruiting state) 4- or 5-carpellate;
pedicel 4–5 mm. **Fruit** (immature): mericarp obloid, **20 × 5–7 mm**,
stipitate over 3–5 mm.

Male flowers, mature fruit and seed unknown.

## Distribution

**Endemic to Gabon**, known from a single collection from Ogooué-Lolo (the
type).

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest at ± 800 m altitude.

## Notes

- Closest to [[Albertisia_porcata|*A. porcata*]] in habit and indumentum
  type but distinguished by the much narrower oblong-elliptic leaves with a
  single dominant basal vein pair (vs. ovate leaves with 2(3) pairs in
  *A. porcata*) and by the chestnut-brown (vs. tomentose grey-brown) indument.
