# *Diospyros kupensis* Gosline & Cheek, *sp. nov.*

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

A species described after the *Flore du Gabon* Ebenaceae treatment. It is
relevant to the regional taxonomy of [[Diospyros]] because the protologue compares it directly with
[[Diospyros_conocarpa]].

## Type

**Holotype**: Cameroon, South West Province, Tombel Subdivision, Mt Kupe above
Kupe village, Muanezum trail, 900 m altitude, 29 January 1995, *Etuge 1369*
(K); isotypes BR, MO, P, SCA, WAG, YA.

## Description

Small treelet **1.5-3(-5) m** tall with verticillate branching; trunk slender,
to about **10 cm diameter** at the base; bark grey to brownish and smooth.
Young branches are pale ferrugineous-sericeous.

**Leaves** chartaceous, oblanceolate-elliptic to narrowly elliptic,
**9-18 x 5-8 cm**, with an acumen **1-2 cm** long; base decurrent, acute to
obtuse; lower surface matt olive-green, with the midrib and petiole bearing
the same ferrugineous indument as the young stems.

**Flowers** are dioecious. Male flowers were first known only in bud; later
material showed compressed glomerules with hirsute anthers. Female flowers are
subsessile, with sepals that enlarge strongly and almost entirely conceal the
much smaller corolla.

**Fruit** solitary, subsessile in the leaf axils, **globose, 2.5-3.5 cm**
diameter, yellow-orange when fresh, with the apex pubescent and the calyx
enlarged into four long, leafy lobes almost as long as the fruit.

## Distribution

**Known only from Cameroon**: so far restricted to the south side of **Mt
Kupe**, Southwest Region.

## Habitat and ecology

Understorey of undisturbed submontane rainforest on steep slopes at about
**700-1250 m** altitude.

The protologue highlights an unusual habit of **vegetative reproduction**:
leaning trunks can produce upright side shoots that develop adventitious roots,
forming local clumps of many stems.

## Taxonomic affinities

The species is described as most similar to [[Diospyros_conocarpa]], but differs
in its **short, appressed indument** (rather than long, rough, spreading
hairs), **acute to obtuse leaf bases** (not cordate), and the absence of the
specialized myrmecophilous leaf bases that characterize *D. conocarpa*.

It also differs in the floral proportions: in *D. kupensis* the sepals are
long and ligulate, about **25 mm**, and almost completely hide the corolla,
whereas in *D. conocarpa* the sepals are shorter and the corolla is larger and
more deeply lobed.
