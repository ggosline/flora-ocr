# *Diospyros vermoesenii* De Wild.

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

36.Diospyros Vermoesenii De Wildeman

Shrub, more or less strongly branched, with slender curved branchlets; young twigs puberulent then glabrous. In the Cristal Mountains this species is reported as a shrub 5 cm in diameter, with pink sapwood and red heartwood containing a black central cylinder.

Leaves with pubescent petiole not exceeding 2 mm; blade lanceolate to elliptic, sometimes slightly falcate, 2.5-10 x 1.5-4 cm, cuneate at the base, with narrowly cartilaginous margin and gradually acuminate apex, the acumen more or less acute and sometimes ending in a small tuft of hairs; blade subcoriaceous, glabrous on both surfaces except on the midrib, which bears a few scattered hairs at least toward the base and is slightly raised above, with 4-5 pairs of lateral veins that may also be pubescent, and with veinlets weakly marked on both surfaces; a few small black glands scattered beneath the blade, especially in its lower part and some distance from the midrib.

Male inflorescences in very short axillary cymes, of which 1-3 flowers develop; pedicels not exceeding 1 mm, with very short pubescent bracts and bracteoles; calyx cup-shaped, 1.5 mm long, with 3 suborbicular lobes extending beyond half the calyx depth, sparsely pubescent outside, though more abundantly so in Cristal Mountains material, and ciliolate on the margin, glabrous inside except for a ring of hairs around the base of the corolla tube; corolla bottle-shaped, 8 mm high, with 7 mm tube bearing a few appressed hairs toward the summit and 3 somewhat acute triangular lobes 1 mm long; 9 stamens attached to the receptacle, with filaments not exceeding 1 mm, very hairy, or glabrescent in Cristal Mountains material, and glabrous lanceolate anthers 2 mm long topped by a pointed acumen 1 mm long; pistillode reduced to a tiny mass with a few hairs.

Female flowers axillary, solitary or 2-4 together in very short cymes, axillary or in the axils of fallen leaves; pedicels almost absent, with very short pubescent bracts and bracteoles; calyx 2 mm long, with 3 broadly cordate lobes, very obtuse at the summit, glabrous within except for a ring of hairs around the base of the corolla tube, sparsely pubescent outside, with ciliolate margins; corolla with somewhat urceolate tube, glabrous except for a few appressed hairs toward the upper part, 4 mm high and 3 mm in diameter, with 3 short obtuse, more or less rounded or slightly apiculate lobes scarcely 1 mm high; no staminodes; ovary elongate-ovoid, 3 mm high, surrounded at the base by a collar of erect hairs, more or less pubescent, with 4(-6?) locules and directly surmounted by 2(-3) small cordiform stigmatic lamellae.

Fruits subsessile, ovoid, apiculate, 2.5 x 1.5 cm, somewhat verrucose, enclosed at the base by the persistent scarcely accrescent calyx; seeds 1-3, 12 x 6 mm.

**Type :**Vermoesen I597， Congo Kinshasa.

This species is localized in the Mayombe, both in Gabon near Tchibanga and in the Congos, but it also seems to occur near the Cristal Mountains in Gabon, where forms transitional toward *Diospyros hoyleana* appear possible.
