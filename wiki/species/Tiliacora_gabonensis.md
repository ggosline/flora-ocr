# *Tiliacora gabonensis* Troupin

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tiliacora]]

Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **30**: 26 (1960).

## Description

Liana up to **at least 25 m** tall; **stem up to 8 cm thick**; **branches
and petioles puberulent**, glabrescent or not. **Leaves**: petiole
**1–2.5(–3.5) cm**, thickened and often geniculate at apex; blade
ovate-elliptic, **7–17 × 4–7 cm**, rounded to cuneate at base, acuminate
over 1–1.5 cm at apex, glabrous above or sometimes puberulent on the basal
part of the midrib, glabrous beneath or with a few scattered hairs on the
midrib; **lateral nerves 3 or 4 pairs of which at least one pair dominant
in length and with domatia in the axils**.

**Inflorescence**: **male cauline**, up to **42 cm long**, racemiform,
tomentose, with stipitate cymules 3–13 mm in diameter, often paired, with
3 to 7 flowers; bracts ovate, ± 2 mm long; **female inflorescence
unknown**. **Male flower**: sepals 9, puberulent outside, glabrous inside,
the outer ± circular, ± 1 mm in diameter, the median transversely
elliptic, 2–3 × 1–2.5 mm, the inner elliptic-obovate, **6–8(–10) × 2–4
mm**; petals 6, narrowly obovate, 2–2.5(–3.5) × 0.5–1 mm; stamens 6,
**6–7(–9) mm long**.

**Infructescence** cauline, up to **25 × 12 cm**, racemiform, with 3-flowered
cymules, stipitate over 2–3 cm in fruit, with 4 to 10 carpels, tomentose.
**Fruit (immature)**: mericarp obovoid to globose, **15–22 × 10–16 mm**,
slightly flattened, ± smooth, glabrous, stipitate over 1–2 mm.

## Distribution

**Endemic to Gabon**: Moyen-Ogooué, Ngounié, Nyanga, Ogooué-Ivindo,
Ogooué-Lolo, Woleu-Ntem.

## Habitat and ecology

Forest islets in savanna, primary forest, **inselbergs**. At 20–840 m
altitude.

## Uses

The bark yields a **glue**.

## Notes

- One of three Gabonese [[Tiliacora]] species and the only **Gabon endemic**
  in the genus. Distinguished by the **pubescent young branches and
  petioles**, **domatia in the leaf nerve axils** and the **cauline,
  racemiform male inflorescences**.
- Female flowers are unknown — neither floral organs nor mature fruit
  were available to Breteler.
