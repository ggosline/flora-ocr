# *Syrrheonema fasciculatum* Miers

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Syrrheonema]]

Ann. Mag. Nat. Hist., ser. 3, **20** (1867).

## Description

Liana up to **20 m** tall; **stem up to 3 cm thick**; branches
subherbaceous, **tomentose**. **Leaves**: petiole **3.5–9(–13) cm**,
curved-geniculate near apex, tomentose; blade broadly ovate-elliptic,
**(5–)11–15(–21) × (3–)6–10(–14) cm**, rounded to obtuse at base, acute to
acuminate over 0.5–1(–1.5) cm at apex, sparsely pubescent above,
glabrescent except on the principal nerves, **± velvety beneath**, **with
4 or 5 pairs of lateral nerves of which 2(3) basal pairs prominent below**.

**Male inflorescence** **1–1.5 cm long**, stipitate; **female unknown**.
**Male flower**: **sepals 9 to 12, whitish**, the outer tomentellous
outside; androecium obovoid-elongated, 2.5 mm long.

**Fruit**: mericarp ± **15 × 12 mm**, **red at maturity**, sparsely
puberulent.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, Democratic Republic of the
Congo.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Ogooué-Ivindo.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest. In Gabon up to 300 m altitude, elsewhere up to 500 m.

## Uses

In the Democratic Republic of the Congo, leaf juice is used against
**ophthalmia**.

- One of three African *Syrrheonema*, of which two are in Central Africa
  (one in Gabon) and one in Upper Guinea.
- Female flower and inflorescence remain **unknown**.
