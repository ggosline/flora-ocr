# *Diospyros monbuttensis* Gurke

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

23.Diospyros monbuttensis Gürke

Bot.Jahrb.26:66 (1898),et 43:208 (1909).-F.WHITE,Bull. Jard.Bot. Brux.27:518-531,tab.54,55 et 57 (1957)； Systematics Association Publication 4:8o-83,tab.3 et 4(1962);FWTA ed.2,2:14 (1963); Nigerian Trees 2; 338,tab.157 (1964).

-Diospyros senensis auct.non KLorzscH :HIERn,Monogr.Eben., Trans. Camb. Phil.Soc.12: 181 (1873); FTA 3: 520 (1877),p. p.

PL.17, p. I17, et CARTE 36,p. 177.

Shrub not exceeding 10 m in height and 20, rarely 30-40 cm in diameter at the base, with short bole, often strongly fluted, sometimes divided into several stems from the base, and with rather spreading open crown, curved branches, and frequent erect shoots. Bark usually smooth, purple-brown, sometimes yellow-brown or green, very thin, flaking remarkably into thin papery films, the section itself pale yellow to egg-yellow; sapwood white, turning yellow in the air; wood yellowish white. Branches and older twigs, with orange-red bark, bear large spreading spines, especially in young plants; slender twigs with sparse branchlets, at first slightly strigillose and orange-tawny, then glabrous.

Leaves with slender petiole 0.5-1.5 cm long, channelled above; blade oblanceolate to obovate, 10-20(-28) x 5-8(-11) cm, generally larger near the ends of the branches above the inflorescences or fruits; base cuneate or obtuse and decurrent; apex acute or obtuse, shortly subacuminate, very rarely cuspidate; midrib deeply impressed above; about 6-8 pairs of arched, widely spaced lateral veins, scarcely raised above; tertiary veins more or less parallel and veinlets forming a loose network; indumentum of adult leaves sparse, composed of very small stiff, more or less obliquely appressed, straight or sinuous hairs, on the upper surface confined to the midrib, on the lower surface more abundant and concentrated on the midrib and secondary veins, sometimes in their axils, more scattered beneath the blade; blade papery, dull green above, pale green beneath, with epidermis of noncontiguous bulliform cells and yellowish or brownish veins when fresh; in dried material dark dull brown above, paler beneath, with veins likewise pale on both surfaces; very often a few black glands are present beneath the blade, somewhat away from and parallel to the midrib, especially in the lower half.

Flowers fragrant, subsessile, gathered in cymules of 3(-5) at the ends of peduncles obliquely diverging from the twig and flattened, 5-18 mm long, rather sparsely covered with appressed puberulence, arising singly on the twig 2-6 mm above the axil of the first leaves of the year, which may be well developed or rudimentary; pedicels not exceeding 2 mm, thickened into a cushion at the summit; bracteoles ovate, 3 x 2 mm, caducous. Calyx closed in bud, more or less bottle-shaped, up to 20 x 3-4 mm, irregularly splitting at the apex into 2-4 superficial teeth, pale green, paler toward the apex but with a brown margin when fresh, strigillose both outside and inside; corolla creamy white, more or less salverform, with cylindrical tube 12 mm long and 3-5 lobes 7-10 x 4.5-6 mm with rounded or subacute apex, glabrous except for a few ciliate hairs on the lobe margins; about 16-20 stamens inserted at two different heights on the base of the corolla tube, glabrous, with filaments 1-2.5 mm and narrowly subsagittate apiculate anthers 3 mm long; no pistillode.

Female flowers fragrant, solitary; pedicel almost glabrous or covered with appressed puberulence, about 10 mm long, diverging obliquely from the twig, somewhat flattened and broadened at the top, arising singly on the twig 2-6 mm above the axils of well-developed leaves. Calyx green, accrescent, more or less urceolate when young, 10 x 8 mm, very thick, with irregularly and slightly split margin, externally covered with tawny or ferruginous velvety pubescence and densely silky inside; corolla yellowish outside in bud, similar to that of the male flower; 0-6(-8?) staminodes with sessile pseudo-anther 2 mm high, inserted at the base of the corolla tube; ovary conical, 3 x 2 mm, glabrous, 8-locular, and almost directly surmounted by 4 foliaceous folded crenulate stigmas scarcely 1 mm high, surrounded at the base by a crown of hairs.

Fruits shortly pedicellate, about 8-10 mm, ellipsoid, broadly ovoid, or subglobose, about 3 x 2.5-4 cm, yellow, then orange, then red, glossy, glabrous except at the persistent style base; fruiting calyx accrescent, enclosing the fruit for half its height, with truncate margin breaking irregularly, tawny externally, tomentellous and patchily glabrescent, and covered inside with tawny tomentum; seeds 2-8, about 1.6 x 0.6 x 0.4 cm, with slightly ruminate endosperm.

**Lectotype designated by F. White (1957):** Schweinfurth 3598, Uele district, DRC (K); paratype: Rowland s.n., Nigeria (iso-P).

This species ranges from Ivory Coast to northeastern DRC but in fact is confined to zones of semi-deciduous humid dense forest; in Cameroon it extends into the gallery forests of the Adamawa plateau.
