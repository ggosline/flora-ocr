# *Lecomtedoxa klaineana* (Pierre ex Engl.) Dubard

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Lecomtedoxa]]

## Description

A **large tree** with glabrous branchlets. Leaves are oblong, rounded at the
apex or shortly and obtusely acuminate, cuneate at the base, up to **13.5 x
5.5 cm**, glabrous, with about **10** lateral veins plus parallel intermediate
veins forming arches near the margin. Petiole about **2 cm**.

Flowers are still considered imperfectly known in the treatment. The corolla
is about **5 mm** long, with **5** lanceolate lobes and **2 appendages per
lobe** of equal length. There are **5 extrorse stamens** with very short
filaments, **5 staminodes**, and a pubescent **5-locular** ovary with long
style.

The fruit is pyriform, laterally ribbed, about **5 x 2.5 x 2 cm**, coriaceous
to subligneous when dry, laterally dehiscent, and one-seeded. The seed is
flat, yellow-brown, about **3.2 x 1.8 x 1 cm**, with a linear scar extending
the full length of the ventral face.

## Distribution

**Range**: only recorded from coastal Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Cap Esterias, Libreville, Pointe Denis, Ikoy km 18, and Mondah.

## Habitat and ecology

Coastal forest near the sea. The species is described as common around
Cap Esterias and Libreville, with straight cylindrical bole **20-25 m** long,
no buttresses, and abundant fruiting in **November**.

## Notes

The treatment treats this as a characteristic hard-wood coastal tree of the
Libreville-Cap Esterias zone, with several local vernacular names centred on
`okalangouma`.
