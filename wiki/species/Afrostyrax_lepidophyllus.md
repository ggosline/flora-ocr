# *Afrostyrax lepidophyllus* Mildbr.

**Family**: [[Huaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Afrostyrax]]

*Bot. Jahrb. Syst.* **49**: 556 (1913).

## Description

Tree **12-30 m** tall; trunk to **40 cm** in diameter; bark yellowish to
brownish; branches glabrous, branchlets densely hairy.

**Stipules**: rapidly caducous, obovate, slightly falcate,
**9-10(-14) x 2-3 mm**, densely ferruginous-hairy outside.

**Leaves**: petiole **(6-)10-12(-15) mm** long, densely ferruginous-hairy;
blade papery, elliptic to ovate-elliptic or obovate-elliptic,
**(5-)8-19(-24) x (1.5-)3-7(-10) cm**, lower surface with ferruginous to
greyish indument; base acute to rounded; apex acuminate for
**(0.5-)1-2.5(-3) cm**; lateral veins **(5-)7-8(-12) pairs**.

**Flowers**: fasciculate in groups of **3-10**; bracts and bracteoles elliptic,
**1-2 x 1-1.5 mm**; pedicel **(4-)6-11(-12) mm** long, ferruginous-hairy.
Calyx **3-4(-5)-lobed**; lobes ovate-elliptic to ovate, **3-4 x 2-3 mm**,
ferruginous outside. Petals obovate-elliptic to elliptic, **5 x 2 mm**,
pale brown, with simple hairs inside and sparse ferruginous hairs outside; claw
absent or barely marked, glabrous. Filaments united toward the base,
**1.2-1.8 mm** long; anthers **0.9-1.1 mm** long, dorsally glabrous. Pistil
**1.5-2.5 mm** long; ovary **1-2 mm** in diameter, hairy; style **0.5-1 mm**
long, with stellate hairs in the lower part.

**Fruit**: ovoid, **1.2-4 x 1.1-3 cm**, densely hairy.

**Seed**: subglobose, **15-20 mm** in diameter, testa smooth and finely
spotted.

## Distribution

**Range**: Ghana, Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, Congo (Brazzaville), the
Central African Republic, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.  
**Gabon**: present, but the source does not specify provinces.

## Habitat and ecology

Humid dense forest, to **1000 m** altitude.

## Vernacular names

esun, eswinya, bole (Fang); findji (Kota); sindjakalo (Myene); mufira (Punu).

## Uses

The bark and seeds are used locally as garlic-flavoured condiments.

## Notes

This is the only Gabonese *Afrostyrax* whose lower leaf surface is densely
covered in stellate, scurfy hairs. That character makes it straightforward to
separate from both [[Afrostyrax_kamerunensis]] and
[[Afrostyrax_macranthus]].
