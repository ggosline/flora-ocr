# *Albertisia mouilaensis* Breteler, **spec. nov.**

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Albertisia]]

Described as new in this Vol 60 treatment.

## Diagnosis (Latin protologue)

> Infructescentiae speciebus gabonensibus generis similis, sed folio
> pinnatinervi sine nervis lateralibus basalibus dominantibus, differt.

(*"Infructescences similar to those of the other Gabonese species of the
genus, but differing in the pinnately nerved leaf without dominant basal
lateral nerves."*)

## Type

**Gabon**, Ngounié, km 36 Mouila–Yeno, dense forest, 19 Sept. 1986,
*Breteler & Lemmens 7787* (**holo-: WAG**; iso-: LBV).

## Description

Liana; main stem 1 cm in diameter, reaching 5 m, with branches, petioles and
midrib of the lower leaf surface **pubescent to puberulent**, hairs appressed
or not, glabrescent. **Leaves**: petiole cylindrical, **(1–)2–4(–5) cm**,
geniculate and thickened at the distal part; blade ovate to elliptic-ovate,
**(4–)7–12(–16) × (2–)3–5(–6) cm**, cordate at base, acuminate over 0.5–1.5
cm and mucronate at apex, glabrous above; midrib flat to slightly impressed
above, prominent beneath; **the 5 or 6 pairs of lateral nerves pinnate,
slender, slightly prominent on both faces** (no dominant basal pair).

Male and female inflorescences and flowers unknown. Infructescence sessile on
a leafy ramule. **Fruit** (immature): mericarp ellipsoidal, **12–15 × 7–8
mm**, slightly flattened, densely pubescent, stipitate over 1–2 mm.

## Distribution

**Endemic to Gabon**, collected twice — in Ngounié (the type) and Woleu-Ntem.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary and secondary forest, at 300–550 m altitude.

## Notes

- Distinguished from all other Gabonese *Albertisia* by the **pinnately
  nerved leaf** (no basal pair longer or stronger than the others). All four
  other Gabonese species have at least one dominant basal pair of lateral
  nerves.
