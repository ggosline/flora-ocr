# *Manilkara le-testui* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Manilkara]]

*Notulae Systematicae* **16**(3-4): 233 (1960).

## Description

Leaves oblong-elongate, attenuate at the apex, cuneate at the base,
**25-30 x 6.5-8.5 cm**, coriaceous, glabrous. About **20** secondary veins are
very prominent beneath and unite close to the margin; the parallel transverse
nervules and veinlets form a conspicuous network. Petiole long, about **4 cm**.

Flowers are fasciculate on older branchlets. Pedicels long, about **2 cm**, and
tomentose. Sepals **6**, tomentose externally, about **7 mm** long. Corolla
with a short tube **1.7 mm** long; **6** lobes, each with **2 appendages** as
long as the lobes, overall **6-7 mm** long. Stamens **6** and staminodes **6**
(sometimes **4-5**), inserted at the level of petal insertion; filaments short
and slightly hairy; anthers elongate and slightly hairy. Staminodes triangular,
laciniate on the margins, and hairy.

**Ovary** hirsute, with about **6** locules (sometimes **4-5**).  
**Fruit** unknown.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from the type gathering in Gabon.  
**Gabon**: source locality given as Ngon, between the Ogooue and Cameroon.

## Notes

The treatment explicitly describes this species as remarkable but **very
imperfectly known**, based on a single Le Testu specimen. It is the only
Gabonese *Manilkara* in the treatment with an ovary of about six locules rather
than twelve.
