# *Triclisia hypochrysea* Diels

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Triclisia]]

In Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 71 (1910).

## Description

Liana with **stem, petiole and lower side of the main leaf venation
densely covered with brown ± appressed hairs**; stem up to **15 cm thick**.
**Leaves**: petiole **(0.5–)1–5(–7) cm**, geniculate or straight at apex;
blade elliptic, **(2–)5–9(–16) × (2–)4–7(–9) cm**, cuneate to obtuse at
base, acute to acuminate over 0.5–1 cm and sometimes very shortly apiculate
at apex, glabrous above, with grey appressed hairs and glabrescent beneath,
**with 3(4) pairs of lateral nerves**, prominent on both faces, with fine
reticulation on both faces.

**Inflorescence**: **male cauliflorous**, paniculiform, up to **4 cm long**,
several together inserted on small bumps, densely tomentose; **female
unknown**; bracts ± orbicular, ≤ 1 mm in diameter. **Male flower**: pedicel
**(3–)4–7 mm**, tomentose; **sepals 17, shiny brown outside**, glabrous
inside, broadly ovate ± 1 × 1 mm, the next 3 × 3 mm, the median
elliptic-ovate 6–7 × (2–)3–3.5 mm, the inner valvate, oblong, **9 × 3.5
mm**; petals 6, obovate, ± 0.5 mm long; stamens slightly fused at base,
**6–7 mm long**, glabrous, anther 1–1.5 mm.

**Infructescence** on the main stem, sessile to shortly stipitate, with
10 fruits on a 1.5 cm stipe. **Fruit**: mericarp subellipsoidal, **2 × 1.5
× 1 cm**, shortly apiculate, **yellow with dense brown tomentum**; endocarp
ruminate, laterally canaliculate.

## Distribution

**Endemic to Gabon**: Estuaire, Ngounié, Ogooué-Maritime.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary forest, often partially disturbed; at 50–500 m altitude.

## Notes

- The only Gabonese [[Triclisia]] with **cauliflorous** male flowers (the
  others are axillary or ramiflorous) and only **3 (or 4) pairs of lateral
  nerves** (the others have 4 or more).
- One of three Triclisia endemics in Gabon together with [[Triclisia_gabonensis]]
  and (described as new in this treatment) [[Triclisia_megacarpa]].
- Female flower **unknown**.
