# *Diospyros hoyleana* F. White

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

16.Diospyros Hoyleana F. White

Bull.Jard.Bot.Brux.26:240 et 245(1956)；FWTA ed.2,2:15,tab. A,7 (1963); Nigerian Trees 2 : 337 (1964). -Maba kamerunensis GURkE,Bot. Jahrb.46 :I5o (1gII)，non Diospyros kamerunensis GURkE (1898). Strombosiopsis buxifolia S. MooRE，Journ.Bot.58 ：226 (1920)，nonMaba buxifolia (RorTB).A.L.Juss. (1804). Specimens of this species appear in various herbaria under the following name: *Maba klaineana* (Pierre ms.).

PL.I1，1-I4,p.8g,et CARTE 33,p. 174.

Shrub or small tree 10-15 m tall and 20-25 cm in diameter at the base; plants
20-25 m tall and 50 cm in basal diameter have been reported, though the diameter
may be erroneous, as may also a diameter of 1.25 m reported from
Congo-Kinshasa. Habit very characteristic, with a vertical stem and a conical
often truncated crown made up of small subverticillate branches ramifying in
horizontal planes. Bark nearly smooth, grey-brown to blackish, with a black
outer layer and reddish inner tissue, crumbling finely; wood pinkish white with
longitudinal black lines. Young branchlets green-blackish, finely pubescent,
with distichous leaves.

Leaves with a finely pubescent petiole 2-3 mm long; blade obliquely rhomboid,
3-5 × 1.5-2.5 cm, margin slightly undulate, base asymmetrical and almost
right-angled, apex acute and barely acuminate, more rarely blunt and emarginate,
sometimes with a tiny tuft of hairs. Blade subcoriaceous, glabrous, dark green
above and dull green beneath, with the midrib raised above and 3-4 pairs of
looped lateral veins that are usually otherwise indistinct; the midrib may be
slightly pubescent beneath. When dry, the blade is dull dark brown, paler
beneath, pustulate above and wrinkled below. Three or four black glands lie on
each side of the midrib, 1-2 mm away from it.

Inflorescences axillary or borne below the leaves, in subsessile fascicles of
1-7 flowers, with tiny crowded concave triangular bracts and bracteoles at the
base, under 1 mm long, coarsely pubescent on the back and margin, the latter
often glandular.

Male flowers with a cup-shaped calyx 1.5 mm high, edged with 3(-4) rounded
teeth reaching halfway down the calyx and finely puberulent. Corolla
yellowish-white, glabrous, tubular, blunt in bud, then 6-7 mm long and with
only 3 tiny deltoid lobes about 1 mm long. Stamens 6-9, inserted at the base of
the corolla, with filaments 0.5 mm long and linear-lanceolate anthers 4 mm
long; long erect hairs occur at the top of the filament, at the base of the
anther, and on the connective, and the anther also ends in a long apicule.
Pistillode reduced to a small tufted mass of erect hairs.

Female flowers with a perianth like that of the male flowers but with a larger
calyx, up to 2 mm high, more deeply lobed, the lobe margins clearly ciliate and
sometimes glandular. Corolla yellowish tinged reddish, more inflated, only
5-6 mm long, with a few appressed scattered caducous hairs near the base of the
lobes. Staminodes 4-8, with a flattened filament 0.5 mm long, attached to the
base of the corolla, and a pubescent pseudo-anther 2 mm long. Ovary ovoid,
2-3 mm high, surrounded at the base by a collar of erect hairs and almost
directly topped by 2 stigmas about 1 mm long, in the form of erect triangular
tongues with papillose margins and lateral edges recurved outward. Ovary
4-locular.

Fruits red then black, ovoid to somewhat elongated, apiculate, up to
2.5 × 2 cm, pubescent only at the base, with a slightly verrucose surface,
standing on the scarcely enlarged calyx with 3 rounded ciliate lobes. Pericarp
thin; seeds 1-4, about 15 × 8 × 6 mm.

**Lectotype:** Zenker 1745, Cameroon (K; iso BM, BR, P), designated by
F. White (1956).

A very close species, *Diospyros feliciana* R. Let. & F. White, exists in
French Guinea in relic vegetation patches. *D. hoyleana* itself is reliably
known only from Nigeria, Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, both Congos,
Angola, and Zambia. It is confined to the understorey of evergreen humid
forest, where it may be locally very abundant; in Cameroon it occurs in both
Biafran and Congolian forest.

The vernacular name "Koadna" and variants such as Kodena, Kuattna, and Kotna,
applied in Yaounde language in Cameroon, and "Effre" used by Fang speakers in
Gabon, seem valid for this well-marked species. Confusion remains possible,
however, with *D. obliquifolia* and probably also with *D. vermoesenii*.
