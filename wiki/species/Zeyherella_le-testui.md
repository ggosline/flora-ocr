# *Zeyherella le-testui* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Zeyherella]]

## Description

A tree with brown-tomentose young branchlets and persistent linear tomentose
stipules about **1 cm** long. Leaves are obovate-oblong, emarginate at the
apex, long-attenuate and sharply cuneate at the base, **9-20 x 2.5-5.5 cm**,
coriaceous, with many close lateral veins depressed above and raised below.
The lower surface is brown-tomentose. Petiole canaliculate, brown-tomentose,
about **2.5 cm** long.

Inflorescences are dense fascicles of pedicellate flowers on old wood.
Pedicels are **8 mm** long. Sepals **5**, brown-tomentose, **5 mm** long. The
corolla is glabrous, with a short **2.5 mm** tube and ovate lobes **4.5 mm**
long. Stamens equal or slightly exceed the corolla. Filaments are broad at the
base and about **2 mm** long. The ovary is hairy and **5-locular**.

Fruit was unknown to the treatment.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Ngango, Lastoursville region.

## Habitat and ecology

Humid forest. The type tree was reported as **12-15 m** tall, with red flowers
in **May**.
