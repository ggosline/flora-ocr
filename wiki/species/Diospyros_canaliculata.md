# *Diospyros canaliculata* De Wild.

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

Small tree 15-20 m tall and 15-20(-30) cm in diameter, with a crooked trunk,
branching low, and many small branches arching toward the ground and supporting
a broad, densely leafy crown; the young pendent leaves are orange-pink-green.
Trunk rhytidome very black; bark rather soft but brittle. In oblique section:
black rhytidome outside, then a very pale yellow band outwardly and sulfur
yellow inwardly, the whole section soon turning egg-yellow in the air and later
violet; wood yellowish, also turning egg-yellow. Older twigs dark grey, notably
covered, even when very slender, with blackish corky pustules.

Leaves with a petiole black in dried specimens, up to 2 cm long, channelled
above; blade oblong, about 20(-28) × 8(-11) cm including the 10-15 mm acumen,
base more or less rounded but somewhat decurrent onto the upper petiole, apex
usually abruptly acuminate with an acumen about 5 mm wide at the base, blunt
and often oblique relative to the blade. Midrib slightly channelled above;
8-10 pairs of lateral veins, very discreetly looped near the margin, diverging
from the midrib at a rather open angle and accompanied by a few shorter, fainter
lateral veins. Minor venation very dense, the principal veinlets almost
transverse. Dry blade margin often minutely revolute, texture subcoriaceous,
olive-green and paler beneath, rarely brownish or blackish. Black glands are
often present beneath the blade, as in *D. physocalycina*, at vein junctions,
near the base of the midrib, and also near or even beside the margin, at least
in the lower part of the blade, sometimes all around it and often near and
beneath the acumen.

Flowers fragrant, grouped in contracted cymes of about ten flowers or more,
exceptionally on young shoots but most often on mature branchlets, branches,
and even the trunk.

Male inflorescences with small concave oval acute ciliate bracteoles. Pedicels
extremely short, 1-2 mm, with a soon-falling pubescence, articulated at the
top. Calyx with open aestivation, cup-shaped and fairly separate from the
corolla, with 4 broadly oval blunt lobes marked only to about one third of the
calyx depth; calyx 4-5 mm long, sparsely and finely clothed outside with short
stiff appressed hairs and somewhat similarly inside, this pubescence falling
quickly. The calyx of *D. physocalycina*, whose male flower is very similar, is
normally truncate and denticulate but may split spontaneously and thus appear
lobed. Corolla with a slightly swollen tube 8-10 mm high, slightly contracted
below 4 obliquely spreading lobes 4-5 × 2-3 mm, blunt at the tip; floral bud
red at the apex and initially pubescent along the lobe margins; open corolla
yellowish white tinged with pink. Androecium of 16 stamens arranged more or
less regularly in two series near the base of the corolla, with filaments about
2 mm in the inner lower stamens and 4 mm in the outer upper stamens, these
filaments slightly puberulent; anthers lanceolate, acute, 3-4 mm long.
Pistillode a small ribbed rudiment.

Female inflorescences also bearing bracteoles. Pedicels up to 5-8 mm long,
glabrous, thickened, articulated at the top; flowers 4-merous, more rarely
3- or 5-merous. Calyx about 10 mm high but rapidly accrescent,
urceolate-quadrangular, with an inverted rounded pyramidal base and lobes
connate below, broadly rounded and apiculate at the top, with deeply reduplicate
valvate aestivation; it is sometimes very sparsely and finely pubescent outside
like the male calyx but more often glabrous. Corolla under 15 mm long, with
obliquely oval lobes 5-7 × 3-4 mm, blunt at the tip, at first exserted and then
caducous, apparently under pressure from the calyx lobes. No staminodes. Ovary
globose, 2 mm in diameter, glabrous, 4-locular, topped by styles fused into a
stout column 2 mm high and ending in two broad triangular tongue-like stigmas.

Fruits edible, on reddish pedicels 5-15 mm long, broadly ovoid, about 3 cm
high, yellow at maturity with a red tint at the base, apiculate, and wholly
hidden by the enlarged calyx, which becomes pale yellow then brown-violet, its
lobes closing over the fruit. Pericarp somewhat fleshy but strongly caustic.
Seeds 2-4, more or less trigonous, acute at the top, about 20 × 8 mm.

**Type:** Em. and L. Laurent, Congo-Kinshasa.

This species ranges from Liberia to Nigeria in western Africa and into central
Africa as far as Angola. In Cameroon, Central African Republic, both Congos, and
Gabon, where it has so far been collected only in southern Nyanga near
Tchibanga, it appears to avoid evergreen humid forest. Its ecology explains why
in Cameroon it has been found only around Ndikinimeki, Yaounde, Deng Deng,
Bertoua, Yokadouma, and Moloundou. It occurs sometimes near watercourses in
forest subject to periodic flooding, but also in terra firme forest where it
may be locally common.

It is very close to *D. physocalycina*, which seems more confined to evergreen
humid forest; the two are distinguished best by the fruiting calyx.
