# *Leptoterantha mayumbensis* (Exell) Troupin

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Leptoterantha]]

Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **19**: 428, t. 19 (1949).

## Synonymy

- *Kolobopetalum mayumbense* Exell, J. Bot. **64**, Suppl. Polypet.: 13 (1926).

## Description

Liana up to **8 m** tall, **completely glabrous**. **Leaves**: petiole
twisted and/or curved-geniculate at base, 1.5–6 cm; blade elliptic, **4–9 ×
1.5–4 cm**, ± narrowly rounded at base, acuminate over ≤ 0.5 cm at apex,
**coriaceous**, with prominent venation on both faces, with **8–10 pairs of
principal lateral nerves intermingled with numerous, ± parallel tertiary
nerves**.

**Male inflorescence** up to 30 cm long, with flowers solitary or in pairs;
peduncle 1–2 cm; bracts narrowly elliptic-ovate to deltoid, 0.6–1.5 mm.
**Female inflorescence** ± similar to the male, often shorter. **Male
flower**: pedicel 0.6–2 mm; outer sepals ovate, 0.5 × 3–0.5 mm; inner
elliptic, 3 × 2 mm; petals narrowly elliptic-obovate, 2 × 0.8 mm, with
involute margins; **stamens in a synandrium** as long as the petals.
**Female flower**: staminodes subulate-linear, 0.5 mm; carpels 1–1.5 mm.

**Fruit**: mericarp ± 1.5 × 1 cm.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Central African Republic,
Angola (Cabinda).
**Gabon**: Ogooué-Ivindo, Ogooué-Lolo, Woleu-Ntem.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary and secondary forest, **inselbergs**. In Gabon at 10–635 m altitude,
elsewhere up to 960 m.

## Notes

- *Leptoterantha* is **monospecific**. Diagnostic feature: the dense,
  ± parallel tertiary venation intermingled with the principal lateral
  nerves, unique among Gabonese Menispermaceae.
