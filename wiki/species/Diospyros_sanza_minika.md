# *Diospyros sanza-minika* A. Chev.

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

34. Diospyros Sanza-Minika A. Chevalier

Veg. ut. Afr. trop fr. 5 : 155 (janv.I909).-F.WHITE,FWTA ed. 2,2 : 12,tab.202 B,5 (1963). -Diospyros nsambensis(α usambensis ）sphalm.)GURkE,Bot.Jahrb.43： 202 (mars I909).- PELLEGR., Fl. Mayombe 2 : 19 (1928). — Diospyros glaucescens GURkE，Bot. Jahrb. 46 ：I5I (IgII)，syn. nov.

Tree reaching 30-40 m in height and 0.6-1 m in diameter, with a straight bole
and no buttresses at the base. Bark black, very regularly and deeply fissured,
extremely hard, more resistant to decay than the wood; inner bark soft and
pinkish. Fresh wood greyish white, rosy in the heart, often with small black
marblings. Crown globose. Young shoots nearly glabrous or with a few appressed
hairs; older branchlets grey-brown, fissured, glabrous. Very young leaves pale
green to reddish, slightly pubescent beneath, especially on the midrib.

Leaves with a slender glabrous petiole, channelled above, 5-10(-15) mm long;
blade lanceolate-oblong to lanceolate-elliptic, 12-27 × 3-10 cm, rounded or
cuneate at the base and attenuate onto the petiole, generally acute and
gradually acuminate at the apex, though the acumen may reach 20 × 5 mm;
subcoriaceous to coriaceous, glabrous when mature except for a few tiny
appressed hairs beneath. Upper surface dark green and glossy; lower surface
pale and glaucous, drying reddish-brown above and pinkish grey below. Midrib
impressed above; 7-12 pairs of fine lateral veins, slightly raised above, as
are the tertiary veins, branching well before the margin. Small black sunken
glands scattered over the lower surface; the lower epidermis bears a fine
whitish mineralized coating.

Male inflorescences in cymes of 3-5 subfascicled flowers, axillary or on older
twigs, with very short pedicels or pedicels up to 3-5 mm long, these covered
with a short dark-brown pubescence and articulated at the top; bracts and
bracteoles more or less deciduous. Floral bud elongated, acute. Calyx
cup-shaped, 6-8 mm high, initially with an entire rim and later irregularly
split, outside with a sparse pubescence of short dark-brown hairs, inside with
a similar but denser appressed pubescence. Corolla about 20 mm long, white or
pinkish within, densely felted outside in bud; as it emerges from the calyx it
bears appressed hairs bent downward, dark brown above the calyx and silvery
inside it. Tube 10-12 mm long, constricted at the throat, surmounted by 4-5
fleshy oval lobes about 10 × 5 mm. Stamens 20-24, inserted on the receptacle in
four rows, the outer row also fused to the extreme base of the corolla tube;
filaments about 1 mm long, anthers linear and acute, 4-5 mm long, glabrous
except that the inner stamens bear a few curved black hairs at the base of the
anther. Pistillode reduced to a tuft of erect dark-brown hairs.

Female inflorescences in fascicles of 3-5 subsessile flowers along older twigs,
or flowers solitary and more rarely several together on leafy shoots. Calyx
similar to that of the male flower but slightly accrescent; corolla also
similar, but with 3-5 shorter lobes not exceeding one third of the length of
the corolla. Staminodes 5-6, 6-7 mm long, slender, glabrous, inserted at the
base of the corolla tube. Ovary ovoid, about 4 mm high, whitish, very
pubescent, with styles very shortly fused and also pubescent, topped by 3-4
erect stigmas about 2 mm long, pubescent outside and with a glabrous leaf-like
blade within; the ovary itself is 6-8-locular.

Fruits solitary or in groups of 2-4 along already old branches, borne on very
short but very thick pedicels 4-5 mm long and 8-10 mm thick; fruits
ellipsoid-cylindric or slightly four-angled, 3-5 cm long and 2-4.5 cm wide,
smooth, pale yellow, covered with reddish or brown hairs when young and often
for a long time afterward. Pericarp woody, about 2 mm thick. Seeds 4-8, set in
a white mucilaginous pulp, oblong-cylindric, slightly flattened, rounded at
both ends, 2-3 cm long and about 7 mm in diameter, with a finely papillose
reddish testa. Fruiting calyx greenish, nearly glabrous outside and pubescent
within, 1.5-2 cm across, with 5 truncate lobes.

**Type:** Chevalier 16284, Ivory Coast (P).

Known from evergreen humid forest from Sierra Leone to Gabon; not yet recorded
from Nigeria in this treatment, and apparently also present in Congo-Brazzaville
near Sibiti (Farron 4403).
