# *Lecomtedoxa biraudii* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Lecomtedoxa]]

## Description

Leaves are large, narrow-obovate-oblong, attenuate and shortly acuminate at
the apex, rounded at the base, glabrous, reaching about **37 x 12 cm**. There
are **10-12 pairs** of secondary veins, evident beneath and arching close to
the margin; the petiole is thick and short, about **1.5 cm**.

Flowers are borne on older branchlets. Pedicels reach **2 cm**. Sepals are
ovate, up to **10 x 6 mm**, white-tomentose outside and somewhat pubescent
inside. The corolla is large, **17-18 mm** long, with oblong lobes **14 mm**
long bearing **2** basal appendages about **6.5 mm** long. There are **5**
extrorse stamens with pubescent dorsal surfaces, ovate acute staminodes
**5.5-6.5 mm** long, and a pubescent **5-locular** ovary with slender glabrous
style.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon.  
**Gabon**: only the type collection `Service forestier 1942`, without locality
details in the treatment.

## Notes

The treatment calls this species **poorly known** and says its flowers resemble
those of [[Lecomtedoxa_nogo]] and [[Lecomtedoxa_heitzana]], but are distinctly
larger.
