# *Omphalocarpum le-testui* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Omphalocarpum]]

## Description

Young branchlets are reddish-blackish and glabrous. Leaves are obovate-oblong,
**9-19 x 4-10 cm**, glabrous, shiny above, with **8-10 pairs** of secondary
veins and a fine network of parallel nervules and veinlets visible on both
faces when dry. The petiole is stout, plano-convex, and **1-1.8 cm** long.

Flowers are fasciculate on the trunk, with pedicels about **1.5 cm** long.
Sepals are elliptic, about **1.2 cm** long. In hermaphrodite flowers the
corolla has **6 lobes** and is about **25 mm** long, with **15 mm** lobes and
**10 mm** tube. Stamens are arranged in bundles of **6** opposite the petals,
with filaments **15-18 mm** long, partly fused at the base. Staminodes are
short, denticulate, sometimes apiculate. The ovary is glabrous and
**18-locular**.

Female flowers are smaller, about **20 mm** long, with **5-6 sepals** and
**6-7 corolla lobes**; the stamens are reduced to short filaments. Fruit was
unknown to the treatment.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Oyem, Moncoumana Idemba, and Endama.

## Habitat and ecology

Humid forest. The source does not add further ecological detail.

## Notes

The treatment contrasts this species with *O. massoko* and emphasizes the less
acute leaf base, the clearly petiolate leaves, and the pedicellate flowers.
