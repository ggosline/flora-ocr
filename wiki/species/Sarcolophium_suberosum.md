# *Sarcolophium suberosum* (Diels) Troupin

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Sarcolophium]]

Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **30**: 30 (1960).

## Synonymy

- *Kolobopetalum suberosum* Diels, in Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 163 (1910).

## Description

Liana up to **10 m** tall, **glabrous**; branchlets finely wrinkled when dry.
**Leaves**: petiole **(6–)8–11(–18) cm**, curved and/or twisted at base;
blade ovate, **8–20 × 5–13 cm**, **broadly and often deeply cordate at base**,
acuminate over 0.5–1.5 cm at apex, papyraceous to coriaceous, with revolute
margins, **with 3 to 5 basal nerves and 1 to 3 additional pairs of lateral
nerves**, **with two black spots in the axils of the two main basal lateral
nerves**.

**Inflorescence** axillary; **male up to 14 cm long**, **female up to 18 cm
(in fruit)**; bracts ovate, ± 1 mm long. **Male flower**: pedicel ± 1 mm;
outer sepals broadly ovate, ± 0.7 × 0.7 mm; inner sepals circular, ± 2 × 2
mm; petals elliptic-obovate, concave, 1.5 × 1–1.5 mm; stamens as long as
petals. **Female flower** unknown.

**Fruit**: mericarp ellipsoidal, ± **17 × 13 mm**, glabrous, with **viscous,
sticky mesocarp**.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Gabon.
**Gabon**: all provinces except Haut-Ogooué, Nyanga, Ogooué-Maritime.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest. At 70–650 m altitude.

## Notes

- *Sarcolophium* is **monospecific** and confined to Central Africa.
- Breteler corrects two errors in Diels (1910): the inflorescence is in fact
  **glabrous**, not pubescent (Diels probably confused it with that of
  *Rhigiocarya nervosa*); and Diels's drawing of the endocarp is wrong — the
  endocarp shown there is actually that of [[Kolobopetalum_auriculatum]].
