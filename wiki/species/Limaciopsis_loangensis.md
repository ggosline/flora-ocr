# *Limaciopsis loangensis* Engl.

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Limaciopsis]]

Bot. Jahrb. Syst. **26**: 414 (1899).

## Description

Liana up to **10 m** tall; stem **up to 10 cm thick**. **Leaves**: petiole
cylindrical, **thickened and often geniculate and ± sulcate in the upper
part**, 1–2.5(–5.5) cm; blade elliptic, **(6–)9–12(16) × 3–5(–9) cm**,
rounded at base, **acute and often mucronate or retuse to emarginate at
apex**, coriaceous, glabrous above, ± sparsely hairy to glabrous on the
principal nerves beneath; lateral nerves 3 or 4 pairs, of which one basal
stronger, with fine tertiary network on both faces.

**Male inflorescence** ± narrow, 10–15 cm long, with tomentose axes; peduncle
absent or up to 1 cm; bracts ovate, 0.5–1 mm. **Female inflorescence** like
the male but only 2–4 cm long, peduncle very short to absent. **Male flower**:
pedicel ≤ 1 mm; sepals spreading, tomentose on both faces, the outer linear
to elliptic, 1–1.5 mm, the inner elliptic, 1.5 mm; petals obovate, emarginate
at apex, ± 1 mm long, with incurved margins at base, puberulent without;
**stamens 6, free**, ± as long as the petals. **Female flower**: pedicel
± 2 mm; sepals ± as in male, slightly larger; petals 1 mm, with rolled margins
at apex, hairy without; carpels ± 1.5 mm, with ovary 1 mm, hairy; styles
± 0.3 mm, glabrous, with dilated stigmas.

**Fruit**: mericarp **2 × 2.5 × 1.7 cm, orange, slightly shiny**. **Seed**
1.7–2 × 0.4–0.5 cm.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon, Republic of the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo,
Central African Republic.
**Gabon**: collected twice in Nyanga.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary and secondary forest, gallery forest, **forest islets in savanna**.
In Gabon up to 100 m altitude, elsewhere up to 500 m.

## Uses

A decoction of the tuber is given to children against convulsions. In Gabon
it is taken with a little salt against gonorrhoea. A decoction of leaves or
twigs is drunk to treat stomach aches.
