# *Diospyros soyauxii* "Gurke & K. Schum."

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

33.Diospyros Soyauxii Gurke & K.Schumann

Bot.Jahrb.14:312(1892)．- F.WHITE，Bull.Jard．Bot.Brux.33： 361,tab.22 (1963).

-Thespesocarpus tiliaceus PiERRE,Bull.mens.Soc. linn. Paris 2：1258 (1896)，syn.nov. (cf.PELLEGR.,Fl. Mayombe 2:I9 (1928).

Shrub 2-3 m tall, with young twigs covered in appressed puberulence. The specimen Letouzey 9024 referred here is, however, a shrub reaching 20 cm in diameter, with erect bole, bark red in section, and red wood darker toward the center where fissures are black-edged.

Leaves with puberulent petiole up to 1.5 cm; blade elliptic or lanceolate-elliptic, up to 21 x 10 cm, with cuneate or rounded base, sometimes slightly subcordate or even truncate, and apex shortly and broadly acuminate, often mucronulate at the tip of the acumen; midrib impressed above with a few appressed bristles at least on young leaves, prominent beneath, as are the 5-8 pairs of lateral veins more or less visibly anastomosing near the margin; tertiary veins numerous, subparallel, close-set; blade, in dried material, dull reddish-brown and glabrous above, gray or pinkish-gray beneath with a covering of appressed or nearly appressed strigillose hairs, especially under the veins; about 3-5 relatively large depressed glands, surrounded by hairs, usually arranged obliquely from the axils of the lateral veins toward the middle of the blade.

Male inflorescences and flowers unknown.

Female flowers axillary, solitary or in cymes of 3-9 flowers, more rarely in highly branched cymes of up to 50 flowers; pedicels 3-13 mm long, with a few narrowly lanceolate bracts and bracteoles at the base. Calyx scarcely accrescent, 3 mm long, tomentellous-strigillose inside and out, with (3-)4(-5) triangular lobes reaching about two thirds of the calyx depth; corolla narrowly conical in bud, 8 x 3 mm, with 4 lobes reaching only one quarter of the corolla depth, tomentellous-strigillose; no staminodes; ovary narrowly conical, rostrate, 4 x 2 mm, tomentellous-strigillose, 4-locular, surmounted by 2 almost completely united styles, each ending in a very short glabrous stigma.

Fruits borne on stout pedicels 1.5 cm long and 0.5 cm thick at the summit, fusiform and more or less 4-angled, 5-7 x 2.5-3 cm, yellow and probably reddish at maturity, sparsely strigillose, finely verrucose; seeds (3-)4, 3.5-4 x 1 x 0.8 cm.

**Neotype designated by F. White (1963):** Soyaux 187, Gabon (K).

This species is so far known only from the Kribi region in Cameroon and the Libreville region in Gabon; material with male inflorescences and flowers is still lacking.

### Material studied in Cameroon

Letouzey 9024,15 km SSE Zingui,soit 5o km SE Kribi (fr.,mars).

* Note: there is hopeless confusion between Klaine's field notebook and the herbarium or carpological labels in the Paris collections regarding numbers 127, 427bis, 452, 452 bis, 157, 399, and 411; F.
