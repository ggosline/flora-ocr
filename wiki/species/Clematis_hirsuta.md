# *Clematis hirsuta* Perr. & Guill. var. *hirsuta*

**Family**: [[Ranunculaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Clematis]]
**Subgenus**: *Campanella* Tamura · **Section**: *Brachiatae* Snoeijer

Fl. Seneg. tent. **1**: 1 (1831).

## Description

Liana climbing by its petioles, leaf rachides or petiolules. Stem reaching
10(–15) m, up to 0.6 cm in diameter, faintly **6- to 8-furrowed**, **densely
puberulous or velutinous even when adult**.

**Leaves**: generally **1-pinnate** (rarely 2-pinnate), with 5–7 leaflets;
petiole and rachis together reaching 6–25 cm; petiole 1–5.4 cm; petiolules
0.2–5 cm. Leaflets ovate to broadly ovate, **1.5–7(–11) × 1–6(–10) cm**,
cordate-rounded at base, acuminate to acute at apex, dentate to denticulate
at margin, **generally 3-lobed or 3-lobulate**, sometimes entire,
papyraceous, densely pubescent or weakly puberulent on both faces, sometimes
velutinous above. Nerves prominent on the lower face; secondary nerves in
2 to 5 ascending pairs, all basal or basal and pinnate; reticulation lax.

**Inflorescence**: axillary and terminal, in cymes united into a raceme or
more often into a **panicle**, 10–40 cm long, with 5 to **numerous** flowers;
peduncle 1–9 cm; bracts ovate, 3-lobed or entire.

**Flower**: bisexual, **2–3.5(–4) cm in diameter**; pedicel 1–2.5(–3.5) cm,
densely puberulous; receptacle subglobose, ± 2 mm in diameter, hairy. Sepals
**4, spreading**, elliptic-ovate-oblong, **8–19(–24) × 4–7(–8) mm**, acute or
slightly apiculate, **white** (sometimes white tinged with pink), **±
densely puberulent within**, velutinous on the margin, with non-prominent
longitudinal nerves. Stamens **30–60**, (5–)8–11 mm long; filament linear,
glabrous but silky-puberulent at base; anther oblong to narrowly oblong,
(1–)1.5–2(–2.5) mm, glabrous, obtuse at apex. Carpels **25–50**; ovaries
ovoid, 1 mm long, densely puberulent; style 6–11 mm at flowering, **2–4(–6)
cm in fruit**, densely villous.

**Fruit**: achenes compressed, broadly elliptic, **3–3.5 × 2.5–3 mm, orange**,
puberulent; styles with hairs ± 5 mm.

## Distribution

**Range**: widely distributed in sub-Saharan tropical Africa, from Senegal to
Ethiopia (also in Yemen and Saudi Arabia) in the north, and from northern
Angola (Cuanza Norte) southward through Zambia to northern Mozambique.
**Gabon**: known from only **a single collection**, from Nyanga.

## Habitat and ecology

Forest edges, savannas. In Gabon the altitude is unknown for the only
collection (estimated between 100 and 400 m); elsewhere at 600–3000 m.

## Notes

- *C. hirsuta* belongs to a group of related species, the **"*Clematis
  brachiata* group"**, formally treated at section or subsection rank by
  Kuntze (1885), Prantl (1887), Snoeijer (1992), and finally Wang (2004).
  Some authors have described varieties of *C. hirsuta* now considered
  synonymous with *C. brachiata* Thunb. *s.str.*
- Lebrun & Stork (2003) treat *C. hirsuta* as a synonym of *C. brachiata*
  *s.l.* (sensu Snoeijer 1992). The Vol 60 treatment **rejects this**,
  following Wang (2000, 2004), Essig (2004) and the African Plant Database
  (2021), and treats *C. hirsuta* as **clearly distinct at the species
  level** from *C. brachiata* *s.str.*
- Other accepted varieties of *C. hirsuta*, besides the autonym, are:
  *var. incisodentata* (A.Rich.) W.T.Wang (Ethiopia; densely woolly leaf
  undersides; sepals glabrous within); *var. junodii* (Burtt Davy) W.T.Wang
  (south-east Africa; papery, hairy, non-reticulate leaf undersides); and
  *var. glabrescens* A.Chev. (Senegal and Republic of the Congo to Sudan and
  Kenya; glabrescent leaf undersides). The single Gabonese collection
  belongs unambiguously to **var. *hirsuta***. The taxonomic value of these
  varieties is judged weak.
- See [[Clematis_grandiflora]] for the contrast between the two Gabonese
  *Clematis* species: *C. grandiflora* is hygrophilous and confined to
  wetter forests, *C. hirsuta* prefers drier savanna and semi-arid regions.
