# *Penianthus longifolius* Miers

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Penianthus]]

Ann. Mag. Nat. Hist., ser. 3, **13**: 124 (1864).

## Synonymy

- *Penianthus klaineanus* Pierre, Bull. Mens. Soc. Linn. Paris n.s. **1**: 76
  (1898).

## Description

**Erect shrub up to 3(–4.5) m tall** — the **largest non-climbing
Menispermaceae** in Gabon (the only other one is the small
[[Anisocycla_jollyana|*Anisocycla jollyana*]], 30–70 cm). **Leaves**: petiole
**3.5–12(–24) cm**; blade obovate to elliptic, **of very variable size,
15–34(–40) × 6–15(–18) cm**, rounded to cuneate at base, acuminate over
2(–3) cm at apex, **coriaceous**, with **8–12(16) pairs of lateral nerves**.

**Inflorescence** on leafy or sometimes leafless branches: peduncle ±
pubescent, 4–11(–17) mm; **male inflorescence with 10–15 flowers**, female
with 10–15(30) flowers; bracts 1 × 0.8 mm. **Male flower sessile**. **Female
flower**: pedicel up to 4 mm, densely pubescent; carpels 1.5–2 × 0.9–1.3 mm,
densely pubescent; stigmas sessile, bifid.

**Fruit**: mericarp ellipsoidal, **(15–)18–31(–34) × 11–18 mm**, **yellow to
orange at maturity**; exocarp 1–2 mm thick; endocarp 1.5–2 mm thick, densely
pubescent outside. **Seed** ellipsoidal, **14–25 × 9–11 mm**.

## Distribution

**Range**: Nigeria, Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, Republic of the Congo,
Democratic Republic of the Congo, Central African Republic, Angola (Cabinda).
**Gabon**: **all provinces**.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary and secondary forest, gallery forest. In Gabon up to 600 m, elsewhere
up to 1050 m.

## Uses

Roots and root bark are widely used as **aphrodisiacs**. To treat sexual
weakness, powdered root is taken with *Aframomum melegueta* (Roscoe) K.Schum.
The powdered, macerated root, alone or with sugarcane juice, is taken as an
emetic, vermifuge and against colic. Root shavings or powdered bark mixed
with water (sometimes leaf paste) are applied as poultices on ripe abscesses;
moistened root is applied to infected wounds, sand fleas and infected nails.
Root or bark juice is instilled in drops in the ear for purulent ear
infections and deafness, and is also used against cough. Bark and root juice
are common ingredients of **arrow poisons**. Leaves are eaten to treat
hernia. In Gabon and DRC, the **stem is used to make arrows** with the
coriaceous leaves serving as fletching. The **very hard wood is sometimes
used for timber**.

## Vernacular names

ovoung grandes feuilles (fr.); dilimba (bapunu, bavungu); ndip, ndjü-mbènye
(fang).

## Notes

- One of only **two non-climbing** Menispermaceae in Gabon — and the only
  one with a hard, usable wood. Common throughout the country, in all nine
  provinces.
- The genus *Penianthus* has four species in the Guineo-Congolian region;
  this is the only Gabonese representative.
