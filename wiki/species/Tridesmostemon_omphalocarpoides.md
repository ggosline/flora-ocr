# *Tridesmostemon omphalocarpoides* Engl.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tridesmostemon]]

## Description

A tree whose very young leaves bear a few coarse grey hairs that are soon shed.
Leaves are obovate-oblong, acuminate, sharply cuneate at the base, glabrous,
coriaceous, and **10-25 x 4-8.5 cm**, with **8-10 pairs** of secondary veins
prominent beneath and arching near the margin. Petioles are **1.5-2.5 cm**.

Flowers are shortly pedicellate, solitary or paired in the leaf axils or just
above the scars of fallen leaves. The **5 sepals** are red and free to the
base. Petals are oblong, united only at the base, and glabrous or sparsely
hairy. There are **5 thick hairy staminal phalanges**, each carrying **2 or 3
anthers**, alternating with thick hairy **3-toothed staminodes**. In female
flowers the anthers abort and the staminodes are rudimentary. The ovary is
hairy and **10-locular** with glabrous style.

Fruits are yellow, spherical, subsessile, and glabrous. They contain **8-10**
flat keeled seeds about **4 x 1.8 x 1 cm**, each with a sunken linear ventral
scar running for almost the full length.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Oubangui, Gabon, and Portuguese Mayombe according to the
treatment.  
**Gabon**: Lastoursville, Lindeme, Oyem, and Mondah.

## Habitat and ecology

Humid forest. The source does not give a more specific habitat note for the
Gabonese collections.
