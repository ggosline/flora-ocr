# *Englerophytum le-testui* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Englerophytum]]

## Description

A shrub with very large lanceolate or elongate-obovate leaves, obtuse and
obtusely acuminate at the apex, cuneate at the base, reaching up to **50 x
12 cm**. The lower surface is greyish or rusty-tomentose. Numerous secondary
and tertiary veins run parallel and are rather obscure, joining into a
marginal vein. The midrib is depressed above and prominent below. Petiole is
twisted and folded when dry, **4-7 cm** long.

Fruits are orange and contain **1-2 seeds**. Seeds are flattened-ellipsoid,
about **2.4 x 1.7 x 1.2 cm**, with a broad oblong scar occupying essentially
the whole ventral face. When there are two seeds, their adjacent faces are
flattened.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: type from Sindara; a sterile specimen from the foothill forest of
the Monts de Cristal is also referred here provisionally.

## Habitat and ecology

Forest understory. The referred sterile specimen is described as from a small
slender shrub.

## Notes

The treatment explicitly calls this species **very imperfectly known** and
distinguishes it from [[Englerophytum_hallei]] by its much larger leaves and
longer petioles.
