# *Diospyros obliquifolia* "(Hiern ex Gurke) F. White"

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

24.Diospyros obliquifolia (Hiern ex Gürke) F. White

Bull.Jard.Bot.Brux.33:364 (1963);FWTA ed.2,2:15 (1963). -Rhaphidanthe obliquifolia HiERN ex GüRkE，Pflanzenfam.4,1 ：165 (1891).- SrAPF in Hook.Ic.Pl.31 : tab.3o3o,A (1915). Rhaphidanthe Soyauxii SrAPF in Hook.Ic.PI.31 : tab.3o3o,B (1915),non Diospyros Soyauxii GURkE et K. ScHUm. (1892). -Diospyros Stapfiana F.WHITE,Bull. Jard.Bot.Brux.33:364 (1g63),syn. nov.

Shrub scarcely exceeding 6 m in height, with erect stem and small subverticillate branches whose twigs spread in horizontal planes or are slightly obliquely ascending, with remarkably distichous leaves; bark blackish, almost smooth, black in section outward and red within; wood creamy white. Young slender twigs covered with a fine ash-tawny pubescence; young foliage whitish-pink.

Leaves with pubescent petiole scarcely exceeding 1 mm; blade generally parallelogram-shaped or rectangular, with diagonal midrib, sometimes a little falcate, reaching 1-3.5(-5) x 0.5-1.8(-2.5) cm, but asymmetrical at the base above the petiole, forming an acute or right angle there, and obtuse or rounded at the apex, mucronulate with filiform tip; midrib impressed above and prominent beneath, the lower surface furthermore covered, like the midrib and the 2-3 pairs of lateral veins looped at the tip, with rather abundant short appressed pubescence; 3-4 black punctiform glands along and a little away from the midrib on each side beneath the blade; blade papery, glossy dark green above, glaucous green beneath when fresh, with a bullate mineralized epidermal coating on the lower surface.

Male flowers solitary or grouped 2-3 in the leaf axils, subsessile, with pedicels about 1 mm long, densely strigillose. Calyx subcampanulate, 1-1.5 mm high, with 3-4 narrowly triangular acute lobes extending almost to the base, slightly pubescent outside and puberulent within; corolla salverform, with cylindrical tube 5-7 mm high, slightly and very finely pubescent outside, and 4 oval subacute lobes not exceeding 2 mm; 2-3 stamens, sometimes with 1 staminode, on the receptacle, 4-5 mm high, with 1 mm pubescent filaments united at the base and linear apiculate anthers 3-4 mm long with pubescent connective; no pistillode.

Female flowers axillary, solitary or paired, subsessile like the male flowers and similar to them, but with shorter corolla, 4.5 mm, no staminodes, and oblong-lanceolate ovary 3.5 mm high, pubescent with erect hairs, 2-3-locular, and apparently surmounted in bud by a thick somewhat flattened stigma.

Fruits subsessile, with pedicel 1.5 mm long, ovoid, somewhat oblique, apiculate, about 2 x 1 cm; pericarp more or less verrucose, slightly pubescent at the apex, crustaceous; seeds up to 3, about 14 x 7 x 5 mm.

**Type:** Mann 1800, Equatorial Guinea (B destroyed; lectotype designated by F. White (1963), K).

The distinction between *D. obliquifolia* and *D. stapfiana* is based only on leaf size and on the abundance of pubescence on the lower surface of the blade; this distinction does not appear worth maintaining. *Diospyros obliquifolia* is mainly known from Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, and Gabon, in evergreen humid dense forest; it has also been collected sporadically in Ivory Coast, probably in the west of that country, in eastern Nigeria, and in the Republic of the Congo near Pointe-Noire, where the leaves are large.
