# *Diospyros onanae* Gosline, *sp. nov.*

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

A post-*Flore du Gabon* Cameroonian species described from two widely
separated collections. The protologue presents it as most closely allied to
[[Diospyros_soyauxii]], but readily separable by its much smaller leaves,
absence of foliar glands, pyramidal flower buds, and the presence of
staminodes in the female flowers.

## Type

**Holotype**: Cameroon, South Province, Mvila Division, about 30 km on the
road from Ebolowa to Minkok and then about 2 km west along a forest
exploitation track, 620 m altitude, male flowers, 6 July 1975, *J. J. F. E.
de Wilde 8273* (K); isotypes WA, G.

## Description

Tree to about **20 m** tall, but reported flowering already at **4 m**. Young
branches, twigs, and petioles are densely pubescent with short brown hairs.
Leaves are ovate to lanceolate, only **4-9 x 2-5 cm**, with a rounded to
slightly cuneate base, an acute mucronate apex, and a glaucous lower surface
with whitish crystalline cells but no foliar glands.

Male flowers are axillary in small fascicles of **1-5**, 4-merous, with a
tubular corolla about **10 x 3 mm** and **12 setose stamens** in two ranks.
The inner four stamens have connivent connectives, a configuration singled out
in the article as unusual. Female flowers are known only in bud, in fascicles
of **4-8**, with a **4-locular** ovary and conspicuous linear to lanceolate
staminodes. Fruit remains unknown.

## Distribution

**Known only from Cameroon**: one collection from the **South Province**
(Mvila Division) and one from the **Southwest Province** near **Banga**,
roughly **270 km** apart.

## Habitat and ecology

Evergreen forest in disturbed settings. The southern specimen came from
secondary forest after logging with remnants of original forest, while the
Banga specimen was collected in mature forest on heavy clay with
*Autranella* and *Microberlinia*.

## Conservation

Assessed in the protologue as **Endangered (EN B1ab(iii))**, based on only two
known collections, an estimated area of occupancy under **10 km²**, and
ongoing habitat pressure from timber extraction and farming near both sites.

## Taxonomic affinities

The species is compared directly with [[Diospyros_soyauxii]], which shares the
same general indument, mucronate leaf tips, 4-lobed calyx, and 4-locular
ovary. The article emphasizes that *D. onanae* differs in its much smaller
leaves and shorter petioles, lack of leaf glands, pyramidal rather than conical
buds, and the presence of female-flower staminodes.

In the older *Flore du Cameroun* keys the male material would run toward
[[Diospyros_gracilescens]] and the female material toward [[Diospyros_tricolor]],
but the protologue argues that the combined character set does not match either
species.
