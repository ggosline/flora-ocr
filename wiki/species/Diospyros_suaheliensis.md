# *Diospyros suaheliensis* E.Mestre, Meeprom, H.N.Rakouth & Lowry, *sp. nov.*

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

An eastern African coastal segregate split in 2025 from the old African
[[Diospyros_ferrea]] concept.

## Type

**Holotype**: Kenya, Coast Province, Lamu District, Witu Forest, **16 m**,
17 November 1988, male bud and male flowers, *Robertson & Luke 5528* (MO);
isotypes EA, K, US, WAG.

## Diagnosis

Characterized by symmetric leaves, often with a **retuse to rounded apex**, a
few dark pitted basal glands, globose fruits **(8-)10-11(-12) x 8-11 mm**, and
a broader fruiting calyx than [[Diospyros_angolensis]].

## Distribution

**Kenya, Tanzania, and Mozambique**.

## Habitat and ecology

**Coastal and lowland riverine forest** from **0-250 m** elevation.

## Conservation

Assessed in the protologue as **Vulnerable (VU B2ab(i,ii,iii,iv,v))**.

## Notes

The article notes a morphological resemblance to [[Diospyros_angolensis]],
possibly linked to similar coastal and riverine environments.
