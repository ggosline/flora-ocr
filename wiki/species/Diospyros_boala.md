# *Diospyros boala* De Wild.

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

5.Diospyros Boala De Wildeman

$$ \mathrm {P L .} 2, \mathrm {p .} 4 \mathrm {I}, \text {e t C a r t e} 3 6, \mathrm {p .} 1 7 7. $$

Tree to 25 m tall, with a slightly fluted trunk. Rhytidome rough, longitudinally
fissured, peeling in small blackish plates. Field observations on this species
were still rather incomplete when the treatment was written. Bark in section
dirty orange, becoming somewhat yellow toward the cambium. Mature twigs
cylindrical, covered with grey bark that is irregularly and superficially
fissured; young twigs somewhat angular, black on dried specimens, and glabrous
(perhaps pubescent at first).

Leaves with a rather slender petiole about 10 mm long, channelled above,
glabrous; blade lanceolate, elliptic, or slightly obovate, to 18 × 8 cm
excluding the acumen, cuneate at the base and slightly decurrent onto the
petiole, cuneate to rounded-acuminate at the apex, the acumen fairly narrow at
the base, about 5 mm wide and about 1 cm long, blunt at the tip. Blade
subcoriaceous, glabrous and somewhat glossy on both sides, drying brownish with
a slight reddish tint; (4-)5-8 pairs of lateral veins slightly arcuate and
finely joined in loops near the margin; tertiary veins more or less
perpendicular to the midrib, minor veins little visible above but rather raised
beneath. At the extreme base of the blade there is often, on each side, a
slight undulate fold associated with two small crater-like glands, usually very
near the midrib; a few similar glands may occur above these, again close to the
midrib or more rarely toward the margin. Other very small glands may occur in
the acumen beneath the blade. The lower surface is often dotted with minute
yellowish waxy epidermal glands, perhaps related to the occurrence of scale
insects.

Male flowers in short axillary cymes of 5-6 flowers, on pedicels up to 5-6 mm
long, with a few pubescent oval bracts and bracteoles at the base; pedicel
articulate at the top and clothed with a dense yellowish pubescence appressed
upward. Calyx campanulate, to 6 mm long above a narrowed basal portion 2 mm
high, with 4-5 ovate-triangular lobes about 4 mm long, loosely pubescent
outside with yellowish hairs appressed upward, the same pubescence also present
inside, at least near the lobe margins. Corolla with a rather broad,
subcylindric glabrous tube about 10 mm high, with 4(-5) orbicular lobes about
5 mm across, sparsely ciliate near their base. Stamens about 20-28, inserted on
the receptacle, somewhat unequal in size; filaments and anthers no more than 2
and 6 mm long respectively, shortly pubescent, with a distinct tuft of hairs
around the tip. Pistillode rudimentary, globose, densely clothed with erect
hairs.

Female flowers solitary in the leaf axils or 2-3 together at the junction of
twigs, with a few broad, concave bracts and bracteoles, remarkably thick,
deciduous, and leaving characteristic U-shaped scars at the base of the pedicel.
Pedicel about 4 mm long, stout, densely covered with yellowish pubescence
directed upward, articulate at the top. Calyx accrescent, at first campanulate,
about 1 cm high, with 4-5 lobes, thicker than in the male flower and reaching
half that height, oval; the whole calyx, like the pedicel, densely covered
outside and inside with yellowish pubescence appressed upward. Corolla with a
barrel-shaped tube 8 mm high, protruding about 2 mm beyond the calyx tips and
topped by 4(-5) broad oval lobes 8 × 6 mm. Staminodes 12(-15), attached to the
corolla tube, slightly unequal, about 4 mm long, pubescent around the
pseudo-anther. Ovary globose, 2-3 mm long, with about 8 marked ribs, covered
with a dense pubescence of erect hairs and topped by 4 curved styles about
3 mm long, also pubescent; each branch ends in a flattened bifid piece with a
leaf-like coating and an acute tip. Ovary 8-locular.

Fruits on pedicels 10-20 mm long, enclosed in the greatly enlarged calyx whose
subcoriaceous lobes are 4.5 × 2.5 cm, cordate at the base, with undulate
recurved margins and apex and a broad flattened midrib still pubescent near the
base. The fruit itself is red, truncated-pyramidal with four more or less
bilobed faces, about 2 cm high and 2 cm in diameter, at first strigose-pubescent
then nearly glabrous, topped by the truncated base of the styles, and containing
8 seeds up to 16 mm long.

**Type:** Vermoesen 261, Congo-Kinshasa (BR).

Known from Cameroon, Gabon, and Congo-Kinshasa.
