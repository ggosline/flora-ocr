# *Diospyros barteri* Hiern

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

3.Diospyros Barteri Hiern

Monogr.Eben.,Trans.Camb.Phil.Soc.12:187 (1873);FTA3:521 (1877).- OLIv.in Hook.Ic.Pl.23:tab.230o (1894).- IRvINE，Woody pl.Ghana : 576,tab.II2 (1961).—F.WHITE,FWTA ed.2,2:I2,tab.202 A，4 et 202 B,4 (1963). — Diospyros rubicunda GURkE, Bot. Jahrb. 43 : 2o6,tab. 3,A-J (1909). — Diospyros hirta GURKE ex HuTcH.et DALz., FWTA ed.1,2:4(1931).

Shrub up to 15 m tall, sometimes scrambling, with young shoots covered in stiff
golden to reddish-brown hairs up to 1.5 mm long; these persist for some time,
after which the branches become glabrous, cylindrical, and very dark grey. The
same hispid indument recurs on several parts of the plant.

Leaves with a 2-3 mm petiole; blade ovate to elliptic, up to 19 × 7.5 cm
including an acumen about 3 cm long, rounded and usually cordate at the base,
acuminate and apiculate at the apex, subcoriaceous, dark green above and later
brown-violet when dry. The upper surface is glabrous except on the impressed
midrib, the 5-6 pairs of lateral veins, and the also-impressed minor veins.
The lower surface is pale green with a whitish pulverulent epidermal covering,
often dotted with tiny aggregates, and dries a striking grey-violet; it is
hispid especially on the veins, which are prominent beneath, while the minor
veins are usually marked but not raised. A few glands occur beneath the blade,
usually near the midrib and more rarely toward the acumen, sometimes
surrounded by tufts of hispid hairs.

Male flowers in few-flowered axillary fascicles, hispid; pedicels 2-3 mm long,
with small linear-lanceolate bracteoles 5-6 mm long. Calyx 7-9 mm long, shorter
than the corolla, with 4-5 lanceolate segments about 5 × 3 mm, glabrous
inside. Corolla 10-12 mm long, with a thick tube, very pubescent outside and
glabrous inside, ending in 4-5 narrow acute triangular teeth. Stamens 12-15,
inserted on the receptacle and at the base of the corolla tube, 5-6 mm long;
filaments hairy for 1-2 mm; anthers linear-lanceolate, apiculate, glabrous.
No pistillode.

Female flowers, poorly known, solitary, axillary, hispid, nearly sessile, with
deciduous acicular bracteoles. Calyx 6-9 mm long, with 4-5 linear-lanceolate
lobes spreading and recurved, scarcely enlarged in fruit, with a long appressed
pubescence inside. Corolla hispid outside, with a 7-8 mm tube and 4-5 acute
lobes about 2.5 mm long. About 12 pubescent staminodes. Ovary conical, glabrous
except near the top, 4-locular, with 2 bifid styles pubescent toward the apex
and as long as the young ovary.

Fruits on a pedicel about 5 mm long, oblongoid and often beaked, reaching
4 × 2-3 cm, pale yellow to bright orange at maturity, glabrous except near the
apex where a dense golden hispid pubescence often persists for some time; they
usually contain 2-3 elongated seeds, plano-convex to trigonous in shape, up to
1.5 cm long.

**Type:** Barter 2194 (given as 20194 by Hiern, apparently in error), Nigeria.

Known only from Ghana, Nigeria, and Cameroon, in evergreen humid forest.
