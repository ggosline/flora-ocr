# *Rhigiocarya racemifera* Miers

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Rhigiocarya]]

Ann. Mag. Nat. Hist., ser. 3, **13**: 101 (1864).

## Description

**Subherbaceous liana**. **Leaves**: petiole **(5–)10–15(–28.5) cm**;
blade broadly ovate, **10–24(–30) × 7–20(–29) cm**, **cordate at base**,
acuminate over 1–2 cm at apex, **with 4 or 5 pairs of lateral nerves of
which 2 or 3 basal pairs**, **with black spots in the axils on the lower
face**.

**Inflorescence**: **male up to 17 cm long, with flowers in fascicles of
(2)3 or 4(5)**, **female up to 20 cm long (in fruit), with solitary
flowers**. **Male flower**: pedicel 1–2 mm; outer sepals ovate, 1.5 mm long;
inner sepals broadly elliptic, 3 × 2–3 mm; outer petals 2 × 1.5 mm, broader
than inner, 2 × 1 mm; stamens 2 mm. **Female flower**: pedicel 1–1.5 mm;
outer sepals 0.8 × 0.7 mm; inner 1.5 × 1.5 mm; petals 1.5–2 mm; staminodes
± 1 mm; carpels 1.5 mm.

**Fruit**: pedicel 4–5 mm; mericarp **1–1.5 × 0.8–1.2 cm**, glabrous, with
**sticky mesocarp**.

## Distribution

**Range**: Benin, Nigeria, Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, Republic of
the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Angola (Cabinda).
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Ogooué-Lolo.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest and forest regrowth. In Gabon up to 300 m altitude,
elsewhere up to 650 m.

## Uses

In Côte d'Ivoire and southern Burkina Faso the species is well known for
**antineuralgic and aphrodisiac** properties; leaf juice is used as eye
drops or nose drops, and powdered leaves are instilled in the nose against
neuralgia and headaches. Decoctions of finely ground seeds, leafy twigs or
roots are used as aphrodisiacs. Leaf paste is applied to wounds as a
haemostatic. In southern Sierra Leone, Liberia and Nigeria, leaf infusions
are drunk against stomach aches and to treat diarrhoea. The plant is also
used against insomnia. Stems are made into toothpicks. The fruit pulp
yields a glue used for handicrafts.

## Notes

- One of four species of [[Rhigiocarya]], three of which are West African
  (Upper Guinea); this is the only one of the Lower Guinea/Congolian region.
- Distinguished from [[Sarcolophium_suberosum]] (which has similar black
  axillary spots and cordate leaves) by **fascicled male flowers** (vs.
  solitary in *Sarcolophium*).
