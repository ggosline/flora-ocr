# *Diospyros kamerunensis* Gurke

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

18.Diospyros kamerunensis Gürke

Bot.Jahrb.26:69 (1898),et 43:207,tab.3,M-O et 208 (1909).-F.WHITE, FWTA ed. 2,2:1I (1963). - Diospyros pallescens A. CHEv.， Explor. bot. Afr. occ. fr. : 397 (1920), nom.nud. - Diospyros sph&rocarpa PIERRE ex DE WILD., Pl. Bequaert. 3 : 553 (1926) syn. nov.

Specimens of this species appear in various herbaria under the following name: *Diospyros megacarpa* (Gurke ms.), or *D. magnacarpa* (Gurke ms.) in Mildbraed.

Shrub or small tree with erect stem, scarcely exceeding 10(-20) m in height; bark rough; wood pink; young twigs covered with a velvety golden-rufous pubescence, older twigs glabrous, smooth, brownish, and often marbled with greenish and grayish tones.

Leaves with petiole 1-2 cm long, pubescent, wrinkled and pleated when dry; blade lanceolate, ovate-oblong, or elliptic, 15-20 x 5-9 cm excluding the acumen, with obtuse base narrowed onto the petiole and apex gradually narrowed into a rather long acumen, about 25 x 10 mm, very slender at the tip; midrib slightly depressed above and very prominent beneath, as are the 4-6(-8) ascending, little-curved lateral veins, looped near the tip; tertiary veins clearly visible but veinlets indistinct; blade papery to subcoriaceous, in the youngest leaves covered on both surfaces with a fine dense appressed golden-rufous pubescence, later becoming glabrous, first rapidly above, then beneath, the lower surface grayish and covered with a very fine whitish mineralized bloom; a few small black depressed circular glands scattered beneath the blade; margin often recurved in dried material but scalloped beneath.

Male inflorescences axillary, composed of 3-6(-20) flowers with narrowly lanceolate to subulate bracts and bracteoles 2-3 mm long, pubescent; pedicels 2-3 mm long, pubescent. Calyx 8 mm high, externally covered with the same fine dense golden-rufous pubescence and internally with a similar appressed pubescence, bearing 4 narrowly triangular lobes 6 x 1-2 mm; floral bud acute; corolla white or yellowish, salverform, 18-20 mm long, thick and coriaceous, still pubescent outside like the calyx, at least along the midline of the lobes, with tube about 8 mm long, slender, constricted at the throat and surmounted by 4 lanceolate acute lobes 10-12 x 4-5 mm; 12 stamens inserted on the receptacle, about 6 mm long, with filament scarcely exceeding 0.5 mm and linear long-pointed anther bearing long erect hairs near the base; pistillode absent.

Female inflorescences axillary, composed of solitary or few flowers, with bracts, bracteoles, and pedicels like those of the male inflorescences but shorter and articulated at the summit. Female flowers rather similar to the male flowers but broader, with scarcely accrescent calyx 8 mm high and 4 lanceolate acute lobes 6 mm long, and corolla with 1 cm tube and 4(-5) acute lanceolate lobes 1 cm long; 8-12 staminodes attached to the base of the tube, resembling the stamens but more slender, 5 mm long; ovary ovoid, 4-5 mm high, covered with a dense erect silky pubescence, 8(-10)-locular, and almost directly surmounted by 4(-5) obliquely erect styles 1-2 mm long, with a few hairs on the outer face and an elongated-lanceolate papillose stigmatic inner face.

Fruits borne on a stout pedicel 5 mm long, articulated at the summit, depressed-subglobose, 4 cm high and 5 cm in diameter, orange-red at maturity, glabrous or still here and there covered with very caducous appressed hairs, containing 8(-10) seeds about 30 x 18 x 12 mm. In West Africa the fruit seems generally ellipsoid, taller than wide, up to 4 x 3.5 cm, and always more or less apiculate; the leaves also appear generally smaller. These characters do not seem sufficient to justify two specific taxa, though they might support recognition of a subspecies.

**Type:** Zenker 945, Cameroon (iso-945b, P).

This species is known from Liberia, Ivory Coast, and Ghana, and otherwise only from the coastal forests of Cameroon, Kribi-Eseka, and Gabon, Libreville.
