# *Neolemonniera ogouensis* (Pierre) Heine

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Neolemonniera]]

## Description

Leaves are subverticillate in groups of **3-4**, stipulate, lanceolate,
obtusely acuminate, cuneate at the base, **5-7 x 2-2.5 cm**, and glabrous,
with **8-10 pairs** of lateral veins. Petiole **1-1.5 cm**.

Flowers are axillary and fasciculate. Pedicels are glabrous, about **9-10 mm**
long. Sepals **5**, oblong, **3-5 mm** long, slightly pubescent or glabrous.
The corolla is **3.5-4 mm** long, with a short tube and **2.5 mm** trilobed
segments. There are **5 stamens** with short hairy filaments, **5** ovate
staminodes with ciliate margins, and a pubescent **5-locular** ovary with
long style.

The fruit is unknown.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: type from the Ogooue at Samkita.

## Notes

The treatment says all species of [[Neolemonniera]] are imperfectly known.
This species is stated to be very close to the Ivorian
*N. clitandrifolia*, and the source warns that the type has only flowering
branchlets, whose leaves are smaller than those of sterile shoots.
