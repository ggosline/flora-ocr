# *Diospyros zenkeri* "(Gurke) F. White"

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

38.Diospyros Zenkeri (Gürke） F. White

Bull.Jard.Bot.Brux.26:241,et 244(1956)；FWTA ed.2,2:14 (1963); Nigerian Trees 2 : 342 (1964). Maba ZenkeriGURkE,Bot.Jahrb.26:63 (18g8).-MILDBRAED,Notizbl. Bot.Gart.Berl.9 :1047 (1926). Diospyros rioularis GURkE,Bot. Jahrb.46 :153 (19II).-- MILDBRAED, loc.cit. Diospyros longicaudata GURkE ex HuTcH.et DALz., Kew Bull.: 54 (1937). Specimens of this species may appear in herbaria under the following erroneous names: *Isolona* sp. and *Ptychopetalum* sp.

Shrub or small tree reaching 20-25 m in height and 20-30 cm in diameter. Rhytidome black, smooth; bark section granular, black outside, pale pink or reddish-brown within with a yellow band next to the wood; sapwood white, heartwood sometimes black. Young twigs glabrous, black in dried material.

Leaves with petiole 5-8 mm long; blade lanceolate or ovate, more rarely elliptic, reaching 12(-16) x 6.5 cm excluding the acumen, with cuneate or often rounded base narrowed onto the petiole, and more or less caudate apex with well-developed acumen up to 2.5 cm long, nearly parallel-sided, 2-3 mm wide, sometimes spatulate toward the top, with rounded tip; midrib impressed above; 2-5 pairs of lateral veins, often impressed above, anastomosing in broad loops well away from the margin; veinlet network rather loose but distinct; blade thin, subcoriaceous, glabrous, slightly glaucous beneath, blackish green to blackish in dried material; a few glands occur along and on either side of the midrib.

Male flowers subsessile in glomerules of 1-9 in the leaf axils, on old twigs, branches, and trunks; calyx broadly campanulate, 3 mm high, truncate or only obscurely bordered by 4-5 low lobes, glabrous; corolla creamy white, fragrant, glabrous, fleshy, tubular-campanulate, 10 mm long, with 3 very short 1 mm deltate-rounded obtuse lobes; 9 glabrous stamens, partly on the receptacle and partly at the base of the corolla tube, with almost absent filaments and lanceolate anthers 6 mm long ending in a 0.5 mm point; pistillode reduced to a minute nipple-like mound.

Female flowers similar to the male but in glomerules of 1-3 flowers, with calyx 4 mm high and corolla shorter, 6 mm, and broader, the lobes 2 mm and more distinct; 6 staminodes 3 mm long in 2 ranks, the inner with long filament, the outer resembling stamens; ovary ovoid, ribbed, 2 mm high, glabrous, surmounted by 3 erect stigmas less than 1 mm high, papillose on the inner and lateral faces, the ovary 6-locular.

Fruits globose, 3 cm in diameter, yellow then black, glabrous, with rough pericarp and a scarcely enlarged fruiting calyx at the base; up to 6 seeds, about 15 mm long.

**Type:** Zenker 858, Cameroon (lecto-K; iso-BM, E, P, MO).

This species is known from the evergreen humid dense forest of eastern Nigeria, Cameroon, Gabon, the DRC, and Cabinda, and may sometimes frequent river margins.
