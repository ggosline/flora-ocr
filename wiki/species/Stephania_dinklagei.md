# *Stephania dinklagei* (Engl.) Diels

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Stephania]]

In Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 265 (1910).

## Synonymy

- *Cissampelos dinklagei* Engl., Bot. Jahrb. **26**: 399 (1899).

## Description

Liana; **stem at least 3 cm thick, glabrous**, irregularly lenticellate, the
lenticels ± pustulate. **Leaves**: petiole twisted and/or curved-geniculate
at base, **3–11 cm**, glabrous; blade broadly ovate to almost orbicular,
**4–9 × 3–8 cm**, obtuse and **peltate over at least 1 cm at base**, acute
to shortly acuminate over 0.5–1 cm at apex, **glabrous on both faces**,
**with 7–10 palmate nerves at the base** and 1 or 2 pairs of lateral nerves
higher up.

**Inflorescence** cauline, sometimes axillary, puberulent to almost
glabrous; **male up to 40 cm long, reddish**; female up to 22 cm long in
fruit, with red peduncles and axes; bracts oblong-linear, ≤ 4 mm long.
**Male flower**: pedicel 1 mm; sepals elliptic, 1–1.5 mm long, puberulent
to micropustulate outside; petals obovate, 0.25–0.5 mm long, truncate and
thickened at apex; **synandrium (0.7–)1–1.5 mm tall**.

**Fruit**: mericarp ellipsoidal, **5–6 × 3 mm**, with persistent stigmas,
glabrous; **endocarp horseshoe-shaped in outline, with excavated faces and
2 ridges bearing small reflexed spines**.

## Distribution

**Range**: tropical Africa, from Guinea east to Tanzania and south to Angola.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Nyanga, Ogooué-Ivindo.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest, gallery forest. In Gabon at 60–500 m altitude, elsewhere
up to 1500 m.

## Uses

A leaf infusion is used against stomach aches. In Ghana the plant extract
is taken as a cough remedy. Leaves are used as an **aphrodisiac** and to
treat male impotence. Plant decoction is used as an emmenagogue. Stems are
taken as a sedative and analgesic. Roots treat internal parasites and seeds
are used against leprosy. The stem yields strong cordage.

## Notes

- *Stephania* is a Palaeotropical genus of about 30 species, mostly in Asia
  and Australia; five in Africa, this being the only Gabonese representative.
- Two recent collections (Moyen-Ogooué, Woleu-Ntem) with male flowers and
  peltate leaves show some affinity with *Stephania* but have markedly
  unequal sepals (vs. subequal in *Stephania*). Their identity remains
  unresolved.
