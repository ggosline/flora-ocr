# *Ancistrocladus congolensis* J.Léonard

**Family**: [[Ancistrocladaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Ancistrocladus]]

Bull. Soc. Roy. Bot. Belgique **82**: 33 (1949).

## Description

Juvenile plants unknown. Adult stems lianescent, up to 20 m tall and up to
5 cm in diameter.

**Adult leaves**: blade subcoriaceous to coriaceous, dark brown on both sides
or reddish-brown beneath when dry, bearing large circular craters 0.2–0.4 mm
in diameter. Leaves of the main stems with elliptic blades, 9.2–18.0 ×
4.8–8.5 cm, L/W ratio 1.8–1.9, cuneate to obtuse at base, obtuse to rounded
or truncate at apex; secondary nerves in 6 or 7 pairs. Leaves at the tips of
the branchlets with elliptic to narrowly elliptic blades, 7–27.8 × 3–10.1 cm,
L/W ratio 2–3.4, acute to obtuse at base, truncate to subacute and sometimes
abruptly acuminate at apex; secondary nerves in 4 to 14 pairs.

**Inflorescence**: extra-axillary, lax, the branched portion 4–5.5 ×
5.5–12 cm; peduncle 3–8 cm, relatively robust; bracts ovate to triangular or
narrowly elliptic-ovate, 0.5–2 mm long, with or without one large abaxial
gland.

**Flower**: pedicel 1.5–4 mm; sepals oblong to obovate, subequal, 5–6 ×
2–3 mm, pale green, with 1–3 abaxial glands; petals with imbricate or
convolute (rarely intermediate) aestivation, obovate, ±4 × 3–3.5 mm, yellow;
stamens in a single whorl; styles 3.

**Fruit**: turbinate-depressed; nut 8–15 × 10–20 mm; sepals woody to
chartaceous, spatulate to oblong, subequal to slightly unequal, 5–10 ×
3–4 mm.

**Seed**: 6–7 mm in diameter.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon, Republic of the Congo, and Democratic Republic of the
Congo.
**Gabon**: Ngounié, Ogooué-Maritime, Ogooué-Ivindo.

## Habitat and ecology

Periodically flooded riverine forest, or terra firma forest including hilltops,
in coastal forest, sometimes in the understorey with *Zeyherella mayumbensis*,
sometimes on sandy soils; in Gabon at 1–480 m altitude, elsewhere up to 375 m.
