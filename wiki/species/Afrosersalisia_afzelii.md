# *Afrosersalisia afzelii* (Engl.) Aubr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Afrosersalisia]]

## Description

A large tree with terminal leaf clusters on slender branchlets. Terminal buds
are ferruginous-felted; young branchlets are glabrous. Leaves are narrowly
oblong-lanceolate, obtusely pointed, sharply cuneate and slightly decurrent at
the base, glabrous and membranous, **6-13 x 2.5-4 cm**. The midrib is raised on
both faces; there are **10-15 pairs** of faint secondary veins. Petiole about
**1 cm**, slightly brown-appressed-pubescent or glabrous.

Small flowers are fasciculate and shortly pedicellate along the branchlets
below the terminal leaves. The calyx is slightly pubescent, with **5** broadly
ovate lobes united at the base. The corolla is about **3.5 mm** long, with
**5** oblong lobes longer than the tube. Stamens are as long as the lobes.
Staminodes are short and denticulate. The ovary and most of the style are
pubescent; the ovary is **5-locular**.

Fruits are red, ellipsoid, about **2.5 x 1.3 cm**, containing one brown
ellipsoid seed with a broad oblong ventral scar. The persistent calyx forms a
small cupule at the fruit base.

## Distribution

**Range**: from Sierra Leone and Liberia to Gabon according to the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Libreville and Yalala.

## Habitat and ecology

The treatment says the species grows in wet bottoms, humid ground, and along
rivers and lagoons, but may also occur on dry ground. Trees may reach **30 m**
high and **1.7 m** in diameter.

## Notes

The wood is described as extremely hard, and the treatment notes the species'
characteristic tufts of erect leaves.
