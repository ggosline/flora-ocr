# *Albertisia porcata* Breteler

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Albertisia]]

Bull. Jard. Bot. Belg. **60**: 405 (1990).

## Description

Liana climbing at least 20 m; stem up to 1 cm in diameter; branches, petioles,
nervation of the lower leaf surface and inflorescences **tomentose**.
**Leaves**: petiole cylindrical, straight or geniculate at the distal part,
**(1–)2–8(–10) cm**; blade ovate, **(10–)17–26(–29) × (5–)10–15(–19) cm**,
cordate at base, acuminate over 0.5–1.5 cm at apex; midrib and the 5–10 pairs
of lateral nerves — of which **2(3) basal pairs** — impressed above, prominent
beneath.

**Male inflorescence** unknown; **female** with solitary flowers, rarely in
3-flowered ± sessile cymes. **Female flower**: pedicel 1–3 mm; bracteoles
narrowly triangular, 5–6 × 2–3 mm, glabrous within; sepals glabrous within,
the outer ± appressed, narrowly triangular ± 4 × 2 mm, the **3 inner fused**,
narrowly ovate, ± 12 × 5 mm; petals 3, thin, transversely elliptic, ± 1.5 mm
long; carpels 11 or 12, ovaries 2 × 1 mm, tomentose; styles up to 5 mm,
glabrous.

**Fruit**: mericarp subellipsoidal, laterally compressed, up to **4.5 × 3.5
× 2.5 cm, with prominent ridges**, stipitate over ± 1 mm, **yellow at
maturity**, densely tomentose; exocarp 2–6 mm thick, mesocarp pulpy, 2–4 mm
thick; endocarp ligneous, 1 mm thick, rugose outside, smooth inside. **Seed**
ellipsoidal, laterally compressed, up to 2 × 1–1.5 cm.

## Distribution

**Endemic to Gabon**, collected twice in Ogooué-Lolo.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest and riparian forest, at ± 300 m altitude.

## Notes

- The closest Gabonese congener is the new species
  [[Albertisia_badia|*A. badia*]], which differs by chestnut-brown indument
  and much narrower leaves with a single dominant basal pair of lateral
  nerves.
- The undescribed [[Albertisia_sp_nov|*A. sp. (nov. ?)*]] resembles
  *A. porcata* in leaf size and shape but differs in being **hirsute** (vs.
  tomentose) and by having 3 or 4 (vs. 2 or 3) long basal nerve pairs.
