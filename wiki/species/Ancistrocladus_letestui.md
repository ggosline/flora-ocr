# *Ancistrocladus letestui* Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Ancistrocladaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Ancistrocladus]]

Bull. Soc. Bot. France **98**: 18 (1951).

## Description

Juvenile plants shrubby, branched, up to 2 m tall. Juvenile leaves clustered
toward the tips of the branches, with very narrowly elliptic-ovate blades,
29–35.5 × 1.4–1.6 cm, L/W ratio 20.7–25.4, long-attenuate at base, caudate-
acuminate then narrowly rounded at apex, papery; secondary nerves in ±40
pairs. Adult stems climbing up to 30 m and up to 5 cm in diameter.

**Adult leaves**: blade chartaceous, dark brown above, lighter brown beneath
when dry, bearing usually oblong (sometimes circular) large craters
(0.5–)2–4 × 0.5–1 mm — distinctively the largest craters in the Gabonese
species. Leaves of the main stems with very narrowly obovate-elliptic to
narrowly ovate-elliptic blades, 4–42.9 × 1.1–5.1 cm, L/W ratio 3–17.2,
truncate to long-attenuate at base, acute to rounded at apex; secondary
nerves in 8 to 33 pairs. Leaves at the tips of the branchlets with very
narrowly obovate-elliptic to narrowly obovate-elliptic blades, 7.5–32.4 ×
1–6.7 cm, L/W ratio 3.5–15.5, long-attenuate at base, rounded to attenuate
at apex; secondary nerves in 8 to 23 pairs.

**Inflorescence**: extra-axillary or subterminal, lax, the branched portion
6–13 × 12–23 cm; peduncle 2.2–6.2 cm, sometimes curved. Bracts at the start
of anthesis narrowly ovate-elliptic to narrowly obovate-elliptic and prolonged
at base, 1–1.9 × 0.6–0.8 mm, with one large abaxial gland; at the end of
anthesis 3–3.5 mm long and becoming **cochleariform to claviform** by
enlargement of the abaxial gland and incurving of the apex — a unique
character among the Gabonese species.

**Flower**: pedicel 4–8 mm; sepals broadly oblong, subequal, 2–3 ×
1–2.1 mm, orange-red, often with 1 or 2 abaxial glands; petals convolute in
bud, suborbicular, ±2.5 × 2.5 mm, cream-coloured; stamens in 2 whorls;
styles 3.

**Fruit**: turbinate; nut ±6 × 7 mm; sepals chartaceous, unequal — the 3
larger obovate-elliptic, ±34 × 11–12 mm; the 2 smaller obovate-elliptic to
spatulate, ±21 × 8–10 mm.

**Seed**: not seen.

## Distribution

**Range**: Nigeria, Cameroon, Gabon, Republic of the Congo.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Moyen-Ogooué, Ogooué-Ivindo, Ogooué-Lolo,
Ogooué-Maritime.

## Habitat and ecology

Mature forest, ridges, streamsides; in Gabon at 125–450 m altitude, elsewhere
up to 950 m.

## Notes

Two characters are diagnostic for *A. letestui* and separate it cleanly at
couplet 1 of the [[Ancistrocladus#Key]]:

1. Adult apical leaves with very narrow blades (L/W ratio 3.5–15.5) and very
   large oblong craters ((0.5–)2–4 × 0.5–1 mm).
2. Bracts that **transform during anthesis** — narrowly ovate-elliptic and
   basally prolonged at the start, becoming spoon- or club-shaped at the end
   as the abaxial gland enlarges and the apex incurves.

The juvenile habit (shrubby, with extraordinarily long, narrow papery leaves
up to 35 cm × 1.6 cm and ~40 nerve pairs) is also distinctive but not
included in the published key, which keys only on adult material.
