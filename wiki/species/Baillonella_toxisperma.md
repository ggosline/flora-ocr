# *Baillonella toxisperma* Pierre

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Baillonella]]

## Description

A **massive emergent tree** with very thick branchlets bearing many leaf scars
and dense clusters of persistent stipules near the tips. Young leaves are
rusty-hairy. Mature blades are very elongate-obovate, **20-30 x 6-10 cm**,
rounded and abruptly acuminate at the apex, cuneate at the base, with many
prominent secondary veins arching near the margin. They become nearly glabrous
except for some rusty hairs on the midrib beneath. Petioles are **3-4 cm**
long.

Flowers are densely fasciculate at the ends of branchlets on pubescent
pedicels **2-3 cm** long. There are **8 sepals**, and the corolla has **8
lobes**, each with **2 dorsal appendages** longer than the lobe itself. The
**8 staminodes** are longer than the stamens and hairy outside at the base.
The ovary is hirsute and **8-locular**.

Fruits are large, spherical, about **6.5 cm** in diameter, grey-green, and
contain **1-2 seeds** in whitish-yellow pulp. Seeds are ellipsoid, about **4.2
x 2.5 x 2 cm**, with the ventral scar occupying almost the entire strongly
convex ventral face; the seed coat is thin.

## Distribution

**Range**: southern Nigeria to Cameroon and across Gabon, extending south to
Mayombe, lower Kouilou, and Cabinda; absent from the main Congolese forest
bassin according to the treatment.  
**Gabon**: said to cover nearly all of Gabon except, apparently, the plain
forests of the "serie des cirques", and to occur throughout the Ogooue basin
to the Franceville region.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary humid forest. The treatment describes it as one of the most
characteristic and impressive emergent trees in Gabon, reaching **45-50 m**
high with **25-30 m** of clear bole and diameter exceeding **3 m**.

Leaf fall accompanies flowering in **September-October**, and fruits mature in
**December**. The tree exudes latex and is locally used medicinally for wound
healing according to the cited source.

## Notes

The treatment spends several pages disentangling the old confusion between
[[Baillonella_toxisperma|*Baillonella toxisperma*]] and
[[Tieghemella_africana|*Tieghemella africana*]], especially around Pierre's
use of the epithet `toxisperma`. It also recognizes a southern form as var.
*obovata*, but does not maintain it at species rank.
