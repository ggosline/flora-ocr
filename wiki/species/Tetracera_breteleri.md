# *Tetracera breteleri* Niang., O.Lachenaud & Sosef, *spec. nov.*

**Family**: [[Dilleniaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tetracera]] · **Section**: *Tetracera*

A species described as new in this treatment ([[vol60]]). The Latin diagnosis
in the protologue:

> Similar to *T. alnifolia* in its entire leaf blade, winged petiole,
> many-flowered inflorescences and flower with 3 petals and 5 or 6 erect to
> patent sepals that are glabrous inside, but with twigs, petioles, bracts and
> exterior of sepals densely soft pubescent, upper leaf surface scabrid with
> short fasciculate hairs, and lower leaf surface with a dense felt of beige
> woolly hairs intermixed with longer soft hairs.

## Type

**Holotype**: Gabon, Ogooué-Lolo, Makande surroundings, about 65 km SSW of
Booué, c. 0°41′S 11°55′E, 3 February 1999 (fl.), *F.J. Breteler et al. 14904*
(WAG [WAG.1578273]); isotypes WAG (2 sheets).

## Description

Large liana; branches glabrescent, the bark exfoliating in strips; branchlets
with **felted indument covering the surface and sparser long, soft, spreading
hairs**.

**Leaves**: petiole canaliculate and narrowly winged, **1.5–3.5 cm**, hairy
like the branchlets but with the long hairs often denser; blade
elliptic-obovate to elliptic, 7–13 × 4–9 cm, rounded to acute at base, obtuse
to rounded or slightly emarginate at apex, the margin entire and revolute,
chartaceous, **discolorous** — the upper surface green and bearing short
fasciculate, very scabrid hairs together with sparser simple hairs; the lower
surface with a **dense felt of beige woolly hairs entirely covering the
surface**, intermixed with sparser long soft hairs. Lateral nerves in 12–20
pairs, reaching the leaf margin; tertiary venation scalariform, fairly dense
but ± hidden by the indument.

**Inflorescence**: many-flowered, terminal and axillary, 8–30 cm long, a
panicle of 2–5-flowered cymes; bracts elliptic, 2–10 mm long, pilose like
the branchlets.

**Flower**: pedicel 0 to 7 mm, pilose like the branchlets; sepals **5 or 6**,
erect or ± spreading, elliptic, 5–8 mm long, **± densely pubescent externally**,
sparsely puberulent within. Petals 3, obovate to broadly obovate-elliptic,
5–7 mm, probably yellowish white. Stamens 2–3 mm long. Carpels pubescent.

**Fruit**: unknown.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon and Republic of the Congo.
**Gabon**: a single collection from the Forêt des Abeilles, Ogooué-Lolo —
the type locality. The species is rare.

**Other material studied**: Republic of the Congo, Prov. Kouilou, M'Vouti,
4°32′S 12°17′E, 17 June 1989, *Dechamps 13051* (BR); Forêt Bangou, 29 December
1971, *L. Makany 1953* (P).

## Habitat and ecology

Forest edges; at approximately 200–400 m altitude.

## Etymology

Named in honour of Frans J. Breteler, the collector of the type specimen, who
already recognised it as a new species in 2004. The authors note: "It is our
pleasure to dedicate it to this great botanist, an exceptional connoisseur of
the African flora and especially that of Gabon, who has devoted a great part
of his life — long after his retirement — to the advancement of the *Flore du
Gabon*."

## Notes

- The remarkable felted indument on the lower leaf surface resembles that of
  *T. boiviniana* Baill. of East Africa, but the latter is a shrub or small
  tree with a dentate leaf margin, simple hairs above and reticulate tertiary
  venation; it has short pauciflorous inflorescences and bilobed petals, and
  thus belongs to section *Akara*.
- *T. breteleri* is also close to *T. eriantha* (Oliv.) Hutch., known from
  Nigeria and Bioko (Equatorial Guinea), but that species has decurrent
  leaf bases, only weakly discolorous leaves, non-felted pubescence, and
  sepals densely silky on both faces.
- This is a **type locality in Gabon** — Gabon contributes the holotype of a
  novelty in this treatment.
