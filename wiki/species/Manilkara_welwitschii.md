# *Manilkara welwitschii* Engl.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Manilkara]]

## Synonymy

- *Mimusops welwitschii* Engl.

## Description

Small tree. Leaves small, elongate-obovate or lanceolate, obtusely acuminate or
simply attenuate at the apex, cuneate at the base, **5-9 x 2-4 cm**, at first
felted beneath and later apparently glabrous. Midrib impressed above and
prominent beneath; lateral veins very faint. Petiole about **8 mm** long and
not exceeding **1 cm**.

Floral buds scurfy-tomentose. Flowers axillary, few, in fascicles, shortly
pedicellate. Staminodes irregularly bidentate at the apex. Ovary with
**10-12** locules.

## Distribution

**Range**: based on the treatment, this species occurs in Angola and also in
material compared from Cote d'Ivoire; the Gabon material cited is from
Mayumba.  
**Gabon**: littoral white sands near Mayumba.

## Habitat and ecology

Littoral white-sand thickets in the Gabon material cited by the author. The
treatment also compares it with a riparian shrub or small tree in Cote
d'Ivoire.

## Vernacular names

minzimou.

## Source

Aubreville A. (1961) *Sapotacees*. In: Aubreville A. (ed.), *Flore du Gabon,
Volume 1*. Museum national d'Histoire naturelle, Paris.

## Notes

The treatment presents this as the closest small-leaved counterpart to
[[Manilkara_microphylla]], but separates it by the absence of the deeply
impressed venation network and by the different insertion of stamens and
staminodes.
