# *Chasmanthera dependens* Hochst.

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Chasmanthera]]

Flora **27(1)**: 21 (1844).

## Synonymy

- *Chasmanthera welwitschii* Troupin, Fl. Congo belge, Rwanda-Urundi,
  Spermat. **II**: 241 (1951), **syn. nov.**

## Description

Subligneous liana, up to **30 m** long; branches striate, **pubescent to
hirsute**, sometimes hispiduleous. **Leaves**: petiole 4–14 cm,
**twisted-curved-geniculate at base**, hirsute; blade **suborbicular to
pentagonal**, 10–16 × 10–22 cm, deeply cordate at base, acuminate to obtuse
at apex, with recurved hirsute margins; primary nerves 5–7, hirsute.

**Inflorescences** axillary, **pendent**: male inflorescence (15–)30–60 cm
long; female 15–22 cm long. **Male flower**: pedicel ± 2.5 cm; outer sepals
very narrowly triangular, 1.5–2 mm long, pubescent without; inner sepals
2.5 × 1.5 mm, with a tuft of hairs at apex; petals ± equal, concave, ± 1.5
× 1 mm, fleshy, glabrous; **stamens** in a synandrium 1.5 mm long, glabrous.
**Female flower**: pedicel 4–5 mm; sepals and petals as in male; staminodes
± 1.2 mm; **carpels glabrous**.

**Fruit**: mericarp **globose**, ± 12 × 8–12 mm, glabrous.

## Distribution

**Range**: widespread in tropical Africa, from Sierra Leone in the west to
Ethiopia and Somalia in the east, and south to Angola and Zambia.
**Gabon**: Ngounié, Nyanga.

## Habitat and ecology

Flooded forest and dense terra firma forest. In Gabon up to 300 m, elsewhere
up to ± 1500 m.

## Uses

Bark and leaves have many uses in traditional medicine. A **fish poison** is
prepared from leaves mixed with those of *Tephrosia vogelii* Hook.f. The
roots are added to palm wine to give it more strength. The Balumbu of Gabon
use the stems as cordage in house building and for making fishing nets.

## Vernacular names

byale-mikos, byale-esamekar (fang); dyang-lókwè (bakèlè); imwitsi-madungu
(ngowé); lukakanza (loango); mondjondju (apindji); ogóli w'aloko (nkomi);
ontsi-ngóma (mpongwè); tsisa (balumbu); ukódi-mwa-élandi (benga).

## Notes

- *C. welwitschii* Troupin is **here placed in synonymy** with *C. dependens*.
  Already in his 1962 monograph, Troupin doubted its validity, and his later
  identifications in Paris show that he accepted only one species in the
  genus, although he never formally published the synonymy.
