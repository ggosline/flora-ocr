# *Tulestea koulamoutouensis* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tulestea]]

## Description

Leaves are oblong, acuminate with a **1.5-2 cm** acumen, cuneate at the base,
with distinctly **undulate margins**, glabrous, and up to **30 x 12 cm**. About
**12** secondary veins join a marginal vein following the sinuosities of the
margin; fine nervules run parallel.

Flowers are in dense fascicles in the leaf axils. Pedicels are **4 mm** long
and pubescent. The calyx has **5** pubescent lobes. The corolla is glabrous,
about **5 mm** long, with oblong apiculate lobes and a very short tube. Stamens
are as long as the lobes; filaments are very short. Anthers are lanceolate,
apiculate, and hairy on the inner face. Staminodes are scale-like. The ovary is
pubescent and **5-locular**.

Fruit was unknown to the treatment.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Koulamoutou.
