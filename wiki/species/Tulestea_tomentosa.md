# *Tulestea tomentosa* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tulestea]]

## Description

A subshrub to about **3 m**. Terminal buds and young branchlets are rusty-hairy.
Leaves are lanceolate, long-acuminate, long and sharply cuneate at the base,
slightly decurrent onto the petiole, **10-20 x 3-4 cm**, and pubescent beneath.
There are about **8-10 pairs** of secondary veins; the midrib is raised on both
faces. Petiole about **1 cm**, hairy.

Flowers are fasciculate in the leaf axils. Pedicel about **2 mm**, hairy.
There are **5** free hairy sepals. The corolla is glabrous, about **2-2.5 mm**
long, with very short tube and ovate lobes. Stamens are shorter than the lobes;
filaments are very short; anthers are slightly hairy outside. Staminodes are
scale-like. The ovary is hirsute and **5-locular**.

Fruit was unknown to the treatment.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Louba, Lastoursville region.
