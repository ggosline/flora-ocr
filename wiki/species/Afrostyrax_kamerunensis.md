# *Afrostyrax kamerunensis* Perkins & Gilg

**Family**: [[Huaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Afrostyrax]]

In Perkins, *Bot. Jahrb. Syst.* **43**: 217 (1909).

## Description

Shrub **2-5(-9) m** tall; branchlets stellate-tomentellous, later glabrescent.

**Stipules**: rapidly caducous, **3 x 1 mm**, with slight stellate-pubescent
indument outside.

**Leaves**: petiole **(4-)5-8(-9) mm** long, stellate-tomentellous; blade
papery, ovate-elliptic, **(2.5-)6.5-13.5(-18.5) x (1.5-)2.5-6(-6.5) cm**,
glabrous, occasionally with a few stellate hairs on the veins beneath in young
leaves; base obtuse to subcordate; apex abruptly and narrowly acuminate for
**0.5-2.5(-4.5) cm**; lateral veins **(3-)4-5(-6) pairs**.

**Flowers**: solitary or in groups of 2 or 3; bracts and bracteoles elliptic,
about **1 mm** long; pedicel **6-10 mm** long, with rather sparse stellate
hairs. Calyx tearing into **2 or 3** more or less reflexed lobes, subcircular
to elliptic, **3-4 x 2-3 mm**, outer surface with rather sparse stellate hairs.
Petals obovate, **3-4.5 x 1-2.5 mm**, greenish white to pale yellow, with
stellate-tomentellous hairs outside and stellate-pubescent hairs inside; claw
**0.5 mm**, glabrous. Filaments **0.2-0.5 mm** long, slightly united at base,
glabrous; anthers about **1 mm** long, dorsally stellate-pubescent. Pistil
about **2 mm** long; ovary **1-2 mm** in diameter, densely stellate-tomentellous;
style pointed, with the same indument in the lower part.

**Fruit**: ovoid, **2-3 x 1-2.8 cm**, dark brown, with stellate-tomentellous
indument.

**Seed**: subglobose, about **17 mm** in diameter, testa smooth and shining.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, Congo (Brazzaville), and the
Democratic Republic of the Congo.  
**Gabon**: present, but the source does not specify provinces.

## Habitat and ecology

Humid dense forest, to **350 m** altitude.

## Vernacular names

esun, eswinya, bole (Fang); findji (Kota); sindjakalo (Myene); mufira (Punu).

## Uses

The bark and seeds are used locally as garlic-flavoured condiments.

## Notes

Among Gabonese *Afrostyrax*, this species is the one with the fewest lateral
vein pairs and the shortest filaments. It differs from
[[Afrostyrax_macranthus]] in its non-revolute blade margin and dorsally hairy
anthers, and from [[Afrostyrax_lepidophyllus]] in lacking the dense scurfy
stellate indument on the lower leaf surface.
