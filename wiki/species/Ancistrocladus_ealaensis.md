# *Ancistrocladus ealaensis* J.Léonard

**Family**: [[Ancistrocladaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Ancistrocladus]]

Bull. Soc. Roy. Bot. Belgique **82**: 30 (1949).

## Description

Juvenile plants unknown. Adult stems lianescent, up to 12 m tall and up to
4 cm in diameter.

**Adult leaves**: blade chartaceous to subcoriaceous, greyish brown to dark
brown above, reddish brown beneath when dry, bearing large circular craters
0.3–0.5 mm in diameter. Leaves of the main stems with elliptic to narrowly
obovate-elliptic blades, 3.4–29.1 × 1.6–9.8 cm, L/W ratio 2.1–4.6, acute to
acuminate at base — sometimes pseudopetiolate — narrowly acuminate or
sometimes obtuse to rounded at apex; secondary nerves in 8 to 15 pairs.
Leaves at the tips of the branchlets with elliptic to narrowly obovate-
elliptic blades, 5–24.9 × 2.2–8.1 cm, L/W ratio 2.5–4.3, cuneate to acute at
base, rarely pseudopetiolate, acute to acuminate or sometimes obtuse to
rounded at apex; secondary nerves in 5 to 12 pairs.

**Inflorescence**: extra-axillary, lax, the branched portion 3–6 × 4–7 cm;
peduncle 2–6 cm, slender; bracts triangular to ovate, 0.2–1.5 mm long, with
or without one large abaxial gland.

**Flower**: pedicel 2–6 mm; sepals oblong to spatulate, subequal, 4–5 ×
2–3 mm, pale green, two or three sepals bearing 1 or 2 abaxial glands;
petals convolute or imbricate in bud, suborbicular, 2–3 × 2–3 mm, golden
yellow; stamens in a single whorl; style 1.

**Fruit**: patelliform; nut 4–5 × 7–8 mm; sepals spatulate to obovate or
oblong, subequal to unequal, 5–10 × 4–8 mm, coriaceous.

**Seed**: ±6 mm in diameter.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon, Republic of the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo,
and Central African Republic.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Moyen-Ogooué, Ngounié, Ogooué-Ivindo.

## Habitat and ecology

Lake margins and riverine forest, dry ridges, slightly disturbed secondary
forest; in Gabon and elsewhere at 20–400 m altitude.
