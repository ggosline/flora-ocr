# *Ancistrocladus guineensis* Oliv.

**Family**: [[Ancistrocladaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Ancistrocladus]]

Fl. trop. Afr. **1**: 175 (1868).

## Synonymy

- *Ancistrocladus uncinatus* Hutch. & Dalziel, Fl. W. trop. Afr. **1**: 196 (1927).

## Description

Juvenile plants shrubby, unbranched or sparsely branched, up to 3 m tall;
juvenile leaves unknown. Adult stems climbing up to 15 m and up to 5 cm in
diameter.

**Adult leaves**: blade chartaceous to coriaceous, dark brown above and lighter
brown below, or greyish-beige on both sides when dry, bearing large circular
to ovate craters 0.3–1 mm in diameter. Leaves of the main stems with elliptic
to narrowly obovate-elliptic blades, 5.5–23.1 × 2.6–8.8 cm, L/W ratio 2.3–4.8,
truncate to cuneate at base, shortly acuminate to rounded at apex; secondary
nerves in 4 to 11 pairs. Leaves at the tips of the branchlets with obovate to
narrowly obovate-elliptic blades, 7.2–33 × 3–9.4 cm, L/W ratio 2.3–4.8,
attenuate or pseudopetiolate at base, shortly acuminate to rounded at apex;
secondary nerves in 7 to 16 pairs.

**Inflorescence**: extra-axillary or subterminal, lax, the branched portion
5–15 × 12–20 cm; peduncle 1.5–8 cm, straight; bracts triangular-ovate, 0.8–1.6
× 0.5–1 mm, with one large abaxial gland.

**Flower**: pedicel 2–5 mm; sepals oblong to elliptic-oblong, **unequal**,
pale green tinged with reddish purple, sometimes with 1–4 abaxial glands; the
3 larger 3.5–5.1 × 1.4–2.5 mm; the 2 smaller 3.2–4.2 × 2–2.9 mm. Petals
imbricate in bud, oblong-obovate, ±8.1 × 3.2 mm, pale yellow tinged with pink;
stamens in 2 whorls; styles 3.

**Fruit**: turbinate; nut 5–8 × 3–6 mm; sepals papery, unequal — the 3 larger
spatulate to narrowly obovate-elliptic, 28–42 × 13–16 mm; the 2 smaller
oblong-spatulate, 5–16 × 4–6 mm.

**Seed**: not seen.

## Distribution

**Range**: Nigeria, Cameroon, Gabon.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Moyen-Ogooué, Ngounié, Ogooué-Ivindo, Ogooué-Lolo,
Ogooué-Maritime.

## Habitat and ecology

Forests (sometimes on sandy soils), including secondary forests, roadsides,
and streamsides; in Gabon and elsewhere at 20–600 m altitude.

## Notes

The conspicuously **unequal sepals** (both in flower and especially in fruit,
where the 3 larger reach 28–42 mm and the 2 smaller stay under 16 mm) are
diagnostic in the [[Ancistrocladus#Key]] and separate this species from the
otherwise similar [[Ancistrocladus_ealaensis|A. ealaensis]] and
[[Ancistrocladus_congolensis|A. congolensis]].
