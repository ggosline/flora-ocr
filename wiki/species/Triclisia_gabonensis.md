# *Triclisia gabonensis* Jongkind & Breteler, spec. nov.

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Triclisia]]

Described in: Breteler F.J. (2022) *Menispermaceae*. Flore du Gabon **60**:
85. **Spec. nov.**

**Diagnosis** (Latin, original): *Triclisiae ripariae* Troupin similis, ab
ea differt indumento rigido longo adpresso ramunculorum petiolorum
fructuumque et flore femineo habitenti tantum 15 carpella versus 30–40 in
*Triclisia riparia*.

**Type**: Gabon, Haut-Ogooué, 23 km on the Moanda–Bakoumba road, 1°40′S,
13°06′E, in secondary forest, 21 Oct. 1970, *Breteler 6993* (holo-: WAG
[WAG0082982, WAG0082983]; iso-: LBV).

## Description

Liana, **2 m** tall; **branches and petioles densely covered with stiff,
brown, appressed hairs 0.8–1.5 mm long**. **Leaves**: petiole often
thickened at both ends, straight or distally geniculate, **0.5–2.5(–3.5)
cm**; blade oblong-elliptic, **(4–)7–10(–19.5) × (1.5–)3–5(–9.5) cm**,
obtuse to rounded at base, acute to acuminate over 0.5–1.5 cm and often
apiculate over 1–2 mm at apex, papyraceous to coriaceous, **with 4 to 6(7)
pairs of lateral nerves**, midrib prominent above and (with the principal
venation beneath) bearing long, brown, appressed hairs, glabrescent.

**Inflorescence**: male unknown; **female ramiflorous**, ± sessile,
cymulate-glomerulate, few-flowered, with brown hairs. **Female flower**
subsessile; sepals **± 18**, with brown hairs, the 15 outer ovate, 1–2.5
mm long, acute to acuminate at apex, the 3 inner erect, free, elliptic-ovate,
concave, **4–5(–6) × 2 mm**; petals 0; staminodes 0; **carpels ± 15**,
2–2.5 mm long, with stiff brown hairs.

**Fruit**: mericarp globose, slightly compressed, **2–2.5 × 2 × 1.5 cm,
with a beak 5–8 mm long**; exocarp 1 mm thick, with stiff brown hairs
0.8–1.5 mm; mesocarp 3–4 mm thick; endocarp ovoid-ellipsoidal, ± 15 × 7
mm, with marked condyle. **Seedling** with well-developed taproot;
hypocotyl absent; first leaves opposite, ovate, shortly petiolate,
subcordate at base, emarginate at apex; subsequent leaves alternate, more
long-petiolate.

## Distribution

**Endemic to Gabon**: Haut-Ogooué, Nyanga, Ogooué-Lolo.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest at 250–650 m altitude.

## Notes

- Closest to [[Triclisia_riparia]] but distinguished by **stiff, long,
  appressed hairs** on branches, petioles and fruits (vs. tomentose in
  *T. riparia*) and by having only **15 carpels** in the female flower
  (vs. 30–40 in *T. riparia*).
- Male flower remains **unknown**.
