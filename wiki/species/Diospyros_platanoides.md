# *Diospyros platanoides* "R. Let. & F. White"

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

27. Diospyros platanoides R. Let. & F.White

Adansonia9: 281 (196g). - F. WH1rE,Nigerian Trees 2 : 344 (1964) ((sp.B)).

P1. 27, 19-23, p. 167, et CARTE 31, p. 172.

Small tree reaching up to 20 m in height and 30 cm in diameter, with trunk distinctly fluted; bark apparently smooth but finely longitudinally striate, gray or dark purple, exfoliating in circular scales up to 12 cm across; bark section pale yellow outward and darker inward; wood yellowish white. Young branchlets, petioles, and the undersides of the youngest leaves bear an appressed strigillose pubescence that is more or less caducous.

Leaves with petiole not exceeding 5 mm, very flattened and broadened above; blade oblong-elliptic or oblong-lanceolate, 7.5-11.5 x 3-4.5 cm, obtuse at the base, with acumen up to 10 x 3 mm and rounded tip; midrib slightly impressed above; 7-9 pairs of lateral veins loop-anastomosing well away from the margin, with accessory lateral veins and a well-marked network of veinlets beneath; blade subcoriaceous, glabrous except sometimes for strigillose hairs persisting beneath the veins, dark green-blackish in dried material; 2 glands are almost constantly present on either side of the midrib near the base.

Male inflorescences axillary or below the leaves, in subfascicled cymes of 2-5 subsessile flowers, with very small triangular bracts and bracteoles pubescent outside. Flowers with calyx about 2 mm long, glabrous, with 3-4 broad rounded lobes reaching one third of the calyx depth; corolla urceolate, 6 x 3.5 mm, with 4 lobes reaching one quarter of its depth, glabrous outside and sparsely strigillose within the tube; about 14 stamens inserted at the base of the corolla tube in 2 irregular series, with flattened filaments 1.5-2.5 mm long, their margins more or less setulose, and lanceolate apiculate anthers 0.75 mm long with setulose margins; pistillode ovoid, 2.5 x 1 mm, apiculate, glabrous.

Female inflorescences, female flowers, and fruits unknown.

**Type:** White 8559, Cameroon (K; iso-FHO, P).

This species, very close to *D. soubreana* of semi-deciduous humid dense forest from Ivory Coast to western Nigeria, is known in Nigeria only from the eastern region and nearby western Cameroon in the Mamfe area; there it is restricted to evergreen humid dense forest. *Diospyros platanoides* has also been found in Gabon near Lastoursville.
