# *Tiliacora klaineana* (Pierre) Diels

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tiliacora]]

In Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 65 (1910).

## Synonymy

- *Glossopholis klaineana* Pierre, Bull. Soc. Linn. Paris n.s.: 84 (1898).
- *Sebicea klaineana* Pierre ex Diels in Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 65
  (1910).
- *Tiliacora odorata* Engl., Bot. Jahrb. Syst. **26**: 400 (1899), **syn.
  nov.**
- *Tiliacora ovalis* Diels in Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 64 (1910), **syn.
  nov.**
- *Sebicea ovalis* Pierre ex Diels in Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 64 (1910).
- *Tiliacora stipularis* Pierre ex Diels in Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 65
  (1910), *pro parte*, fruits only.

## Description

Liana; branches finely striate, **glabrous**. **Leaves glabrous**: petiole
canaliculate to wrinkled above, **(5–)7–15(–20) mm**, generally geniculate
at apex; blade ovate-elliptic, **(8–)10–16 × (4–)5–9 cm**, rounded to
cuneate at base, acuminate over 1–1.5(–2) cm at apex, with **4 to 8 pairs
of lateral nerves**, often with one basal pair longer than the others.

**Inflorescence** puberulent; **male in pseudoraceme**, often fascicled,
**at least 20 cm long**; cymules scattered along the axis, sometimes
sessile, stipitate over 1–3 mm, with 1 to 3 flowers; **female racemose,
(4–)5–7(–10) cm long, with solitary flowers**; bracts narrowly triangular,
1–1.5 mm long. **Male flower**: sepals ciliolate, the 6 outer broadly
ovate to deltoid, 1–2 mm long and broad, the 3 inner narrowly elliptic to
narrowly elliptic-obovate, **(5–)7–8 × 3 mm**; petals 6, oblong, 1.5–2 mm,
acute to obtuse or notched at apex. **Female flower**: sepals and petals
similar to those of the male; **carpels 20 to 25, ± 2 mm long, glabrous**.

**Fruit**: mericarp obovoid, **2–2.5 × 1–1.3 cm**, glabrous.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Gabon.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Ngounié, Ogooué-Ivindo.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary and secondary forest. Up to ± 200 m altitude.

  klaineana* (**syn. nov.**).
