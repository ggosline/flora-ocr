# *Dialytheca gossweileri* Exell & Mendonça

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Dialytheca]]

J. Bot. **73**, Suppl. Polypet. **1**: 9 (1935).

## Description

Liana; stem sparsely lenticellate, with **very small spines**. **Leaves**:
petiole subcylindric, wrinkled when dry, **2–6(–8) cm**, with curved and/or
twisted basal part; blade ovate-elliptic, **6–8 × 4–6 cm**, **deeply and
broadly cordate at base**, acuminate over 5 mm, with principal nerves
prominent on both faces — **2 basal pairs and 2 lateral pairs**.

**Male inflorescence** up to 8 cm long; peduncle ± 1 cm; bracts narrowly
ovate, ± 1 mm long. **Male flower** orange in bud; pedicel 1.5–3 mm; outer
sepals broadly ovate to orbicular, 0.6–0.7 mm long, the inner obovate, 2–2.5
× 2–2.5 mm; outer petals orbicular, concave, **thick**, 1–1.5 mm in diameter;
inner petals obovate, **thick**, 1 mm long; **stamens with filaments almost
entirely fused**, 1.2 mm.

Female flower and fruit unknown.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon and Angola (Cabinda).
**Gabon**: collected once in Nyanga.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest at ± 150 m altitude.

## Notes

- *Dialytheca* is **monospecific**. The combination of three stamens with
  filaments fused into a column with a broadened, triangular connective —
  and extrorse anthers — distinguishes the genus from all other Gabonese
  Menispermaceae.
