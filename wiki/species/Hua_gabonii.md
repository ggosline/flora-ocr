# *Hua gabonii* Pierre

**Family**: [[Huaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Hua]]

Drawing by Delpy in herb. Pierre, based on Klaine 2859b, distributed in 1904
(or at latest 1905); *Ann. Mus. Congo Belge, Bot., sér. 5* **1**: 289, pl. 65
(1906).

## Synonymy

- *H. parvifolia* Engl. & Krause, *Bot. Jahrb. Syst.* **45**: 338, fig. 4 (1911)

## Description

Shrub **2-8 m** tall; branchlets sparsely puberulent to glabrescent.

**Stipules**: triangular, **0.5-1 mm** long, rapidly caducous; outer face with
whitish hairs, inner face glabrous.

**Leaves**: petiole **(3-)4-7(-10) mm** long, channelled above and transversely
wrinkled when fresh, glabrous; blade elliptic to obovate-elliptic,
**(5-)8-19(-28) x (1.5-)2.5-7(-8.5) cm**, glabrescent, with circular glands
especially toward the base; base obtuse; apex abruptly and narrowly acuminate
for **0.5-1.5(-2) cm**; lateral veins **(6-)8-9(-11) pairs**.

**Flowers**: fasciculate in groups of **(2-)7-12(-17)**; bracts and bracteoles
more or less triangular, **0.5-1 mm** long; pedicel slender,
**(4-)8-14(-18) mm**, glabrescent. Sepals **5**, elliptic, **3-4 x 1-1.5 mm**,
inner face with a marginal row of glands. Petals **4 or 5**,
obovate-triangular, **1-2 x 1-1.4 mm**, dark red to purple, pubescent except
dorsally near the apex; claw filiform, **2 mm** long, glabrous. Filaments
**0.2-0.4 mm** long; anthers **0.2 mm** long, glabrous. Ovary **0.5 mm** high,
with dense simple hairs; style simple; stigma minute.

**Fruit**: ovoid, **1.5-2.5 x 1-2 cm**, glabrous, yellow-green, opening by
**5 involute valves**.

**Seed**: ovoid-ellipsoid, **15-20 x 10-15 mm**, testa pale brown, veined and
slightly shining.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Gabon, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo; the
source notes that it should also be looked for in Congo (Brazzaville).  
**Gabon**: present, but the source does not specify provinces.

## Habitat and ecology

Humid dense forest, to **420 m** altitude.

## Vernacular names

esun, eswinya, bole (Fang); findji (Kota); sindjakalo (Myene); mufira (Punu).

## Uses

The bark and seeds are used locally as garlic-flavoured condiments.

## Notes

This is the only species of [[Hua]]. Within Huaceae it is immediately notable
for its free sepals, peltate long-clawed petals, single basal ovule, and
capsule opening by 5 valves.
