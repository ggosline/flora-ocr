# *Diospyros viridicans* Hiern

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

37.Diospyros viridicans Hiern

Journ.Bot.59 :129 (192I).- F.WHITE,FWTA ed.2,2 :IO (1963); Nigerian Trees 2 :34o (1964). -Diospyros Kekemi AUBREv.et PELLEGR.，Bull. Soc.Bot.Fr.83 ：621tab.1,1 (1937).

PL.12,14-26, p. 95,et CARTE 35, p. 176.

Tree reaching 20-25 m in height and 40 cm in diameter, with long straight bole and narrowly conical crown of short spreading branches, the young twigs puberulent then glabrous; rhytidome blackish, at first smooth, then fissured and scaly; bark very thin; wood white. Branching remarkably pseudosympodial, with shelf-like projections at the base of the petioles, and the twigs are also often angular. In one Gabonese specimen, Le Testu 8009, the young twigs are covered with a tawny pubescence that is quickly shed; the petioles there are likewise pubescent and relatively short.

Leaves with slender petiole 1.5-2 cm long, channelled at least in the upper part; blade elliptic, oblong-elliptic, or somewhat obovate, reaching 17(-20) x 11 cm but often smaller, cuneate at the base and often narrowed onto the top of the petiole, abruptly and shortly acuminate at the apex; midrib impressed above; 5-8 pairs of ascending arched lateral veins, prominent beneath, linked by a finely raised network of parallel veinlets more or less perpendicular to the midrib; blade glabrous, glossy above, usually reddish-brown or olive-brown when dry; apparently without glands beneath. In the same Gabonese specimen, Le Testu 8009, the lower surface is distinctly pubescent, especially along the veins, with curved hairs.

Male inflorescences in small loose cymes with pubescent peduncle 4 mm long, axillary or borne on older branchlets, with 12-20 flowers; pedicels 2-4 mm, pubescent, articulated at the summit; floral bud rounded at the top. Calyx not exceeding 2.5 mm, narrowed at the base, glabrous except for a few small hairs on the margin, divided nearly to the middle into 4 obtuse or somewhat orbicular lobes; corolla 6-8 mm long, glabrous, divided nearly to the base into 4 oblong lobes, the tube not exceeding 2 mm; 20-30 stamens strongly exserted, with pubescent filaments 0.5-1 mm long, united to the base of the corolla tube and among themselves in groups of 2-3, and narrowly oblong anthers about 3 mm long, very slightly pubescent dorsally; pistillode rudimentary, with a few hairs.

Female inflorescences similar to the male but with fewer flowers, 8 or more; floral bud broadly acute at the apex. Female flowers with calyx prolonged downward for 1 mm and articulated on the pedicel, above divided into 4-5 triangular lobes 3 mm long, distinctly convolute, the outside with fine loose appressed pubescence and the lobe margins ciliate; corolla 5 mm high with 4-5 oblong lobes 4 mm long; 4-5 rudimentary staminodes at the base of the corolla and ovary; ovary ovoid-conical, 1.5 mm, glabrous, directly surmounted by 4-5 more or less oblanceolate stigmas 1.5 mm long and 8-10-locular.

Fruits shortly pedicellate, about 5 mm, globose, 2-3 cm in diameter, glabrous, yellow then red then black at maturity; fruiting calyx with reflexed lobes about 4-6 x 2.5-3 mm; usually 4 brown seeds with wrinkled surface, 10-12 x 8 mm, with ruminate endosperm.

**Type:**Gossweiler 6955,Cabinda.

This species occurs in humid dense forests, apparently both evergreen and semi-deciduous, from Sierra Leone, Ivory Coast, and Ghana through Nigeria, Cameroon, and Gabon to both Congos and Cabinda.
