# *Beirnaertia cabindensis* (Exell & Mendonça) Troupin

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Beirnaertia]]

Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **29**: 220, fig. 12 (1959).

## Synonymy

- *Tiliacora cabindensis* Exell & Mendonça, J. Bot. **73**, Suppl. Polypet.,
  Addenda: 7 (1935).
- *Beirnaertia yangambiensis* Louis ex Troupin, Bull. Jard. Bot. État
  Bruxelles **19**: 420, t. 18 (1949).

## Description

Liana with **stems and leaves glabrous**; stems finely striate when dry.
**Leaves**: petiole finely striate when dry, straight or geniculate distally,
**(2–)3–6(6.5) cm**; blade ovate to elliptic, **(5–)7–10(–11.5) × (3–)5–6(–7.5)
cm**, broadly rounded at base, acuminate over about 0.5 cm, **coriaceous**,
3-nerved at base and with (1)2 or 3 additional lateral nerves on each side.

**Inflorescence** a panicle of 2- to 4-flowered, ± glomerular cymules, at
least 15 cm long, **glabrous**, with slender ramifications 1.5–4.5 cm.
**Male flower** ± sessile; outer sepals 1 × 0.5 mm, membranous and ciliolate;
inner sepals 2.8–3.5 × 1–1.6 mm, glabrous; petals ± 0.6 × 0.2 mm, glabrous;
**stamens 3**, 2–2.5 mm long. **Female flower**: staminodes inserted at the
base of the gynophore, ± 1 mm long; gynophore 1 mm; carpels subovoid, 1–1.5
mm long, with minute style.

**Fruit**: mericarp ellipsoidal, **5–7 × 4–5 cm** (note: large), **orange at
maturity**; mesocarp 0.5 cm thick; endocarp 2–3 mm thick. **Seed 4–7 × 1 cm**.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon, Republic of the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo,
Central African Republic, Angola (Cabinda).
**Gabon**: Haut-Ogooué, Moyen-Ogooué, Ngounié.

## Habitat and ecology

Dense humid forest, gallery forest. In Gabon at **70–420 m**, elsewhere up to
600 m.

## Notes

- *Beirnaertia* is monospecific.
- The distinctive **3-merous male flower with only 3 free stamens** sets the
  genus apart from other Menispermaceae in Gabon.
