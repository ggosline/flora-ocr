# *Syntriandrium preussii* Engl.

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Syntriandrium]]

Bot. Jahrb. Syst. **26**: 412, Taf. 24 (1899).

## Description

Suffrutescent liana with **glabrous stem** (sometimes puberulent). **Leaves
heteromorphic, simple to lobed or 2- to 3-foliolate**. **Petiole** ±
cylindrical, **5–11 cm**, twisted and/or curved-geniculate at base; petiolules
of lateral leaflets sometimes absent, 1–3 mm; that of the terminal leaflet
up to 3.5 cm. Blade of the entire leaf or terminal leaflet obovate-elliptic,
**9–12 × 4–8 cm**; **lateral leaflets very asymmetric at base**; terminal
leaflet sometimes cordate; all shortly acuminate over 0.5 cm and often
mucronate at apex, **glabrous**, with 5–9 pairs of lateral nerves.

**Inflorescence** finely puberulent; **male up to 1.5 m long**, female up
to 20 cm, sessile or with very short peduncle; bracts 1–1.5 mm long.
**Male flower**: pedicel 1–4 mm; outer sepals subovate, ± 0.8 mm long;
inner sepals suborbicular, 1.4–1.6 mm long; petals concave, elliptic, 1–1.2
mm long, fleshy; **stamens 0.6–0.8 mm long**. **Female flower**: pedicel
± 5 mm; outer petals 2 × 1.5 mm, inner 1–1.5 mm; carpels 1.5 mm.

**Fruit**: mericarp ovoid-ellipsoidal, **± 1.5 × 1 cm**, smooth, glabrous,
**greenish, dotted with white**. Seed 0.8–1 cm long.

## Distribution

**Range**: Côte d'Ivoire, Nigeria, Cameroon, Gabon, Democratic Republic of
the Congo, Central African Republic.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Ogooué-Lolo.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest. In Gabon up to 300 m altitude, elsewhere up to 600 m.

## Notes

- *Syntriandrium* is **monospecific** in tropical Africa. Diagnostic
  vegetative feature: **heteromorphic, sometimes 2- or 3-foliolate
  leaves** — unique among Gabonese Menispermaceae, where leaves are
  otherwise simple.
- The very long male inflorescence (to 1.5 m) is also unusual.
