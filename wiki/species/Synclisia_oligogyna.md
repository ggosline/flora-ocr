# *Synclisia oligogyna* Breteler, spec. nov.

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Synclisia]]

Described in: Breteler F.J. (2022) *Menispermaceae*. Flore du Gabon **60**:
68. **Spec. nov.**

**Diagnosis** (Latin, original): *Synclisiae scabridae* Miers similis, ab ea
differt indumento multo breviore ramunculorum foliorumque, foliis sine nervis
lateralibus prominentibus, sepalis brevioribus et minus numerosis carpellis.

**Type**: Gabon, Woleu-Ntem, 5 km on the road from Tchimbélé to Kinguélé,
3 km NW of Tchimbélé, 0°38′N, 10°23′E, alt. 560 m, 2 Feb. 1990, *Wieringa 514*
(holo-: WAG [WAG1102863, WAG1102864]; iso-: LBV).

## Description

**Slender liana**, **puberulent on branches, petioles and inflorescences,
with appressed hairs ˂ 0.2 mm**. **Leaves**: petiole ± cylindrical, **1.7–3.5
cm**, slightly thickened; blade ovate, **5.5–11.5 × 2–4.5 cm**, broadly
rounded and very faintly peltate at base, acuminate over (0.5–)1–1.5 cm at
apex, smooth, shiny and glabrous above, puberulent on midrib and lateral
nerves beneath; midrib impressed above, prominent beneath; **with 4 or 5
pairs of lateral nerves, without basal pairs**.

**Male inflorescence** unknown. **Female inflorescence** with solitary
flower or in 3-flowered racemes; bract narrowly elliptic-ovate, 2 mm long,
puberulent. **Female flower**: pedicel **7–12 mm**, puberulent; **outer
sepals triangular, ± 0.5 mm**, puberulent outside; **inner sepals fused,
free and pointed at apex, ± 5 mm long**, orange, puberulent at outer base
with silvery hairs; petals 3, broadly ovate, concave, ± 0.5 mm long,
pubescent outside, glabrous inside; staminodes not seen; **carpels 5 to
7**, 2–2.5 mm long, with ovoid, hirsute ovary ± 1.5 mm long; styles hirsute
at base, ± 1 mm long (stigmas included).

**Fruit** unknown.

## Distribution

**Endemic to Gabon**, known from a single collection in Woleu-Ntem.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest at 560 m altitude.

## Notes

- One of two species of [[Synclisia]] in Gabon (the other is the more
  widespread [[Synclisia_scabrida]]). Distinguished from *S. scabrida* by
  the much shorter indumentum (˂ 0.2 mm vs. ≥ 1 mm), the leaf nerves not
  prominent, fewer carpels (5–7 vs. 15–20) and shorter sepals.
- Known only from the **female-flowered holotype**.
