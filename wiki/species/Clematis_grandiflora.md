# *Clematis grandiflora* DC.

**Family**: [[Ranunculaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Clematis]]
**Subgenus**: *Campanella* Tamura · **Section**: *Viorna* (Pers.) Prantl

Syst. nat. **1**: 151 (1817).

## Description

Liana climbing by means of its petioles, leaf rachides or petiolules. Stem
(1–)5–10(–15) m long, up to 1 cm in diameter, faintly **10- to 15-furrowed**,
sparsely pubescent to glabrescent.

**Leaves**: generally **once-imparipinnate, 5-foliolate**; petiole and rachis
together reaching 10–25 cm; petiole (2–)4–9(–12) cm; petiolules (3–)5–20(–35)
mm, often **twining**. Leaflets ovate to elliptic, **5–14 × 2.5–8 cm**,
cordate or sometimes rounded at base, acute-acuminate (rarely rounded) at
apex, with margin denticulate to serrate or sometimes unequally lobed,
papyraceous to subcoriaceous, pubescent to glabrescent on both faces.
Primary nerves (sub)palmate, parallel-arched, 5 to 9 — the inner 3
long-ascending — rarely with a few pinnate nerves. Reticulation very dense,
particularly on the lower face.

**Inflorescence**: a **raceme 8–50 cm long**, composed of 1 terminal flower
and 1–3 pairs of opposite flowers subtended by leaf-like bracts. Peduncles,
rachis, pedicels, bracts, buds and outer face of the sepals all covered with
fawn-coloured pubescence. Peduncle 1–9 cm. Bracts ovate, acute, 3-lobed or
entire, with irregularly dentate margin.

**Flower**: bisexual, **3–5 cm in diameter**; pedicel 1–2.5(–3.5) cm.
Receptacle conical, 6–8 mm in diameter, densely hairy. Sepals **4, erect**,
ovate-elliptic to elliptic, **3–5 × 1.5–2 cm**, **strongly revolute at the
apex**, **greenish yellow**, glabrous within, with prominent longitudinal
nerves on the outer face. Stamens **150–200**, 20–26 mm long; filament
compressed, densely hairy; anther linear, 3–4 mm, glabrous, obtuse at apex.
Carpels **up to 250**; ovaries ovoid, 1 mm long, densely covered with
silvery hairs; style 15–20 mm at flowering, **7–10(–12) cm in fruit**,
densely covered with silvery hairs.

**Fruit**: achenes fusiform, compressed, 4–5 × 1–2 mm, hairy; styles with
spreading hairs ± 8–15 mm long.

## Distribution

**Range**: a widespread species, from Guinea to Ghana, southeastern Nigeria
(Cross River State), Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea (including Bioko), Gabon,
Republic of the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Uganda, northwestern
Tanzania, Ethiopia, and northern Angola (Cuanza Norte, Cabinda). The number
of herbarium collections suggests the species is **commoner in West than in
Central Africa**.
**Gabon**: known from only **two collections**, from Nyanga and Ogooué-Lolo.
Nevertheless, the species is reported as widespread by Raponda-Walker &
Sillans (1961).

## Habitat and ecology

Hygrophilous on heavy soils — forest edges and clearings, along streams, in
gallery forests, savanna groves, secondary forest, occasionally in primary
forest, and beside coffee plantations in Ethiopia. In Gabon at 100–650 m
altitude, elsewhere at 0–2000(–3300) m. Fruiting in July–August (both Gabonese
collections).

## Uses

The plant is decorative and recommended for cultivation by Staner & Léonard
(1951).

## Notes

- **Not to be confused** with *Clematis montana* Buch.-Ham. ex DC.
  'Grandiflora', often called *Clematis* 'Grandiflora', a temperate-region
  garden cultivar.
- The distribution of the two Gabonese *Clematis* species suggests that
  [[Clematis_hirsuta|*C. hirsuta*]] **prefers savanna and even semi-arid
  regions**, while *C. grandiflora* is **confined to wetter forests**.
