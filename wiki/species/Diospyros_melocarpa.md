# *Diospyros melocarpa* F. White

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

24.Diospyros melocarpa F. White

Bull.Jard.Bot.Brux.33:347,tab.19,J-O (1963)；FWTA ed. 2,2 ：14(1963);Nigerian Trees 2 : 34o (1964). Specimens of this species appear in various herbaria under the following name: *Maba melocarpa* (Louis ms.).

PL. 26,12-20,p. 159,et CARTE 31, p. 172.

Small tree reaching 15(-30) m in height and 30 cm in diameter, with straight bole slightly fluted at the base; crown small, branchlets blackish; rhytidome gray-black, exfoliating in numerous small papery scales; bark brittle, green then black on the outer oblique section, orange-pink to brown within, without the usual well-developed yellow cambium; wood pink, hard, becoming more brownish toward the center, with black-bordered fissures.

Leaves with petiole up to 8 mm long, channelled above; blade broadly ovate-elliptic to lanceolate-elliptic, up to 9 x 5.5 cm excluding the acumen, with cuneate or rounded base and caudate-acuminate apex; narrow acumen up to 15 mm long, with parallel margins and rounded or slightly spatulate tip; midrib slightly impressed above; 3-5 pairs of ascending lateral veins, joined in faint loops, veinlets invisible; blade glabrous, with scattered black glands beneath, often dull dark reddish-brown above and paler, somewhat ruminate-looking beneath in dried material.

Male inflorescences subsessile, axillary or below the leaves, sometimes on old wood, with 1-20 flowers on pedicels 1-2 mm long. Calyx glabrous, cup-shaped, 2.5 mm high, with entire or slightly denticulate and faintly ciliate margin; corolla yellowish, coriaceous, conical and obtuse at the apex in bud, glabrous, tubular, 8 mm high, with 3 deltate lobes 1 mm long; about 12 stamens inserted on the receptacle, with filaments 1 mm long, strigose, and narrowly lanceolate anthers 4 mm long, long-apiculate, about 1 mm, densely strigose at least on the lower half of the connective; pistillode rudimentary, 0.5 mm, glabrous.

Female inflorescences and flowers unknown.

Fruits very shortly pedicellate, with thick pedicel 6 mm long, subglobose, about 3.2 x 3.5 cm, yellow, perhaps later red, glabrous, containing 5-6 seeds, and surrounded at the base by the slightly accrescent calyx, irregularly 3-4-lobed. Seed 26 x 15 x 12 mm, dark brown, transversely roughened, with slightly ruminate endosperm.

**Type:** Gilbert Dir.for. 200, DRC (BR; iso-BM, K, MO, P).

This species is known from Nigeria, Cameroon, Fernando Po, Gabon, and both Congos; it occurs in evergreen humid dense forest and is known in Cameroon only from the Mamfe, Douala, and Kribi regions, and in Gabon only from the Lastoursville region.
