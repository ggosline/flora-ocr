# *Tiliacora macrophylla* (Pierre) Diels

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tiliacora]]

In Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 67 (1910).

## Synonymy

- *Glossopholis macrophylla* Pierre, Bull. Soc. Linn. Paris n.s. **1**: 84
  (1898).
- *Tiliacora soyauxii* Engl., Bot. Jahrb. Syst. **26**: 401 (1899).

## Description

Liana; **stem and petiole puberulent, often quickly glabrescent**.
**Leaves**: petiole ± cylindrical, **(1–)1.5–2.5 cm**, **distal part
generally much thickened over (0.5–)1–1.5 cm and often geniculate**;
blade obovate-oblong-elliptic, **13–26 × 5–11 cm**, obtuse-rounded to
slightly cuneate at base, acuminate over (0.5–)1–1.5 cm at acute apex,
glabrous on both faces or with puberulent midrib and lateral nerves
beneath, **with 6 to 8(9) pairs of lateral nerves**, with fine,
prominent reticulation beneath.

**Inflorescence** in pseudoracemes, puberulent; **male fascicled**, with
cymules stipitate over (0.4–)1–1.5(–2.5) cm and ± 12 flowers; female
similar to the male but not fascicled, 4–7 cm long. **Male flower**:
sepals 9, the 6 outer ovate to suborbicular, 0.5–1.7 × 1.2–1.5 mm, acute
at apex, the 3 inner narrowly elliptic-oblong-obovate, **5.5–6(–7) ×
2–2.5(–3) mm**; petals 6, narrowly elliptic-oblong-obovate, ± 2.5 mm
long, acute at apex; stamens 6, slightly exserted, **(4.5–)6–6.5 mm
long**, glabrous, with **filaments fused at least to the middle**.
**Female flower**: sepals and petals similar to those of the male, the
inner sepals smaller, 2.5–4 mm long; gynophore axis discoid at apex,
1.5–3 mm tall; carpels ± 15, ovary 0.5 mm, glabrous.

**Infructescence** 4 cm long and broad. **Fruit**: mericarp obovoid,
curved, **15–18 × 10–12 × ± 8 mm**, smooth, glabrous, **yellow at
maturity**.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Gabon.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Moyen-Ogooué.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary and secondary forest at low altitude (≤ 50 m).

## Notes

- Distinguished from the other Gabonese [[Tiliacora]] species by the
  **strongly thickened distal part of the petiole** and the leaves with
  **6–8(9) pairs of lateral nerves but no dominant basal pairs and no
  domatia**.
