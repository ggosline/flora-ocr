# *Omphalocarpum procerum* P. Beauv.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Omphalocarpum]]

## Description

A **large tree** with glabrous young branchlets and rusty-tomentose terminal
buds. Leaves are lanceolate, not acuminate or only very shortly and obtusely
so, very narrowly cuneate and decurrent at the base, **8-13 x 3-5 cm**,
glabrous, with **6-7 pairs** of secondary veins. The petiole is long and
slender, reaching **4 cm**.

Flowers are sessile and surrounded by broad ovate bracts tomentose outside,
about **8 mm** long. The corolla is about **2.5 cm** long, with lobes **16
mm** long and tube **9 mm**.

Fruits are large, flattened-globose and depressed at the apex, about **15 cm**
in diameter.

## Distribution

**Range**: one of the wide-ranging species of the genus, extending from the
Upper Guinea region east to the Congo basin according to the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Tchibanga and Campement Ngoungou-na-Pounga.

## Habitat and ecology

Humid forest. The source does not provide a more specific habitat note for the
Gabon collections.
