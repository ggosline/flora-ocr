# *Albertisia* sp. (nov. ?)

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Albertisia]]

A possibly undescribed species, recorded once in Gabon. Not formally named
in this treatment because the available material is sterile (only an
infructescence with three drupe scars; no fruit, no flowers).

## Description

Liana; branches, petioles and lower leaf-surface nervation **hirsute**.
**Leaves**: petiole cylindrical, geniculate at the apex, 4–13 cm; blade
ovate, **18–31 × 9–16 cm**, cordate at base, acuminate over 0.5–1.5 cm and
sometimes with a curved apicule reaching 1.5 cm at apex; venation impressed
and ± glabrous above, prominent beneath, with **8–10 pairs of lateral nerves
of which 3 or 4 basal pairs**.

Inflorescences and flowers (male and female) unknown. Infructescence on a
leafy branch, axillary, sessile, with **three drupe scars**. Fruit unknown.

## Distribution

**Endemic to Gabon**, collected once in Ogooué-Lolo.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest at ± 300 m altitude.

## Source

Breteler F.J. (2022) *Menispermaceae*. In: Sosef M.S.M., Florence J.,
Bourobou Bourobou H.P. & Bissiengou P. (eds), Flore du Gabon, Volume 60.
Margraf Publishers, Weikersheim. doi:10.5281/zenodo.14900487

## Notes

- Resembles [[Albertisia_porcata|*A. porcata*]] in leaf size and shape but
  differs by **hirsute** indumentum (vs. tomentose in *A. porcata*) and by
  **3 or 4 dominant basal nerve pairs** (vs. 2(3) in *A. porcata*).
- Formal description awaits flowering and fruiting collections.
