# *Diospyros preussii* Gurke

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

29.Diospyros Preussii Gurke

Bot.Jahrb.14: 313 (1892),et 26:71 (1898).-F.WH1TE,FWTA ed.2,2: 14(1963); Nigerian Trees 2: 332 (1964). -Diospyros Ledermannii GURkE, Bot. Jahrb.46 : 152 (1gI1),syn. nov. - Diospyros Letestui PELLEGr., Bull Mus. nat. Hist. nat. 30 : 328 (1924); Fl. Mayombe 2 : 17,tab. 4 (1928). - Diospyros Klaineana DE WILD.ex PIERRE, Pl. Bequaert. 3 : 544 (1926), syn. nov. Specimens of this species appear in various herbaria under the following name: *Diospyros zenkeri* (Gurke ms.); cf. Bot. Jahrb.26:71 (1898).

Shrub reaching up to 10(-15) m in height and 20(-30) cm in diameter at the base, but more often not exceeding 5 m, with slender green branchlets becoming blackish-brown or grayish, glabrous. Rhytidome black with superficial vertical fissures and a few cauliflorous protuberances; bark black in section outside, pale yellow to pinkish inside.

Leaves with glabrous petiole 5-15 mm long, channelled above; blade oblanceolate or oblanceolate-elliptic, up to 46 x 16 cm, base cuneate-rounded onto the petiole, exceptionally broadly rounded, margin somewhat cartilaginous and recurved beneath, at least in dried material, apex shortly acuminate, 1-2 cm, with rounded acumen tip; midrib slightly raised and rounded above, more or less keeled beneath; 6-10(-15) pairs of arched ascending lateral veins, at least in the upper half of the blade, discreetly anastomosing near the margin, prominent on both surfaces together with the dense network of veinlets; blade glabrous, glossy green above and pale green beneath when fresh, becoming glossy grayish green above and pale grayish-brown beneath with mineralized epidermis when dry; black glands scattered over the entire lower surface.

It would be desirable to verify in the field whether this species is dioecious like apparently all the other *Diospyros* treated in this flora, or instead monoecious, with male and female flowers on the same individual. Gurke mentioned that Zenker 852 bore both flower types; in the Paris herbarium Le Testu 2298 also appears, from the surviving drawings, to include both male and female flowers. The usual reliability of those collectors argues in favor of probable monoecy. The same is suggested by the Nigerian specimen Latilo FHI 41335, and possibly by the Wageningen material De Wilde 1785, which may have been split after collecting into 1785A with male flowers and 1785B with female flowers.

Male flowers crowded in cymes of 30 or more on old branchlets and the trunk, with slender pedicels 2 mm long, articulated at the summit. Calyx campanulate, tightly clasping the base of the corolla, 2.5 mm long, glabrous, with 4 acute triangular lobes reaching half its height; corolla white, glabrous, salverform, 15 mm long, with 4 obtuse lanceolate-elliptic lobes 5-6 mm long; (14-)16 stamens inserted on the receptacle in pairs, with filaments 4-6 mm long united for about 3-5 mm and with acute lanceolate anthers about 3 mm long, pubescent; pistillode ovoid, 2-3 mm, more or less ribbed, sometimes 2-toothed, glabrous.

Female flowers very different from the male in the calyx, but gathered likewise on old branchlets and the trunk, with pedicel 5 mm long, articulated at the summit and becoming stouter after anthesis. Calyx accrescent, subcoriaceous, reddish, glabrous, campanulate, 8 mm high, with 4(-5) broad cordiform lobes 6 mm wide, acute at the apex, reaching almost to the base of the calyx, soon reflexed outward, with undulate margins; corolla white, glabrous, salverform, 10 mm long, with 4(-5) obtuse elliptic lobes as long as the tube; 8(-10) subulate villous staminodes, 6-8 mm long; ovary ovoid, 5 mm, glabrous, with 8(-10) locules, clearly surmounted by 4(-5) styles 8-9 mm long united in their lower half and ending in erect oblong stigmas.

Fruits shortly pedicellate, at most 8 mm, ovoid-conical, apiculate at the summit, 3 x 2 cm, somewhat ribbed, bright red then brown, glabrous, smooth, and almost completely hidden by the accrescent calyx, which may reach 5 cm, coriaceous, dark purple, without visible venation, and divided to half its height into 4(-5) large lobes with reduplicate, at least in young fruit, undulate margins; seeds 4-8(-10), 20 x 10 mm.

**Type:**Preuss 474, Cameroun (delet.P,lecto- de F.WHIrE (1968): Braun s. n., Cameroun (BM).

This species is reported in Nigeria only from the eastern regions of Ogoja, Uyo, and Calabar, then from Cameroon and Gabon; it favors evergreen humid dense forest and often occurs on low swampy ground.
