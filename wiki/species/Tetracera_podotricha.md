# *Tetracera podotricha* Gilg

**Family**: [[Dilleniaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tetracera]] · **Section**: *Tetracera*

Bot. Jahrb. Syst. **33**: 200 (1902).

## Synonymy

- *Tetracera alnifolia* Willd. var. *demeusei* De Wild. & T.Durand, Bull. Soc. Roy. Bot. Belg. **39**: 53 (1900).
- *Tetracera alnifolia* Willd. var. *podotricha* (Gilg) Staner, Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **15**: 304 (1939).

## Description

Liana up to 20(–40) m long; stem up to 13 cm in diameter; wood yellowish.
Branches glabrous to pubescent; branchlets sparsely pubescent to hirsute,
with fasciculate hairs intermixed with longer simple hairs.

**Leaves**: petiole **winged**, 1–2.5 cm, **usually long-villous**; blade
elliptic to obovate, (3–)6–20(–31) × (2–)3–10(–14) cm, cuneate to obtuse or
rounded at base, obtuse to rounded or sometimes truncate and shortly apiculate
at apex, entire to dentate at the margin, papery to coriaceous, smooth to
slightly scabrid on both faces, dark green and glabrous to pubescent above,
pale green and ± densely pubescent on the nerves (and sometimes between them)
beneath. Lateral nerves in 8–17 pairs, **often deeply impressed (gaufré)
above**, generally reaching the leaf margin; tertiary venation scalariform.

**Inflorescence**: many-flowered, a panicle of 3–7-flowered cymes, 10–30 cm
long, generally exceeding the subtending leaf considerably; axes hirsute to
pubescent; bracts narrowly elliptic-ovate, 1–2 mm long.

**Flower**: fragrant; pedicel 0.5–4 mm; bud globose to ellipsoid, 2.5–4 mm
wide; sepals **4(5), reflexed**, suborbicular to elliptic, pale green often
becoming reddish in fruit; the outer 3–4 × 1.5–2.5 mm, the inner 4–5 ×
2.5–3.3 mm; sparsely pubescent externally, glabrous within, ciliate. Petals
3, obovate-oblong to elliptic, 4–5 × 2.5–3.5 mm, white, rounded or slightly
emarginate at apex. Filament 2–5 mm; anther white to pale yellow. Carpels
(2)3(4), glabrous to scabrid.

**Fruit**: with 1–3(4) **divergent** follicles, ovoid to ellipsoid, **5–7(–8)
× 3–5(–6) mm**, abruptly narrowed into a beak 1–3 mm long, lustrous, glabrous
to scabrid, green becoming reddish to red-brown.

**Seed**: 3 mm long (including aril), brown-black; aril deeply lacerate.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea (Río Muni), São Tomé, Gabon, Republic
of the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Central African Republic,
Angola (Cabinda and north).
**Gabon**: common and widely distributed in all provinces.

## Habitat and ecology

Dense or degraded forests, edges, on terra firma or flooded ground, sometimes
on inselbergs; in Gabon at 5–780 m altitude, elsewhere at 0–700 m. The species
is heliophilous in its young stages, when it colonises gaps and forest edges,
but persists in forest long after canopy closure.

## Vernacular names and uses

**French**: *liane à eau*, *liane du voyageur*. Probably the same uses as
[[Tetracera_alnifolia|*T. alnifolia*]] — the two species are very similar and
often confused.

## Notes

- *T. podotricha* differs from the other Gabonese species in its **reflexed
  sepals**.
- The species has long been treated as a synonym of [[Tetracera_alnifolia]],
  but the two are easily distinguished, at least in Gabon. *T. podotricha* is
  widespread in central Africa, while *T. alnifolia* there appears restricted
  to the coastal regions along the Gulf of Guinea (extending only a little
  inland through the Ogooué basin).
- Material formerly cited as *T. stuhlmanniana* Gilg or *T. leiocarpa* Stapf
  in the Checklist of the Vascular Plants of Gabon (Sosef et al. 2006)
  belongs to *T. podotricha*. *T. stuhlmanniana* is a synonym of *T. potatoria*;
  whether *T. leiocarpa* is also synonymous with *T. podotricha* requires more
  West African material to settle, but in any case *T. podotricha* is the
  oldest unambiguous name for the Gabonese plants.
- Some specimens from the Congo basin assigned to *T. podotricha* var.
  *glabrescens* De Wild. (1906) resemble *T. podotricha* in flower size but
  have nearly glabrous leaves and sepals not always reflexed; their status
  needs further study.
