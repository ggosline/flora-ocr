# *Manilkara fouilloyana* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Manilkara]]

*Bull. Soc. Bot. Fr.* **104**: 278 (1957).

## Description

Large tree. Leaves oblong-obovate, with obtuse but acuminate apex and cuneate
base, **12-15 x 5-6 cm**, very coriaceous, grey- or reddish-felted beneath.
Midrib very prominent below; numerous fine lateral veins scarcely visible above
and hidden below by the felted indument; petiole **1.5-2 cm** long.

Flowers axillary in fascicles. Pedicels **2-3 cm** long, ferruginous-tomentose.
Sepals oblong, obtuse, **5 mm** long, pubescent outside and glabrous inside.
Corolla **6 mm** long: lobes **3.5 mm**, tube **2 mm**, appendages **4 mm**;
the tube is notably thick at the point where stamens and staminodes are united.
Stamens **6**, about **2.5 mm** long. Staminodes ribbon-like and laciniate at
their tips. Ovary tomentose, with **11-13** uniovulate locules, prolonged by a
glabrous style about **7 mm** long.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon, Cameroon (Douala), and nearly to the southern edge of the
Guineo-Congolian forest in the Central African Republic (Bangou forest).  
**Gabon**: especially frequent in the Monts de Cristal according to the source.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary forest on terra firme; not a riparian species. The source describes it
as a tall straight-boled tree of very humid and rainy regions.

## Notes

The treatment separates this species from [[Manilkara_lacera]] chiefly by its
larger flowers on stouter pedicels and by its ribbon-like laciniate
staminodes. It is presented as the Gabonese ecological counterpart of
*Manilkara sylvestris* from primary forest in Cote d'Ivoire.
