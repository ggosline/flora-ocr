# *Diospyros bipindensis* Gurke

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

4. Diospyros bipindensis Gurke

Bot.Jahrb.26 ：7O (r8g8).— M1LDBRAED，Notizbl.Bot.Gart.Berl.9：1051 (1926).-F.WHITE,FWTA ed.2,2:11,tab.202B,3 (1963).

-D.Büsgenii GURkE,Bot.Jahrb.46:153(1911).

-D. flavovirens GURkE in MILDBRAED,Wiss. Ergebn.Deutsche Zentral-Afr.Exped. 1907-08,2 : 525 (1914).

PL.1,p.37,et CARTE 32,p.173.

Shrub or tree rarely more than 20 m tall, the bole shallowly fluted; bark dark
brown, apparently smooth but with very close, vertically anastomosing fissures,
very hard and crumbling under machete blows. In oblique section the bark shows
a black outer rhytidome, then a cream layer stained yellow toward the outside
and ochre-yellow inward; sapwood white then pale yellow; heartwood white to
yellowish, sometimes black. Crown rounded, with arching branches. Young
branchlets green and cylindrical, later grey-blackish and densely fissured,
very often bearing ellipsoid swellings and more rarely spherical ones.

Leaves with a 5-10(-15) mm petiole; blade subcoriaceous, oblong and sometimes
slightly obovate, reaching 10-15(-26) × 4-6(-10) cm, usually cuneate-obtuse at
the base and a little decurrent onto the top of the petiole, ending abruptly in
an acumen up to 2.5 cm long with a blunt tip. Midrib slightly impressed above,
as are the 5-8 pairs of lateral veins, which join in loops well away from the
margin; minor venation rather lax. Dry blade olive-brown; distinct glands seem
to be absent beneath the blade, though a few scattered glands may occur on each
side of the midrib, at least near the axils of the lower lateral veins.

Flowers very fragrant, with a creamy-yellow corolla, solitary in the leaf axils
and on young hardened shoots, sometimes 2-3 together in female flowers and more
rarely 2-4 in male ones, on short pedicels 1-3 mm long. Male flowers pendulous
beneath the branches; calyx pale green, about 8 mm long, cup-shaped but
narrowed and prolonged at the base for 1-1.5 mm to the pedicel articulation,
with 4 narrowly triangular lobes about 5 × 4 mm, blunt at the apex and
apparently glabrous but actually bearing short, thick, black, pointed,
appressed-ascending hairs both outside and inside, with a tuft at the tip of
each lobe. Corolla 2 cm long, with 4 oblong lobes 10-12 mm long, blunt at the
apex, glabrous. Stamens 16 in two series, inserted on the corolla tube; their
1-2 mm filaments are slightly pubescent, as is the base of the 3 mm long
linear-lanceolate anthers; the same black hairs seen on the calyx extend onto
the inner base of the corolla tube. Pistillode apparently absent. Female
flowers with a 10 mm calyx divided almost to the base into 4 triangular,
cordate, acuminate lobes, apparently glabrous or sometimes bearing black hairs
like those of the male calyx; the calyx becomes accrescent and reddish brown in
fruit, with papery to subcoriaceous erect lobes 3-4 cm tall, their margins
undulate and not reduplicate, the venation fan-like. Corolla glabrous,
18-20 mm long, with a 4-5 mm tube and 4 oval lobes 14-15 × 7-8 mm, blunt at
the tip. Staminodes 8, rudimentary. Ovary ovoid-conical, 3-4 mm long,
glabrous, 8-locular, topped by 4 linear styles with erect lanceolate stigmas as
long as the ovary.

Fruit on a pedicel about 5 mm long, at maturity whitish-pruinose and perhaps
later orange, at first globose and enclosed within the enlarged calyx, later
truncated-pyramidal with 4(-5) ridges, apiculate, as long as the calyx
(about 2.5 cm), and containing 7-8 seeds up to 18 mm long.

**Syntypes:** Zenker 746, 864, and 933, Cameroon (864 and 933: iso-P).

Widespread in Cameroon, Gabon, both Congos, Angola (Lunda), and Uganda, in
evergreen humid forest and also near semi-deciduous forest. The wood is said to
be used for making crossbow bows.
