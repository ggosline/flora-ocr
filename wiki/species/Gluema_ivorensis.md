# *Gluema ivorensis* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Gluema]]

## Description

A tree with young branchlets finely rusty-tomentose. Leaves are grouped at the
ends of branchlets, elongate-obovate to oblong-lanceolate, acuminate and
mucronate, cuneate at the base, **12-32 x 3-9 cm**, apparently glabrous but
with minute traces of pubescence beneath even on older leaves. There are
**12-18 pairs** of lateral veins, prominent beneath, with a characteristic
raised network of veinlets. Petiole **2-5 cm**, channelled near the blade.

Flowers are axillary in fascicles below the leaves. Pedicels are rusty
tomentose and **1.2-1.5 cm** long. The **5** calyx lobes are ovate and
tomentose outside. Corolla lobes are **6 mm** long and each bears **2 dorsal
appendages** of equal length with slightly denticulate margins. There are **5
stamens** opposite the petals and **5** acute subtriangular staminodes,
pubescent on both faces and coalescent around the style in bud. The ovary is
hirsute and **5-locular**.

The fruit, described from Ghanaian material, is ovoid, attenuate at both ends,
about **6.5 x 3.5 cm**, tomentose, and laterally dehiscent. The single seed is
about **5 x 2.3 x 1.5 cm**, with a narrowly oblong scar extending the full
length of the ventral face.

## Distribution

**Range**: Ivory Coast and Gabon according to the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Depouye, Zonangue, and Libreville km 18.

## Habitat and ecology

Especially along rivers. The tree reaches moderate height but may exceed
**1 m** in diameter; the bole is tortuous and without buttresses, and latex is
abundant.

## Notes

The treatment says the venation recalls [[Lecomtedoxa_heitzana]], but the
presence of even faint pubescence beneath the leaf blade is a useful field
distinction pointing to [[Gluema_ivorensis]].
