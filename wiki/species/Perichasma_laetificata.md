# *Perichasma laetificata* Miers

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Perichasma]]

Ann. Mag. Nat. Hist., ser. 3, **18**: 22 (1866).

## Synonymy

- *Stephania laetificata* (Miers) Benth., in Benth. & Hook.f., Gen. pl.
  **1**: 962 (1867).

## Description

Liana with woody base, at least **10 m** long; **stem hispid, brownish
yellow**. **Leaves**: petiole ± cylindrical, wrinkled when dry,
**(2–)4–10(–14) cm**, geniculate/curved at base, hispid, glabrescent or not;
blade broadly ovate to suborbicular, **(8–)14–17(–22) × (7–)11–17(–21) cm**,
obtuse at base, acute, sometimes slightly acuminate and often mucronate at
apex, sometimes ± obscurely and coarsely crenate at margin, glabrous above
or sparsely hispid with appressed hairs and glabrescent, ± sparsely tomentose
beneath, **with 5 or 6 pairs of lateral nerves of which 3 or 4 basal pairs**.

**Inflorescence** up to 20 cm long, **tomentose, densely flowered**; bracts
linear, 2–5 mm, with long cilia, sometimes mixed with shorter hairs.
**Male flower**: pedicel 0.5–1 mm, glabrous; sepals obovate, ± 1 × 0.7 mm,
obtuse at apex, the inner much longer than the outer, glabrous; petals
similar to sepals but ± half as long, glabrous; **synandrium** 0.8 mm tall.
**Female flower**: pedicel 1–1.5 mm, pubescent; **single carpel** subglobose,
glabrous; stigmas 3, linear, ± 0.3 mm.

**Fruit ellipsoidal**, 2 × 1.5 cm, hispid, yellowish, with laterally excavated
faces when dry.

## Distribution

**Range**: Nigeria, Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, Democratic Republic
of the Congo, Central African Republic, Angola (Cabinda).
**Gabon**: all provinces except Haut-Ogooué, Ogooué-Ivindo and Ogooué-Lolo.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest and forest regrowth. In Gabon at 50–600 m altitude, elsewhere
up to 900 m.

## Uses

Leaf juice is applied to scarifications on the forehead to treat severe
migraine. Leaf macerate is drunk against snake bites, and a piece of stem
tied around the ankle is worn to prevent them. Stems are also used to make
ankle and wrist bracelets for babies, to help them walk. In CAR, crushed
leaves in salt water are given to babies for constipation. Roots are
considered edible.

## Notes

- *Perichasma* has only two species: *P. laetificata* (widespread in tropical
  Africa) and a Cameroonian endemic. The genus is characterised by the
  **single carpel** in the female flower (vs. 3 or more in most other
  Menispermaceae) — a feature it shares with *Cissampelos*.
