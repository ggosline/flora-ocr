# *Lecomtedoxa nogo* (A. Chev.) Aubrev.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Lecomtedoxa]]

## Description

A tree with glabrous branchlets and large obovate-elliptic to obovate-oblong
leaves, shortly acuminate, obtuse or rounded at the base, coriaceous, and
glabrous, **15-25 x 6-12 cm**. About **10** strongly arched secondary veins
anastomose near the margin; the petiole is thick and **2-3 cm** long.

Flowers are fasciculate on old branchlets, larger branches, and the trunk.
Pedicels reach **1.8 cm**. The **5 sepals** are imbricate and slightly
pubescent. Corolla lobes are **7.5-8 mm** long, each with **2 lateral
appendages** about **3.5 mm** long, shorter than the lobes. There are **5
extrorse stamens**, long ovate staminodes usually ending in a filiform tip,
and a pubescent **5-locular** ovary with long glabrous style.

The fruit is a dry laterally dehiscent capsule, oblong and ribbed, about
**8.5 cm** long and **2-3 cm** wide, with opposite curvatures at base and apex.
The single seed is flattened-fusiform, about **7 x 2 x 1.2 cm**, with the scar
occupying the full ventral face.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: restricted to the Fernan Vaz lagoon area.

## Habitat and ecology

Marshy ground along the Fernan Vaz lagoon, where the treatment says it may form
nearly pure stands. Trees may reach **1 m** in diameter; fruits mature from
**June to August**.

The account says the seeds were reportedly used to make an edible fat. The
vernacular name `nogo` is given as Nkomi.
