# *Tetracera rosiflora* Gilg

**Family**: [[Dilleniaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tetracera]] · **Section**: *Akara*

Bot. Jahrb. Syst. **33**: 199 (1902).

## Synonymy

- *Tetracera mayumbensis* Exell, J. Bot. **64**, Suppl. 1: 2 (1926).

## Description

Liana up to 30 m long; stem up to 6 cm in diameter, the bark fissured
longitudinally; branches glabrous; branchlets with sparse appressed hairs.

**Leaves**: petiole 3–7(–10) mm, **canaliculate but not winged**, ± pubescent;
blade obovate to obovate-oblong or rarely elliptic, (4–)5.5–17.5 × (2–)2.5–8
cm, rounded to obtuse or rarely barely acute at base, rounded to shortly
acuminate and often apiculate at apex, undulate to dentate in the upper
portion, coriaceous, green on both faces and weakly discolorous, glabrous or
with the midrib sparsely pubescent above, ± pubescent on the nerves
(sometimes also the nervules) beneath, sometimes ciliate at the margin.
Secondary nerves in 8–15 pairs, **strongly impressed (almost as prominent as
the midrib)**, rather ascending, deeply impressed (gaufré) or rarely flat
above, generally reaching the leaf margin; tertiary venation ± scalariform,
the nerves spaced 2–5 mm apart.

**Inflorescence**: with 5–15 flowers, in terminal or axillary cymes, 4–12 cm
long, **shorter than the subtending leaf or barely exceeding it**; axes
tomentellous to pubescent; bracts 2–8 × 1–3 mm, pubescent.

**Flower**: pedicel **6–20 mm, fairly robust**, ± straight, ± pubescent to
tomentose, the articulation often with hairs forming a ± dense ring beneath
the flower; bud **(6–)8–12 mm in diameter** — the largest in the genus
in Gabon; sepals 4, ± erect and remaining appressed against the carpels in
fruit, suborbicular to elliptic, **10–15 × 7–11 mm**, glabrous externally,
**densely tomentose-silky within** (except a glabrous zone toward the
margins), ciliate, becoming reddish in fruit. Petals **4, obtriangular-
obcordate, unguiculate, emarginate at apex**, 13–20 × 10–15 mm, white.
Filament 5–6 mm. Carpels (3 or) 4, glabrous.

**Fruit**: with 1–4 erect ellipsoid follicles, **12–20 × 7–10 mm**, with a
2–4 mm beak, lustrous, glabrous, reddish, each with 1–4 seeds.

**Seed**: subglobose to ellipsoid, slightly compressed, 4–5 mm long,
lustrous, brown-black; aril exceeding the seed.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Gabon, Republic of the Congo, Democratic Republic of
the Congo, Central African Republic, Angola.
**Gabon**: Haut-Ogooué, Ogooué-Ivindo, Ogooué-Lolo, Woleu-Ntem.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary and secondary forests, forest edges, savannas; in Gabon at 450–500 m
altitude, elsewhere at 0–600 m.

## Notes

In the [[Tetracera#Key]], *T. rosiflora* is separated from
[[Tetracera_poggei]] (the only other Gabonese species in section *Akara*) by
its larger sepals (10–15 vs 5–10 mm), strongly tomentose-silky inner sepal
surface, larger flower bud, large strongly impressed lateral leaf venation
with scalariform tertiary venation, and robust pubescent pedicel.
