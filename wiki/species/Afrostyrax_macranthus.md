# *Afrostyrax macranthus* Mildbr.

**Family**: [[Huaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Afrostyrax]]

*Bot. Jahrb. Syst.* **49**: 555-556 (1913).

## Description

Shrub **3-6(-10) m** tall, bark whitish-grey; branches glabrescent, branchlets
with ferruginous, tomentose, hispid hairs.

**Stipules**: persistent, narrowly elliptic-ovate, slightly falcate,
**3-5 x 1 mm**, densely hairy.

**Leaves**: petiole **(3-)4-6(-10) mm** long, densely hairy; blade subcoriaceous,
elliptic or obovate to ovate-elliptic, **(3.5-)7-14(-21) x (1.5-)3-6(-7.5) cm**,
glabrous or glabrescent on the veins beneath; base rounded to obtuse; apex
abruptly and often long-acuminate for **(0.5-)1-2(-3) cm**; lateral veins
**6-8(-9) pairs**.

**Flowers**: fasciculate in groups of **1-3**; bracts and bracteoles narrowly
triangular, **1-1.5 mm** long; pedicel **5-7 mm** long, densely hairy. Calyx
tearing irregularly into **(2-)3(-4)** reflexed lobes, elliptic to ovate-
elliptic, **4-5 x 2-4 mm**, densely hairy outside. Petals ovate-elliptic,
about **4.5 x 2.5 mm**, white or pale yellow; inner face with stellate hairs,
outer face with simple hairs at the base; claw absent or barely marked;
glabrescent to glabrous. Filaments united at the base, bent near the summit,
**1.5(-2.2) mm** long; anthers **1.5-2 mm** long, glabrous dorsally. Pistil
**2-3 mm** long; ovary about **1 mm** in diameter, with dense hairs on the
ovary and lower style.

**Fruit**: ovoid, **1-2.8 x 1-2 cm**, densely hairy.

**Seed**: ellipsoid, **20-22 x 16 mm**, testa veined and slightly shining.

## Distribution

**Range**: southern Cameroon and Gabon.  
**Gabon**: present, but the source does not specify provinces.

## Habitat and ecology

Lowland humid dense forest.

## Vernacular names

esun, eswinya, bole (Fang); findji (Kota); sindjakalo (Myene); mufira (Punu).

## Uses

The bark and seeds are used locally as garlic-flavoured condiments.

## Notes

Compared with [[Afrostyrax_kamerunensis]], this species has more lateral veins,
a revolute blade margin, denser indumentum on pedicel and calyx, longer
filaments, and glabrous anthers. It is also more narrowly distributed, being
known only from southern Cameroon and Gabon.
