# *Englerophytum kouloungense* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Englerophytum]]

## Description

This species is known only from **sterile material** in the treatment.
Young branchlets bear coarse appressed hairs. Leaves are stipulate; stipules
are linear-foliaceous and about **1.5 cm** long. The blade is lanceolate,
acuminate and mucronate at the apex, cuneate at the base, **16-35 x 4-7 cm**,
with about **30** secondary veins raised beneath and joined by a marginal vein.
Tertiary veins run parallel to the secondaries. The lower surface is grey- and
rust-tomentose. Petiole **1-1.5 cm**.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Koulounga, in the Mondah region.

## Habitat and ecology

Forest on a sandy hill in the Koulounga reserve. The source says the tree had a
diameter of about **30 cm**.

## Notes

The treatment compares this species to Congolese *E. vermoeseni* and separates
it mainly by the appressed pubescence on the midrib beneath rather than the
hirsute condition seen in the Congolese species.
