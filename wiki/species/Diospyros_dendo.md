# *Diospyros dendo* "Welw. ex Hiern"

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

Small tree reaching 15(-25) m in height and 20(-30) cm in basal diameter, though often narrower; trunk frequently twisted and slightly fluted; bark smooth, dark gray-black or greenish-gray, peeling in thin brittle flakes and exposing fresh gray-violet surfaces; bark section with a black outer band and bright yellow within; wood hard, white, but often black at the center. Branches slender, smooth, dark ash-gray; young twigs very dark gray to black when dry, covered with a fine short rather loose puberulence, often more or less appressed, golden tawny in color and eventually more or less caducous; young foliage tinged orange and reddish.

Leaves with petiole scarcely exceeding 5-6 mm, puberulent with appressed hairs; blade fairly variable in shape and size, most often oblong-lanceolate but sometimes lanceolate or elliptic, commonly 10-15 x 4-5 cm, sometimes smaller or up to 18 x 7 cm; base generally rounded or obtuse but slightly decurrent, sometimes acute, the margin turned under near the base, which almost always bears on each side of the midrib on the lower surface an elongated elliptic dark brown to blackish gland, often depressed and sometimes perceptible above; a few similar glands may exceptionally occur lower on the blade near the midrib; apex weakly acuminate in lanceolate forms, much more abruptly so in oblong-lanceolate or elliptic forms, the acumen reaching 2 cm long and 5 mm wide at the base, still 2 mm wide just below the very obtuse tip; on the lower surface of and near the acumen a few rounded glands are very frequently present; blade finely coriaceous, glabrous except near the base, at least on young leaves where the petiole puberulence extends beneath, usually reddish on dried material, the upper surface being glossy and darker; midrib impressed above, often puberulent toward the apex, that puberulence also frequently present beneath the acumen and nearby; (4-)5-6(-8) pairs of lateral veins, faint above and exceptionally impressed, very ascending and little curved in the lower veins, strongly curved in the middle and upper veins and discreetly loop-anastomosing near the margin; veinlets obscure, especially above, subparallel to one another.

Inflorescences axillary or slightly supra-axillary, sometimes below the leaves, in short contracted cymes with axes pubescent with tawny appressed hairs scarcely exceeding 2 mm, bearing a few caducous, ovate bracts and bracteoles about 2 mm long, concave, obtuse at the tip, and likewise pubescent; male flowers grouped 5-20 together, with pubescent pedicels articulated at the summit and about 2-3 mm long, more slender and longer than those of the female flowers, which are grouped 2-5 together and have pedicels broadened into a cushion at the apex.

Male flowers 6-8 mm high; calyx 3 mm long, campanulate, divided to about half its depth into (4-)5-6(-7) more or less equal narrowly ovate lobes with acute or obtuse apex, the calyx densely pubescent outside with short stiff upward-appressed golden-tawny hairs and more softly pubescent within; corolla cream-colored, glabrous, ellipsoid in bud, then with a campanulate tube surmounted by (4-)5(-6) lobes reaching two thirds of the corolla depth, the lobes oblong-obovate and reflexed at anthesis; 20-24(-26) stamens, half inserted near the base of the corolla tube and half near the throat, with filaments 1-3 mm long, either glabrous, apparently in Cameroon and northern Gabon, or pubescent-setulose, apparently in southern Gabon and Angola; anthers oblong, 3-5 mm long, with conical point at the apex, glabrous or pubescent-setulose like the filaments; pistillode small, glabrous, massive, and more or less ribbed.

Female flowers 8-10 mm high; calyx accrescent, similar to that of the male flowers but more deeply lobed, with 4-6 narrower, sharper segments with somewhat reduplicate lateral margins; corolla similar to that of the male flowers but with erect lobes; no staminodes; ovary 4-locular, glabrous, conical, 2 mm long, prolonged by 4 short united styles, each ending obliquely in a finely cut and folded foliar stigmatic appendage, widely spreading and recurved outward.

Fruit with pedicel up to about 5 mm long, ovoid, 1.5 x 1.2 cm, pointed at the apex with the truncated base of the styles, glabrous, glossy dark purple, containing 2-4 seeds 10-12 mm long; fruit surrounded at the base by the accrescent, rather widely open calyx, whose coriaceous lanceolate lobes, laterally cordate at the base, may reach 3 x 1.5-1.8 cm, with undulate margin, stretched residual internal pubescence, and external pubescence concentrated only near the base along the median part of the lobe, which then projects inward; venation of the lobes more or less flabellate, calyx dark reddish-brown.

**Syntypes:** Welwitsch 2537 (iso-P) and 2538, Angola.

This species ranges from Nigeria through Cameroon, Gabon, and the Mayombe to Angola (Golungo Alto). It appears to inhabit evergreen humid forest in the northern part of its range; in the southern part, especially in the Bayaka Mayombe and Angola, it may develop somewhat distinct characters, notably in the androecium, and may tolerate slightly different ecology. It also seems to occur farther inland, at Lastoursville and Belinga, and even in the Sangha valley near Ouesso in the Republic of the Congo.

An extreme form of this species should probably also be included here; it is found sporadically in Cameroon and also in the DRC. It is characterized mainly by shortened internodes, very small leaves, at most 6.5 x 3 cm, with about 3 pairs of lateral veins, and smaller flowers with a corolla only 3.5-4 mm high. Intermediate forms, such as Daramola FHI 29805, appear to connect these unusual specimens with *Diospyros dendo* Welw. ex Hiern. This reduced-leaf form may correspond to an anomaly in the plant's physiology, but new field observations are indispensable, especially since the relevant specimens seem to bear two rather inconspicuous leaf glands near the axils of the first pair of lateral veins rather than two distinct glands near the extreme base of the blade; in addition, the stamens have a 1 mm anther, rather reduced relative to the 1-2 mm filament, and that anther is ciliolate along the margin.
