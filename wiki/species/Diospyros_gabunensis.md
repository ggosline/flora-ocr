# *Diospyros gabunensis* Gurke

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

13.Diospyros gabunensis Gurke

Bot.Jahrb.26:72(18g8).-F.WHITE,FWTA ed.2,2:12,tab.202 B,6 (1963)；Nigerian Trees 2:334 (1964). — Diospyros castaneifolia A. CHEv., Journ. de Bot.ser.2,2:I16 (1909). -Diospyros mamiacensis GüRkE,Bot. Jahrb.43:2o5 (1909). - Diospyros megaphylla GürkE,Bot. Jahrb.43: 2o5 (19o9)，syn. nov. - Diospyros Gilgiana GüRkE,Bot. Jahrb.43 : 2o6,tab.3,K-L (1909). - Diospyros Lujae DE WiLD., Pl. Bequaert.3: 547 (1926)，syn.nov.,non Maba Lujae DE WILD. (1926) = Diospyros deltoidea F. WHITE, cf.Bull. Jard.Bot.Brux.16 :24o (1956).

Shrub or small tree not exceeding 20 m in height, with trunk 20-30 cm in diameter, vertical, cylindrical, slender, little branched, and bearing long branchlets. Bark black, smooth, sometimes fissured, hard and very brittle, black in section toward the outside and light brown within, the innermost portion yellow; sapwood pale yellow, heartwood sometimes streaked black. Older twigs somewhat grayish and finely striate. Young twigs and young foliage densely velvety, golden-brown to dark brown.

Leaves with brown-velvety then glabrescent petiole 5-10(-15) mm long, flattened or somewhat channelled above toward the summit; blade variable in size and shape, oblong, oblong-lanceolate, oblong-oblanceolate, or elliptic, sometimes 12 x 3 cm, more usually 20-30 x 6-10 cm, sometimes up to 48 x 20 cm; base acute or more generally obtuse, even rounded or cordate in *D. megaphylla*, the blade often extending onto the top of the petiole and tending to form two auricles, each with a gland on the lower surface surrounded by the velvety pubescence that covers the extreme base of the blade, at least on young leaves; in narrow-leaved forms the two glands are sometimes indistinct; margin sometimes slightly undulate; apex acute or more generally obtuse or rounded, sometimes with scattered glands beneath, abruptly narrowed into a triangular acumen 5-10 mm wide at the base and 15-25 mm long; midrib flat or slightly impressed above, as are the c. 8-10 pairs of lateral veins, more or less ascending and little curved, linked by more or less parallel tertiary veins and a network of veinlets remarkably prominent on both surfaces; blade subcoriaceous to coriaceous, glossy on both surfaces, often olive-gray in dried material, glabrous above, puberulent beneath at least on the veins in young leaves, then glabrous.

Flowers fragrant, in few-flowered axillary clusters among the leaves or below them, exceptionally on older branchlets or on the trunk; male flowers 1-10 together, subsessile or with pedicels 1-2 mm long; female flowers solitary or 2-5 together, sessile, and accompanied like the males by broadly ovate, densely pubescent bracts and bracteoles, up to 1 cm long in some female flowers. Calyx and corolla rather similar in the two sexes, the male flowers about 20 mm high, somewhat smaller than the female flowers, which are about 25 mm high; calyx accrescent, subcampanulate or tubular, 8-10 mm high in male flowers and up to 15 mm in female flowers, with 5-7 acute or obtuse triangular teeth 3-4 mm long, the outside covered with a striking finely velvety dark chocolate-brown to blackish pubescence and the inside with dense short appressed hairs, sparser and sometimes confined to the lobes in male flowers; corolla white, funnel-shaped, very thick, with a dense silky silvery appressed pubescence outside and glabrous within, the tube a little longer, about 10 mm, than the 4-6 oblong lobes, about 8 mm, which are ovate with obtuse apex.

Male flower with androecium of 20-30 stamens in 2-3 rows. The original diagnosis of *D. megaphylla* reports 45-50 stamens, but the Paris herbarium specimens assigned to that taxon appear to have only 20-30, rarely a few more. Stamens about 10 mm high overall, with filaments c. 3 mm long, all completely fused to the base of the corolla tube, the apparently sessile anthers all placed at the same level, linear, obtusely pointed at the apex, and very hairy, especially at the base; pistillode rudimentary, a thick discoid mass with about 10 peripheral ribs and sometimes topped by a tuft of erect black hairs.

Female flower with 5-10 pubescent linear staminodes as long as the corolla tube and resembling the stamens but more slender; ovary ovoid-conical, 5-6 mm long, covered with a thick densely velvety pubescence, divided into 8-10 locules and surmounted by 4-5 erect styles 3 mm long, united at the base, pubescent, bilobed at the tip with terminal and inner papillose surfaces.

Fruits sessile, globose, 2.5(-3) cm in diameter, densely covered with dark chocolate-brown to black velvety pubescence and tightly enclosed for nearly half their height by the accrescent calyx, which forms an equally velvety cup, truncate or with irregularly developed lobes; seeds 8-10, 15-20 mm long.

**Type:** Soyaux 36, Gabon.

This species occurs in Sierra Leone, Liberia, Ivory Coast, Ghana, the Calabar region of Nigeria, then Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, and Gabon; it is also known from the Brazzaville region in the Republic of the Congo and extends into the DRC and Zambia. Throughout its range it appears to be associated chiefly with very humid dense forest, and in Cameroon it has been recorded only from the Kumba and Kribi regions.
