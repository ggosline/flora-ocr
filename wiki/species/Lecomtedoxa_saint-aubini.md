# *Lecomtedoxa saint-aubini* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Lecomtedoxa]]

## Description

Leaves are obovate, caudate-acuminate, cuneate at the base, glabrous, and
clustered at the ends of branchlets. The blade is about **15 x 7 cm** with an
acumen about **1 cm** long, and about **15** secondary veins with parallel
tertiary veins, all very faint. Petiole about **1.5 cm**.

Flowers are pedicellate and fasciculate near the branchlet tips; the
description is based only on immature floral buds. Pedicels are about **10
mm**. The calyx is ovoid and tomentose, about **7 mm** long. The corolla has
**5** oblong petals **4.5-5 mm** long, each with **2 short appendages** only
**1-1.5 mm** long. There are **5 stamens**, **5** denticulate staminodes with
long hairs at the apex, and a hairy **5-locular** ovary.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon.  
**Gabon**: only the type collection `Service forestier 1941`, without locality
data in the treatment.

## Habitat and ecology

Not specified in the source.

## Source

Aubreville A. (1961) *Sapotacees*. In: Aubreville A. (ed.), *Flore du Gabon,
Volume 1*. Museum national d'Histoire naturelle, Paris.

## Notes

The treatment explicitly calls this species **very imperfectly known** and says
it is represented in Paris only by the holotype in bud. It is compared most
closely with [[Lecomtedoxa_klaineana]], but differs by more obovate leaves,
fainter venation, and much shorter corolla appendages.
