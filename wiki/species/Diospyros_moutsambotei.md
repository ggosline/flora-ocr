# *Diospyros moutsambotei* E.Mestre, Meeprom, H.N.Rakouth & Lowry, *sp. nov.*

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

A new species described in 2025 from the African material formerly included in
[[Diospyros_ferrea]]. It is the only segregate in that revision with a type
locality in **Gabon**.

## Type

**Holotype**: Gabon, Ogooué-Ivindo, Parc National de l'Ivindo, station de
recherche Ipassa, **477 m**, 14 March 2019, fruiting, *Bidault et al. 4494*
(MO); isotypes BR, BRLU, LBV, P, WAG.

## Diagnosis

Distinguished by symmetric, non-falcate leaves with an evident **pair of basal
glands**, usually an **acuminate** apex, broadly ellipsoid fruits
**12-15(-20) x 9-11 mm**, and a broad 3-lobed fruiting calyx with very shallow,
rounded-triangular lobes.

## Description

Treelet **2-8 m** tall. Leaves are ovate to elliptic, rarely obovate or
suborbicular, **1.5-10.5 x 1-4.5 cm**, with the paired glands most evident on
the lower surface near the blade base. Fruits are solitary or paired, broadly
ellipsoid when ripe, and usually one-seeded.

## Distribution

**Gabon, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Republic of the Congo, and the
DRC**. In Gabon the article cites occurrences in **Ogooué-Ivindo,
Ogooué-Lolo, and Woleu-Ntem**.

## Habitat and ecology

Riverine and **periodically inundated forest**, often associated with
**Gilbertiodendron dewevrei**. Reported from about **350-700 m** elevation.

## Conservation

Assessed in the protologue as **Vulnerable (VU B2ab(i,ii,iii,iv,v))**.

## Taxonomic affinities

Of the five species split from the old African *D. ferrea* concept, this is
the only one whose range overlaps with [[Diospyros_smeathmannii]]. The article
separates it by its symmetric non-falcate leaves, the evident paired basal
glands, and its broader fruits.
