This species, widely represented in Central Africa, shows variation in habit, blade shape, calyx appearance, pubescence of the stamens and ovary, fruit shape, and perhaps ecology. Field observations and the assembly of new material will no doubt clarify several still poorly known points and may eventually justify subdivision of the species.

Shrub a few meters tall or small tree not exceeding 20 m, but sometimes reaching 30-50 cm in diameter; very often several stems arise from the base, or the bole is irregular, fluted, and knotted, with ascending branches curving outward at the tips to form a densely bushy crown; rhytidome black with numerous superficial vertical fissures; bark section dirty orange to reddish brown inside beneath the black rhytidome band; cambium yellow; sapwood pinkish white; heartwood black, sometimes hollow in the center; branchlets glabrous and smooth, grayish or brownish when dry; the youngest twigs apparently sometimes finely puberulent.

Leaves with glabrous channelled petiole 5-10 mm long; blade broadly ovate-lanceolate or, more often, elliptic or lanceolate-elliptic and sometimes somewhat falcate, occasionally very oblong-elliptic, reaching (10-)21 x (3-)9(-11) cm, with cuneate acute or obtuse base and acuminate apex, the acumen up to 20 x 5 mm with the tip itself rounded; blade coriaceous, glabrous, with (4-)5-8(-9) pairs of lateral veins loop-anastomosing well away from the margin, deeply impressed above and prominent below like the midrib, though sometimes rather indistinct, the veinlets forming a loose network that is extremely weakly raised, or even invisible, beneath; dried material tawny-olive to reddish brown; fairly numerous glands scattered beneath the blade, each surrounded by fine radiating striae in dried material.

Male inflorescences of 1-10 subsessile flowers with imbricate pubescent bracts and bracteoles, arranged in contracted cymes in the leaf axils and lower down on older branchlets. Male flowers white and fragrant, with low rounded floral bud becoming slightly pointed at the apex; calyx cupular or almost dish-shaped, 2-3 mm high, glabrous or very sparsely clothed with appressed hairs, with truncate margin bearing 3 minute teeth, sometimes obscurely ciliate and sometimes torn so as to appear lobed; corolla tubular, campanulate at anthesis, thick, 8-10 mm high, with 3 subobtuse deltate lobes scarcely exceeding 2-3 mm and often bearing a few appressed hairs outside; (6-)9(-12) stamens inserted on the receptacle, with filament scarcely exceeding 0.5 mm and anther about 3 mm long, relatively lanceolate, with short obtuse tip, puberulent at least on the connective; no pistillode.

Female inflorescences similar to the male ones but bearing fewer female flowers; these similar, though somewhat larger, to the male flowers; calyx slightly accrescent; 6 staminodes, 3 inserted at the base of the corolla and 3 around the ovary, with pseudo-filament 1 mm long and lanceolate pseudo-anther 1.5 mm long, finely puberulent on the back; ovary broadly ovoid, 2-3 mm high, glabrous, pubescent toward the summit or entirely pubescent, with (5-)6 locules, directly surmounted by 3 foliar oblong stigmas 1.5-2 mm long, their margins densely crenulate and undulate.

Fruits yellow or orange, then apparently red, finally black, either subglobose and slightly depressed at the summit, with more or less hexagonal cross-section in dried material, or somewhat ovoid, about 2.5 cm long, glabrous, and with finely granular-verrucose surface when dry; at the base a slightly accrescent, irregularly lobed calyx about 1 cm in diameter; seeds (5-)6, about 2 x 1 cm.

**Type:** Mildbraed 3076, DRC.

This species is known from Nigeria, Cameroon, Gabon, both Congos, the Central African Republic, and Angola (Lunda). It often appears to favor stream margins, but it also seems commonly to occur in the understory of terra firme forest.
