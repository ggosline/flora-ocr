# *Diospyros pseudomespilus* Mildbr.

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

30.Diospyros pseudomespilus Mildbraed

Notizbl.Bot.Gart.Berl.9:1o5o (1926). -Diospyros undabunda HIERN ex GREvEs,Journ.Bot.67,suppl.Gamop.:So (1929).—F.WHITE,FWTA ed.2,2:12(1963);For.fl.North.Rhodesia : 328 (1962); Nigerian Trees 2:332 (1964).

This species, unknown from Gabon, appears poorly represented in Cameroon, and the information below draws on material from several parts of its range, especially Nigeria and the Central African Republic.

Shrub 2-12 m tall, or occasionally a tree up to 40 m, with straight bole, light crown, and dark gray to black smooth rhytidome with superficial longitudinal fissures; bark yellow-brown inside; young twigs covered with tawny pubescence, later glabrescent, as also the upper surface of the young leaves, while the lower side of the midrib remains pubescent for rather a long time.

Leaves with pubescent petiole up to 10-12 mm; blade oblong or oblanceolate-elliptic, averaging 18 x 6 cm and reaching 30 x 10 cm, with cuneate or obtuse, even slightly cordate base, but distinctly narrowed onto the top of the petiole, shortly acuminate at the summit with acumen scarcely exceeding 1.5 cm; 7-12 pairs of oblique, little-curved lateral veins joined near the apex, impressed above like the midrib and connected by rather widely spaced veinlets; all the venation, and often the blade itself beneath, bears short stiff scattered hairs standing more or less obliquely and sometimes appearing fascicled in groups of 2-3; sometimes one, rarely two, broad rounded glands occur on each side near the base of the lower surface, fairly far from the midrib and sometimes even on the margin.

Male flowers, on pedicels up to 3 mm, borne 3-5 together in axillary cymes or on older defoliated branchlets; female flowers solitary or 2-3 together, also shortly but stoutly pedicellate and borne in the same positions; pedicels densely pubescent. Male calyx 8 mm high, with narrowed basal portion 1 mm long, densely covered inside and out with tawny pubescence, with 4-5 deep narrow triangular lobes; female calyx accrescent and similar, but with very thick, broadly triangular lobes edged by an undulate margin. Corolla 12-15 mm, with slightly swollen angular tube reaching half the total height and 5 elliptic lobes 6-7 x 4 mm, finely pubescent outside, even on the exposed lobe margins, and also bearing a dense tomentellous band along the ribs of the tube and lobes, though the fine tube pubescence is restricted to the male flower. Androecium of 20-25 stamens inserted on the receptacle, filaments not exceeding 1 mm, hirsute at the summit, anthers about 4 mm, finely puberulent, slightly apiculate and also puberulent at the tip; pistillode rudimentary, 1-2 mm, bristling with tawny hairs. In female flowers there are 12-16 glabrous staminodes, 5 mm long, half filament and half flattened fusiform pseudo-anther, inserted at the base of the corolla tube; ovary ovoid-conical and somewhat ribbed, 3-4 mm high, covered with erect tawny bristles concealing at the summit a short column divided into 4-5 erect styles 2 mm long, each ending in a V-shaped stigmatic appendage with 2 triangular lips obliquely inclined outward, the ovary itself 8-10-locular.

Fruits subsessile, globose, somewhat depressed, reaching 4 cm in diameter, red at maturity, eventually glabrescent except around the persistent style base at the summit and toward the base; fruiting calyx 2.5-4.5 cm high, star-shaped and composed of 4-5 lanceolate lobes free almost to the base, pubescent especially along the outer midline and with strongly undulate margins; seeds 4-10, perhaps 20 mm or more, with very hard seed coat.

**Type:** Mildbraed 6134, Cameroon (iso-HBG).

Species known from Nigeria, Cameroon, both in evergreen and apparently also semi-deciduous humid dense forest, the Central African Republic, Cabinda, the DRC, Angola, and Zambia.
