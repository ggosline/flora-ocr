# *Lecomtedoxa heitzana* (A. Chev.) Aubrev.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Lecomtedoxa]]

## Description

A tree with large erect leaves at the ends of branchlets. The blades are
oblong-lanceolate, glabrous, up to **40-50 cm** long, narrowed at the base and
obtusely acuminate at the apex, with **12-15 pairs** of raised secondary veins
linked by transverse parallel nervules. Petiole **8-10 cm**, thick and
plano-convex.

Flowers are borne on older branchlets. Pedicels are about **1 cm** long.
Sepals are triangular, acute, and about **9 mm** long. The corolla is about
**1 cm** long with a short tube and lanceolate lobes; the lateral appendages
are very acute and about **5 mm** long. There are **5 stamens** with very
short filaments, **5** glabrous suborbicular staminodes ending in an acute
point, and a hairy **5-locular** ovary.

Fruits are very elongate-pyriform, **10-13 cm** long and **5-7 cm** thick,
tomentose, with **5** longitudinal ribs, and laterally dehiscent. The seed is
flat, brown, pointed at both ends, about **6 x 2.5 x 1.5 cm**, with a narrow
scar running the full length of the ventral face.

## Distribution

**Range**: apparently endemic to Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Pointe Denis and Mfoa on the Mbéi River.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary humid forest. The treatment says the species is rare and very localized
in Gabon. The bole is often irregular, fluted, and bossed at the base, with
surface roots marked by pale lenticel lines.

## Source

Aubreville A. (1961) *Sapotacees*. In: Aubreville A. (ed.), *Flore du Gabon,
Volume 1*. Museum national d'Histoire naturelle, Paris.

## Notes

The source gives the vernacular name `Adzacon` and emphasizes the unusually
localized range.
