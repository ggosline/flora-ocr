# *Zeyherella mayombense* (Greves) Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Zeyherella]]

## Description

A shrub with ferruginous-tomentose young branchlets. Young leaves are rusty
beneath and briefly covered above by long silky silver hairs. Leaves are
clustered in rosettes separated by long internodes, with small persistent
hook-like stipules **2-3 mm** long. Blades are lanceolate, acuminate,
**6-13 x 2-4 cm**, glabrous above and rusty-pubescent beneath, with the lower
margin remaining ferruginous. Petiole short, about **10 mm**.

The plant is cauliflorous, with dense fascicles of small flowers along the
trunk. Pedicels are **3-5 mm** long. Sepals **5**, pubescent outside. The
corolla is red, **3-4.5 mm** high, with short tube and **5** oblong-ovate
lobes. Stamens **5**, extorse, with filaments **1.5-2 mm** long; anthers are
apiculate, sometimes with the tip more or less three-toothed. Staminodes are
rudimentary and crenulate. The ovary is pubescent and **5-locular**.

Fruits are very small, one-seeded, flattened-ellipsoid, about **1.8 x 0.7 cm**,
rusty-pubescent. Seeds are about **1.5 x 0.8 x 0.6 cm** and marked by a narrow
scar extending over most of the ventral face.

## Distribution

**Range**: Mayombe and Gabon.  
**Gabon**: Libreville km 18 reserve, Liby, Lac Gomee, and Ikoy.

## Habitat and ecology

Near the Gabon estuary, especially at mangrove margins. The treatment says it
occurs near Libreville on the edge of the mangrove.

genus and highlights its estuarine habitat near mangroves.
