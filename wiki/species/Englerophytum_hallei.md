# *Englerophytum hallei* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Englerophytum]]

## Description

A **small tree** with leaves in rosettes at the branchlet tips. Stipules are
subulate, rigid, pubescent, and persistent. Leaves are elongate-obovate,
acuminate with a slightly emarginate acumen, very sharply cuneate at the base,
**12-18 cm** long, and grey or rusty-appressed-pubescent beneath. The many
parallel lateral veins are barely visible. Petiole about **1 cm**, appressed-
pubescent.

Flowers are cauliflorous in fascicles near the base of the trunk, up to about
**1.5 m** above ground. Pedicels are about **1 cm**. Floral buds are ovoid and
slightly reddish. The calyx has **5** thick lobes, a little connate at the
base. The corolla is **7-8 mm** long, with tube shorter than the lobes. The
extrose anthers are sessile, about **3 mm** long, and inserted on the rim of a
tube that projects about **2.5 mm** above the level of lobe fusion. The ovary
is pubescent and **5-locular**; the style is glabrous.

Fruit was unknown to the treatment.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Monts de Cristal.

## Habitat and ecology

Humid forest. The flowers in the type were recorded in **August**.
