# *Diospyros fragrans* Gurke

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

12. Diospyros fragrans Gurke

Bot.Jahrb.46：154(19II).—MILDBRAED，Notizbl.Bot.Gart.Berl.9：1050 (1926).-F.WHITE,FWTA ed.2,2:11,tab.202A,3 (1963);non Mabafragrans HIERN ex GREVES.

Specimens of this species appear in various herbaria under the following name: *Diospyros mucronata* (Pierre ms.).

PL.8,1-10,p. 73,et CARTE 28,p.169.

Small tree reaching up to 15 m in height, sometimes formed by several erect stems; trunk shallowly and irregularly fluted at the base, and in fresh sandy soil sometimes bearing small stilt roots there, with cauliflorous swellings; rhytidome dark brown, exfoliating in papery scales about 5 x 2.5 cm; bark section blackish outside and dark ochre within; wood creamy yellowish. Crown small, with long upper branches appearing almost whorled, obliquely ascending, and very openly branched; young branchlets slender, green blotched with black and then uniformly blackish when dry, covered with a fine appressed pubescence of yellowish hairs, leaves distichous.

Leaves shortly petiolate, 2-3 mm, with oblong-lanceolate blade 7-11.5 cm long including the acumen and 3-4 cm wide, obtuse or broadly rounded at the base, caudate-acuminate, the acumen 10-15 mm long, acute and often mucronate at the tip; blade papery, glossy bluish green and glabrous above, dull whitish bluish green beneath, with the midrib and 3-5 pairs of lateral veins finely pubescent, the surface itself only sparsely appressed-pubescent; lateral veins almost indistinct above, arched and fading toward the margin; no visible veinlets; in dried material the blade gray-green or sometimes brownish above, gray-whitish to slightly yellowish beneath, the entire lower surface dotted with fine crystalline punctations apparently of two sizes, the larger being clusters of the smaller; numerous small depressed black glands occur along and usually very close to the midrib, 1-2 mm away, extending into the acumen.

Male inflorescences in fascicled cymes of 6-15 flowers, borne on old wood, sometimes almost to ground level, furnished with lanceolate bracts and bracteoles 2-3 mm long, pubescent outside. Pedicels 3-5 mm long, articulated late near the apex, pubescent. Calyx greenish with reddish veins, 3-4 mm high, sparsely puberulent outside, with (4-)5 lobes shorter than or nearly as long as the tube, narrowly triangular to deltate, acute at the apex, bearing externally at least one large crateriform gland near the tip and sometimes others elsewhere, these glands, perhaps pathological in origin, occasionally also occurring on the corolla tube; corolla pearly white, reaching 35 mm in height, white, very fragrant, glabrous, with tube 10-16 mm long, constricted at the top, and 5 lanceolate lobes about 20-25 mm long; 18-20 glabrous stamens inserted at the base of the corolla tube in pairs belonging to two concentric cycles, with filaments 2-4 mm long and linear anthers about 6 mm long, long-pointed at the apex; pistillode a raised mass covered with erect stiff hairs, the whole not exceeding 2 mm in height.

Because specimens with female flowers are poorly represented in the Paris herbarium and the collected fruits appear to have disappeared, the following descriptions of those parts are derived largely from the original descriptions by Gurke and Mildbraed.

Female inflorescences in densely fascicled cymes of 5-8 flowers, likewise borne on old wood nearly to the ground, furnished with lanceolate bracts and bracteoles 2 mm long, pubescent outside. Pedicel 5-10 mm long, with short stiff hairs and a plate-like apex. Calyx campanulate, with 5 triangular lobes whose tips quickly curve outward, similar to that of the male flowers and with the same glands, but additionally pubescent inside; corolla white, 25 mm long, with the tube swollen at the base, 10-11 mm long and 9-10 mm wide, and lanceolate lobes 12-13 x 4-5 mm; about 10 linear pointed staminodes, 8 mm long; ovary broadly ovoid, 4-6 mm long, 10-locular, densely covered with stiff yellowish-brown hairs and surmounted by very short united styles, with 5 flattened-subulate stigmas curved inward, 2-4 mm long, glabrous inside and hispid outside.

Fruits ovoid-subglobose and somewhat depressed, about 3-4 cm in diameter, apiculate, sparsely covered with silky hairs and additionally with a short ferruginous pubescence that is more or less caducous and causes itching; fruit surrounded at the base by the accrescent calyx, up to 1.5 cm in diameter; 10 seeds about 30 x 13 x 8 mm, wedge-shaped in cross-section and strongly convex on the outer face.

**Syntypes:** Zenker 1740 (iso-P), Ledermann 682, and Huckstadt 137 from Cameroon, and Tessmann 369 (iso-HBG), probably from Equatorial Guinea rather than Gabon.

This species may occur in Nigeria (Benin), but it is mainly centered in Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, and the Republic of the Congo, in the wettest dense-forest zones.
