# *Diospyros mespiliformis* "Hochst. ex A. DC."

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

Specimens of this species appear in various herbaria under the following name: *Diospyros ibo* (Gurke ms.).

Tree reaching 30 m in height and 70 cm in diameter, with straight trunk, sometimes fluted at the base, and crown globose or more or less umbrella-shaped in old trees, the branches much divided and the foliage rather dense. Rhytidome dark brown or blackish, longitudinally wrinkled and breaking into square plates; bark section black outside and pink within; sapwood pale pinkish-brown; heartwood often nearly black or at least black-veined. Senegal ebony is actually supplied by *Dalbergia melanoxylon* of the Papilionaceae. Branches are covered with a thin gray bark hiding a bright reddish-brown layer; buds elongate and silky.

Leaves with short petiole up to 8-10 mm long; blade elongate-elliptic or elliptic-oblanceolate, up to 16 x 6 cm but more commonly about 8 x 3 cm, with rather broadly cuneate base, margin often slightly undulate, and acute or slightly acuminate apex, the tip itself rounded, sometimes truncate or even slightly emarginate; about 20 oblique lateral veins at about 45 degrees, scarcely visible, forming with the veinlets a dense network slightly raised on both surfaces; very often a few small rounded black glands occur on the lower surface on either side of but away from the midrib, around the middle of the blade; blade somewhat coriaceous, dull gray-green above, yellow-green beneath, with traces of appressed hairs beneath in old leaves, drying reddish.

Male inflorescences axillary, distinctly pedunculate, 5-8 mm, rather loose, bearing 3-9 or more flowers on pubescent pedicels 1-3 mm long, articulated at the summit and provided with 1-2 lanceolate bracteoles. Calyx campanulate, 3-4 mm high, densely, finely, and shortly silky on both surfaces, with 4-5 short triangular lobes; corolla about 7 mm high, densely silky outside, tubular-swollen with a 4 mm tube and 4-5 fleshy acute lobes 3 mm long, the corolla ovoid-conical in bud; 10-16 stamens, often in pairs, inserted on the receptacle, with short filaments not reaching 1 mm and anthers 3 mm long with a few hairs on the connective; pistillode rudimentary and pubescent, or absent.

Female inflorescences axillary, with solitary flowers, more rarely 2-3 together, on pubescent pedicels 5 mm long. Female flowers, rarely trimerous, are similar to the male flowers but much larger, with a slightly accrescent calyx 1 cm high whose very deep cordiform lobes have an undulate margin with 2 rounded wings and a fleshy silky thickening inside at the base in the center; 6-12 glabrous staminodes 4 mm long inserted at the base of the corolla tube; ovary ovoid-conical, silky, with 4(-6) locules, surmounted by short united styles with 2 erect bilobed stigmas 1 mm long.

Fruits subglobose, apiculate, 2.5-3 cm in diameter, yellow, somewhat verrucose, at first densely pubescent then almost glabrous, surrounded at the base by the slightly accrescent calyx 1-1.2 cm high, covered with tawny puberulence and shaped like a starry cup with 4-5 ovate-triangular lobes 5-6 mm high and undulate margins, or at least with a broad undulation in the lower third; seeds 4-6, about 15 mm long, in a sweet edible pulp.

**Syntypes:** Schimper 655 and 1243, Abyssinia (iso-P).

This species is widely distributed from Senegal to the Red Sea and Arabia; southward it reaches southwestern Africa and the Transvaal. Throughout that range it occurs only outside the zones of humid dense forest and is therefore absent from the Congo basin, though it does occur in some West African regions along the northern edge of the forest blocks. Preferring dry climatic zones, it grows both in humid riverine galleries and in dry forests and wooded savannas, on rocky inselbergs as well as along rivers; in Cameroon it occurs essentially only north of the Benue, though it is also present farther south, including the Adamawa plateau.

Note: *Diospyros elliotii* (Hiern ex Scott Elliot) F. White, equal to *Maba mannii* Hiern, not *Diospyros mannii* Hiern, is a species distributed from Senegal to Nigeria and characteristic of riverine forests that are marshy or periodically flooded in savanna regions; it appears also to have been found in the Central African Republic, so its presence in Cameroon is possible and indeed very likely.

It is distinguished as a small tree not exceeding 12 m in height, with glabrous lanceolate leaves up to 12 x 5 cm, only weakly acuminate and rounded at the apex; 5-6 pairs of lateral veins with veinlets scarcely visible; flowers in axillary clusters or on older branchlets, with calyx 3 mm long, finely pubescent, and bearing 3-4 deep rounded lobes; corolla 6 mm long with 3 lobes 2 mm long; globose orange glabrous fruits 2-2.5 cm in diameter, with scarcely accrescent fruiting calyx bearing 3-4 rounded lobes; seeds with ruminate endosperm.
