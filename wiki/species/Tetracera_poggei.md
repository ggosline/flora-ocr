# *Tetracera poggei* Gilg

**Family**: [[Dilleniaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tetracera]] · **Section**: *Akara*

Notizbl. Königl. Bot. Gart. Berlin **1**: 71 (1895).

## Synonymy

- *Tetracera fragrans* De Wild. & T.Durand, Ann. Mus. Congo Belge, Bot. **1**: 55 (1899).

## Description

Liana up to 6 m long; stem up to 7 cm in diameter; branches glabrous, the bark
grey or brown and fissured longitudinally; branchlets glabrous or with sparse
appressed hairs.

**Leaves**: subsessile or with petiole up to 7 mm, **canaliculate but not
winged**, glabrous or with a few sparse hairs; blade ovate-oblong to elliptic,
3–13 × 1.5–6 cm, attenuate to acute at base and ± decurrent on the petiole,
acute to acuminate at apex, entire or dentate especially in the upper portion,
papery to subcoriaceous, weakly discolorous, glabrous above, glabrous or
sparsely pubescent on the primary and secondary nerves beneath. Secondary
nerves in 6–10(11) pairs, weakly impressed (clearly less prominent than the
midrib) and weakly ascending, flat or slightly grooved above, joining in
arches or sometimes reaching the leaf margin; tertiary venation **reticulate**.

**Inflorescence**: with 3–10(17) flowers, in 1–3 terminal or axillary cymes,
6–9(–16) cm long; peduncle 1.5–2 cm, glabrous to ± pubescent; bracts linear
to narrowly elliptic-ovate, 2–6 × 0.5–2 mm, acute, sparsely ciliate.

**Flower**: pedicel **6–20(–30) mm, slender and ± flexuose**, glabrous or
very sparsely pubescent; bud 4–6 mm in diameter; sepals 4, ± erect and
remaining appressed against the carpels in fruit, suborbicular to elliptic,
**5–10 × 5–8 mm**, the inner slightly larger than the outer, glabrous to
sparsely pubescent towards the base externally and sometimes spotted with
small raised yellowish-white blotches, ciliate, with rather sparse appressed
silky hairs within. Petals **4, obcordate, unguiculate, emarginate at apex**,
9–15 × 4–10 mm, white. Filament 3–5 mm. Carpels (3)4, glabrous.

**Fruit**: with 1–4 erect or oblique follicles, ellipsoid to ovoid,
**10–15 × 7–11 mm**, with a 1–4 mm beak, red turning yellow at maturity,
lustrous, each with 2–4 seeds.

**Seed**: globose to ellipsoid, 2.5–4 mm long, brown-black; aril exceeding
the seed.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon, Republic of the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo,
Angola.
**Gabon**: rare; recorded from Haut-Ogooué and Ogooué-Maritime.

## Habitat and ecology

Fallows, regrowth, degraded forests, savannas; in Gabon at 240–600 m altitude,
elsewhere up to 1100 m.

## Uses

In the Democratic Republic of the Congo, the plant (part not specified) is
used in the treatment of skin diseases, and the roots as an aphrodisiac
(Boutique 1967).
