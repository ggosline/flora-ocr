# *Jateorhiza macrantha* (Hook.f.) Exell & Mendonça

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Jateorhiza]]

J. Bot. **73**, Suppl. Polypet.: 10 (1935).

## Description

**Twining herb with red-orange latex**, strongly **hispid**, purple to dark
brown when dry. **Leaves**: petiole **(8–)13–20(–26) cm**, twisted and/or
curved/geniculate at base; **blade entire to 3- to 5(6)-lobed**, ± circular
in outline, **23–36 cm in diameter**, herbaceous, with lobes narrowly
acuminate over (3–)5–10 mm; lateral nerves 5–7 pairs of which 2 or 3 basal
pairs.

**Male inflorescence** **pendent or not, up to 45 cm long**, with main axis
sparsely hispid and secondary axes glabrous or nearly so; peduncle 5–6(–12)
cm; bracts early caducous, pinnate, (3–)5–7 mm long, with very sparse hispid
hairs. **Female inflorescence erect**, 20–25(–44) cm long, with hirsute-hispid
axis; peduncle 3–7 cm. **Male flower** white, glabrous, (sub)sessile; sepals
ovate, (3.5–)4.5–5.5 × 2–2.5 mm; petals narrow, concave, geniculate, ± 2.5
× 0.5–1 mm; stamens 2–2.5 mm, filaments fused over three quarters, anther
extrorse. **Female flower** cream-white; pedicel 6–7(–11) mm; sepals as in
male; petals 3 × 1.5 mm; staminodes 1.5–2 mm; carpels 2.5–3 mm, with
**glandular hairs**.

**Fruit**: mericarp ± ellipsoidal, **20–25 × 20 mm** (hairs included),
fleshy, **yellow-orange-red**, with brown hispid hairs; mesocarp mucilaginous,
white; endocarp reniform, cream-yellow. **Seed** up to 15 mm long.

## Distribution

**Range**: from Nigeria westward (sic — eastward) to Democratic Republic of
the Congo and Angola (Cabinda) in the south.
**Gabon**: **all provinces except Haut-Ogooué**.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest, swamp forest and forest edge. In Gabon at 10–750 m, elsewhere
up to 800 m.

## Uses

In Cameroon, the bark is used against snake bite, and stem hairs are applied
to ulcers as a poultice. The fleshy roots are eaten in times of famine.

## Notes

- A **female collection from the Ngounié** (route Ikobey to Bakongue) shows
  an inflorescence much longer than in other female collections (44 cm vs.
  20–25 cm) and pedicels of (5–)7–11 mm (vs. 6–7 mm) — flagged but not
  formally separated.
- *Jateorhiza* is the source of **calumba root**, a medicinal product (from
  the Mozambican congener *J. palmata*); the present species is its closest
  relative.
