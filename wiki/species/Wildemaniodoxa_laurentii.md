# *Wildemaniodoxa laurentii* (De Wild.) Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Wildemaniodoxa]]

## Description

A small riparian tree with ferruginous-tomentose young branchlets. Leaves are
oblong, obtuse and shortly acuminate at the apex, obtuse or rounded at the
base, about **14 x 6 cm**, with many secondary and tertiary veins running
nearly at right angles from the midrib and joining a marginal vein close to the
edge. The lower surface is initially ferruginous-tomentose, later greyer.
Petiole **1-1.5 cm**.

Flowers are borne on older branchlets. Pedicels are rusty-tomentose,
**6-7 mm** long. Sepals **5**, ovate, rusty-tomentose outside, glabrous within,
**2-2.5 mm** long. The corolla has **10 oblong lobes**, **2.5-3 mm** long, with
tube **2-2.5 mm** long. There are **10 stamens** exceeding the lobes; filaments
are **1.5 mm** long and inserted at the level of lobe fusion. The anthers are
conspicuously apiculate. Staminodes are absent or very rudimentary. The ovary
is hirsute and **10-locular**.

Fruit was unknown to the treatment.

## Distribution

**Range**: Congo basin with an Angolan variety also mentioned in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Evorombil.

## Habitat and ecology

Riparian habitat. The source calls it a small river-margin tree.

## Notes

The treatment emphasizes that the flower construction is unique among African
Sapotaceae. It also notes an Angolan variety with narrower, more acuminate
leaves.
