# *Manilkara microphylla* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Manilkara]]

*Notulae Systematicae* **16**(3-4): 233 (1960).

## Description

Shrub. Buds glandular; branchlets glabrous and marked by very conspicuous
lenticels. Leaves small, glabrous, very coriaceous, lanceolate to
obovate-oblong or oblong, obtuse, attenuate or distinctly acuminate at the
apex, cuneate at the base; blade **5-7 cm** long and **15-28 mm** wide;
petiole about **12 mm**. The numerous parallel lateral veins run to the margin
and are deeply impressed in the thick blade, giving the leaf a very distinctive
textured appearance.

Small flowers in axillary fascicles. Pedicel **5-7 mm**, scurfy-tomentose.
Sepals **6**, about **5 mm**, pubescent outside, glabrous or finely hairy on
the margins inside. Corolla about **5-6 mm** with a very short tube,
about **1 mm**; each of the 6 lobes bears 2 appendages as long as the lobe.
Filaments **2 mm**, anthers **2 mm**. Staminodes **2.5 mm**, bidentate.
Stamens and staminodes are united at the base into a thick ring about **2 mm**
high, itself adnate to the base of the corolla for about **1 mm**.

**Ovary** pubescent, prolonged by a long style, with **10-12** locules.  
**Fruit** unknown.

## Distribution

**Range**: known from the Lastoursville region of Gabon and from the edge of a
pond at Brazzaville in the Republic of the Congo.  
**Gabon**: Poupa and Lindeme in the Lastoursville region.

## Habitat and ecology

Poorly known. The source records it from the interior of Gabon and contrasts it
with littoral small-leaved species such as [[Manilkara_lacera]] and
[[Manilkara_welwitschii]].

## Notes

The source calls this species remarkable at first sight because of the very
deeply impressed venation of the tiny thick leaves. It is still poorly known
and fruitless in the treatment.
