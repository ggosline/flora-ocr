# *Diospyros longiflora* "R. Let. & F. White"

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

19.Diospyros longiflora R. Let.& F. White

Adansonia 9 : 279 (1969).

PL.14,p. 103,et CARTE 29,p.170.

This species is currently known only from one Cameroonian specimen with male flowers and one sterile Gabonese specimen; field information is still lacking, as are female flowers and fruits.

Shrub reaching 7 m in height and 25 cm in diameter at the base; rhytidome dark brown to blackish. The youngest twigs bear scattered oblique rather long hairs, also found on the petioles, beneath the veins, and here and there on the margin of young leaves; later the organs become glabrous. Branching pseudosympodial, with a shoulder-like swelling at the base of the petioles.

Leaves with petiole 1 cm long; blade lanceolate to oblong-lanceolate, reaching 38 x 12 cm, with obtuse or rounded base, cartilaginous margin, and acute, only weakly acuminate apex; midrib impressed above; about 5 pairs of lateral veins, strongly ascending and broadly curved, linked by tertiary veins almost perpendicular to the midrib; veinlets forming a loose network, little visible to the naked eye; blade coriaceous, dark green above and glaucous green beneath when fresh, greenish-brown above and whitish green beneath in dried material, at least in young leaves; lower surface covered with a finely granular mineralized epidermal coating; a few fine depressed glands especially grouped in the axils of lateral veins and scattered here and there along the midrib.

Male inflorescences in pseudofascicled cymes, either axillary and few-flowered or on older branchlets and then bearing up to about 10 flowers; at the base of the cymes are small ovate-lanceolate bracts and bracteoles, pubescent at least along the margin; pedicels 3-5 mm long, pubescent and articulated at the summit; calyx cupular, 4 mm high, with scattered appressed hairs, the same inside and especially on the margin, which is truncate and bears 4-5 very broad low teeth, though the calyx is often irregularly torn; corolla yellowish, swollen at the base and very tapered above in bud, with a few appressed hairs, formed of 4-5 narrowly lanceolate lobes reaching almost to the very base of the corolla, 2.5 cm high, remarkably thick at the base, where the aestivation appears valvate externally although it is convolute internally and in the upper part of the floral bud; 12-15 stamens inserted on the receptacle, with subsessile linear anthers 5 mm long, strigillose, ending in a short acute point hidden by erect bristles; no pistillode.

Female inflorescences, female flowers, and fruits unknown.

**Type:** De Wilde W. 1735, Cameroon (holo-WAG).

This species is known only from Cameroon, in the Eseka region, and from Gabon, in the Cristal Mountains.

### Material studied in Cameroon

De Wilde W.1735 (type)，5o km S Badjob,au SW d'Escka,au bord du Nyong pres du grand pont (fl.δ, janv.).

### Material studied in Gabon

Normand s.n.,monts de Cristal, pres Mela (stér.).
