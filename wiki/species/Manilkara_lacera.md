# *Manilkara lacera* (Bak.) Dubard

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Manilkara]]

## Synonymy

- *Mimusops lacera* Baker
- *Mimusops angolensis* Engl.
- *Mimusops angolensis* H. Lecomte

## Description

Tree or shrub. Leaves broadly obovate, sometimes suborbicular, rounded and more
or less emarginate at the apex, sometimes shortly and obtusely acuminate,
cuneate at the base, **4-12 x 2-8 cm**, coriaceous, felted beneath with
appressed silvery, grey, or tawny hairs. Numerous fine lateral veins are
generally inconspicuous. Petiole to **3 cm** long.

Flowers axillary, **2-5** together. Pedicels **8-15 mm** long, more or less
covered with tawny scurfy hairs. Sepals ovate-triangular, about **5 mm** long,
tomentose outside. Staminodes laciniate. Ovary tomentose, with **9-13**
uniovulate locules.

Fruits obovoid, yellow or red, containing several seeds. Seeds elliptic, flat,
brown, with a basiventral scar.

## Distribution

**Range**: widely distributed from Sierra Leone to Gabon and Angola.  
**Gabon**: especially abundant on Atlantic beaches and littoral sands; the
source also notes inland riparian and forest-edge forms connected by a broad
continuum.

## Habitat and ecology

The typical form is a **front-of-beach** species, often forming narrow almost
pure bands of small trees on sand just above high tide. The source also records
intergrading inland forms along lagoons, rivers, and gallery forests. It is
very sensitive to fire and regenerates by suckering when damaged by wind or sea.

## Vernacular names

mbimou (Fang), mbimo (Owimo), bandinga linga (Batanga).

## Source

Aubreville A. (1961) *Sapotacees*. In: Aubreville A. (ed.), *Flore du Gabon,
Volume 1*. Museum national d'Histoire naturelle, Paris.

## Notes

This is one of the treatment's most explicitly **ecologically variable**
species. The author treats the beach form as unmistakable, but emphasises a
continuous gradient of inland forms that makes strict delimitation difficult.
