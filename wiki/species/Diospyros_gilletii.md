# *Diospyros gilletii* De Wild.

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

14.Diospyros Gilletii De Wildeman

Bull. Jard. Bot.Brux.5 : 63 (1915),et 388 (1919).

- Diospyros Giletii var. Sapinii DE WILD., Pl. Bequaert. 3: 54o (1926). -Diospyros potamophila MiLDBr.,Notizbl.Bot.Gart.Berl.9 :Io54 (1926), syn. nov.

Shrub only 6-8 m tall, but with trunk up to 30 cm in diameter, supporting a broad globose crown, very leafy, with curved branches bearing many branchlets and more or less pendulous leaves, the young leaves red; rhytidome blackish, fissured, and exfoliating longitudinally; bark thin, blackish-brown in section toward the outside and egg-yellow within; wood white. Branchlets somewhat angular in section, blackish and glabrous, the juvenile twigs covered with a dense puberulence like that of the inflorescences, made up of oblique, dark brown-black, stiff, short, pointed, caducous hairs. In several specimens the bark of the branchlets bears a dense covering, probably mycelial and perhaps specific, formed of erect capillary hairs of irregular size, branched at the tip.

Leaves with petiole 10-12 mm long, distinctly channelled above in the upper part; blade elliptic-lanceolate, often somewhat falcate, more rarely broadly ovate or elliptic, up to 20 x 9 cm, broadly cuneate and only rarely obtuse at the base, weakly acuminate at the apex with an obtuse-tipped acumen; 6-10(-12) pairs of lateral veins, slightly arched, more prominent beneath than above, fading toward the margin; tertiary veins more or less parallel and finely visible beneath; blade papery to subcoriaceous, brownish when dry, glabrous on both surfaces, though young leaves have scattered hairs near the base beneath, similar to those of the juvenile twigs and inflorescences. The margin at the extreme base of the blade is turned under and encloses two elliptic or fusiform glands elongated lengthwise; a few tiny circular glands are also almost always present on the lower surface beneath the acumen and lower down.

Male inflorescences axillary, sometimes also on older branchlets, about 2 cm in diameter, and able to bear up to about fifty flowers, rather contracted and short-branched, though the main branches may approach 1 cm in length; these branches are covered with pubescence similar to that of the calyces. Male flowers fragrant, with pedicel scarcely exceeding 1 mm, articulated at the summit and likewise pubescent; calyx cup-shaped, distinctly narrowed at the base, 3 mm high, externally covered with a dense dark brown-black pubescence of oblique, stiff, short, pointed hairs that detach easily, the interior glabrous; calyx divided for half or more of its depth into (4-)5(-6) triangular lobes; corolla green in the blunt ovoid bud, then yellow outside and white within, with a very short glabrous tube 1 mm long and 5 lobes 5-6 x 3-4 mm, glabrous on both surfaces; 15-17 somewhat unequal stamens, glabrous, attached to the corolla tube, with filaments not exceeding 2 mm and anthers 2 mm long; pistillode rudimentary, glabrous, at the center of the flower.

Female inflorescences axillary or below the leaves, similar to the male inflorescences but bearing at most 5-8 flowers; flowers similar to the male flowers, but with calyx less narrowed at the base, accrescent, and also silky-pubescent within; corolla with 4-5 lobes not exceeding 5 x 3 mm; no staminodes, or only a rudimentary staminode between the corolla lobes at their base; ovary ovoid, 3 mm high, almost directly surmounted by 4 spreading sinuous stigmas and containing 4 locules.

Fruits with pedicel about 4 mm long, disarticulating below the calyx; accrescent calyx about 12 mm long, with subacute lobes 6 mm long that become glabrous on both surfaces except near the base and show fairly visible flabellate venation; fruit itself exceeding the calyx lobes, globose, slightly depressed at the top, with an apicule formed by the more or less persistent style base, 10-15 mm in diameter, glabrous, smooth, green then yellow then dark red and glossy; pericarp very thin, 0.5 mm, enclosing a single locule with at most 4 seeds, 8 x 5 x 4 mm, embedded in sticky pulp.

**Syntypes:** J. Gillet 2879, 2890, and L. Gentil s.n., DRC (BR).

This species occurs in Cameroon, the Central African Republic, both Congos, and Gabon. It is almost everywhere reported as a riverbank shrub, occurring in the upper valleys of the Sanaga, Nyong, and Dja in Cameroon and of the upper Ivindo in Gabon, and overall it is concentrated east of the longitude of Yaounde. It extends elsewhere into the Sangha and Congo basins, from the Kemo valley north of Bangui to the Djuma valley in Kasai, and is present as far upstream as Kisangani along the Congo.
