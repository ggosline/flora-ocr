# *Synclisia scabrida* Miers

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Synclisia]]

Ann. Mag. Nat. Hist., ser. 3, **20**: 171 (1867).

## Description

Liana with slender branches; **petioles and leaf nerves (mainly beneath)
hispidulous to sometimes velvety-ferruginous, with hairs ≥ 1 mm**.
**Leaves**: petiole straight, **1–6 cm**; blade ovate, **(4–)7–12(–15) ×
(3–)5–10 cm**, **cordate at base** (sometimes weakly), acute-acuminate over
0.5–1 cm and mucronate at apex, **with 4 to 7 pairs of very distinct
lateral nerves on both faces, of which (1)2 or 3 basal pairs**, hairy above
at least when young.

**Male inflorescence** with often solitary, sometimes paired flowers;
female with solitary flowers. **Male flower**: pedicel **(0.5–)1–1.5 cm**;
outer sepals 1–2 mm long, densely villous-pubescent; **inner sepals 7–8 mm
long**; petals 0.6 × 0.4 mm; stamens 6, rarely 9. **Female flower**:
staminodes spatulate; **carpels 15 to 20**, laterally compressed; ovary
subglobose, ± 1.5 mm in diameter, the lateral faces covered with stiff,
long, erect hairs; style lateral, filiform, 3 mm, glabrous.

**Fruit**: mericarps in a dense cluster, **1.2–1.7 × 0.8–0.9 cm**, orange.
Seed 1–1.5 cm long.

## Distribution

**Range**: southern Nigeria, Cameroon, Gabon, Republic of the Congo,
Democratic Republic of the Congo, Central African Republic, Angola.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Ogooué-Maritime.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary or degraded forest. In Gabon up to 100 m altitude, elsewhere up
to 500 m.

## Uses

In Nigeria and Cameroon a leaf decoction is used to treat **gastric
ulcers**. In Nigeria the root is used against **malaria**, to prevent
miscarriage and to calm mentally ill patients. In Gabon the bitter root,
sometimes mixed with the stem bark of *Garcinia klainei* Pierre ex Engl.
(= *G. epunctata* Stapf), is added to palm wine drunk to treat venereal
diseases and as an aphrodisiac; it also treats prostatic disorders, asthma
and hernia. Leaves serve as **high-protein fodder for ruminants**.

## Vernacular names

goat weed (English).

## Notes

- The wider-ranging of the two Gabonese [[Synclisia]] species; the other,
  [[Synclisia_oligogyna]], is endemic and known from a single collection.
- The genus description of male flowers, drupes and seeds is based **only**
  on this species, since they are unknown in *S. oligogyna*.
