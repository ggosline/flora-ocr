# *Diospyros korupensis* Gosline, *sp. nov.*

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

A post-*Flore du Gabon* Cameroonian species described from Korup National Park
and nearby Mt Cameroon localities. The protologue places it closest to
[[Diospyros_longiflora]], but separates it by its larger leaves, rounded petal
apices, and short included ciliate anthers.

## Type

**Holotype**: Cameroon, Southwest Region, Mundemba, Korup Project
Headquarters, 200 m altitude, male flowers, 10 December 1994, *Thomas D. W.
10285* (YA); isotypes K, MO, SCA, WAG, P, BR, G.

## Description

Tree to about **25 m** tall and **40 cm** diameter, with dark reddish-brown to
black twigs and mustard-yellow slash. Leaves are slightly ovate to slightly
obovate, about **25-45 x 11-15 cm**, with strongly ascending secondary veins
and a conspicuously glaucous, mineralized lower surface.

Male flowers occur in fascicles of about **30-50** on old branches, with a
cupular calyx and a long white corolla whose lobes are much longer than the
tube. Female flowers are borne in fascicles of about **5-12**; the ovary is
densely bristly and **10-locular**. Fruits are ovoid, about **4 x 5 cm**, and
contain **7-10** orange-segment-shaped seeds.

## Distribution

**Known only from Cameroon**: southern **Korup National Park** and adjacent
localities on the southwest slopes of **Mt Cameroon**.

## Habitat and ecology

Lowland evergreen rainforest with rainfall generally above **3000 mm/year**.
The article reports the species as locally common in the southern Korup plots
and not obviously tied to a narrow microhabitat within the park.

## Conservation

Assessed in the protologue as **Vulnerable (VU B2ab(iv))**, based on an
estimated area of occupancy of about **600 km²** and the fact that the Mt
Cameroon localities lie outside protected areas and may be threatened by land
conversion.

## Taxonomic affinities

The article treats [[Diospyros_longiflora]] as the closest known ally. Both
share ascending venation, a glaucous abaxial leaf surface, cupular male
calyces, and long deeply lobed corollas. The new species differs in its larger
leaves, rounded rather than pointed petal apices, short non-exserted ciliate
anthers, and occurrence in well-drained forest rather than the riverine and
swamp forests emphasized for *D. longiflora*.

The protologue also notes broader similarity to [[Diospyros_sanza_minika]] and
[[Diospyros_suaveolens]], suggesting that these four species may form a
natural alliance despite being placed in different infrageneric sections by
White.
