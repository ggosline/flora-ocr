# *Tinospora penninervifolia* (Troupin) Troupin

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tinospora]]

Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **29**: 223 (1959).

## Synonymy

- *Hyalosepalum penninervifolium* Troupin, Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles
  **19**: 432 (1949).

## Description

**Slender liana with white latex**, up to **5 m** long, **glabrous**;
older branches lenticellate. **Leaves**: petiole **1.5–6 cm**, curved
and/or twisted at base, generally thickened at both ends; blade
ovate-elliptic, **(4–)7–12(–16) × (1.5–)4–8 cm**, rounded to shortly
cuneate at base, acuminate over 0.5–1.5 cm at apex, **with (4)5 to 7(9)
pairs of lateral nerves**, prominent on both faces, midrib flat to
slightly impressed above, prominent beneath.

**Inflorescence**: **male up to 20 cm long**, with peduncle 0.5–1 cm,
bracts narrowly ovate-triangular, 1–2 mm long; **female up to 6 cm long**,
with triangular bracts ± 0.6 mm long. **Male flower**: pedicel 2–3.5 mm;
outer sepals ovate, 0.5–1 × 0.5 mm; **inner sepals free**, erect to
spreading, obovate, 2.5–3 × 1–1.5 mm, thin; petals 1.5–2 × 0.6–1 mm,
the outer slightly larger than the inner; stamens 2 mm. **Female flower**:
buds with pedicel ˂ 1 mm; open flowers not seen.

**Fruit unknown**.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon, Republic of the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Haut-Ogooué, Ngounié, Ogooué-Lolo.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary and secondary forest, forest islet in savanna. In Gabon at 5–780 m
altitude, elsewhere up to 400 m.

## Notes

- *Tinospora* is a Palaeotropical genus of about 33 species; only this one
  is present in Gabon.
- Originally described by Troupin in his now-defunct genus *Hyalosepalum*,
  later transferred to *Tinospora* by the same author.
