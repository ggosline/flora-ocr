# *Omphalocarpum elatum* Miers

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Omphalocarpum]]

## Description

A small to medium tree, reaching about **25 m** high. Young branchlets are
glabrous. Leaves are obovate-oblong to lanceolate, rounded or obtusely
acuminate at the apex, attenuate and cuneate at the base, **12-25 x 4-8 cm**,
glabrous, with about **10 pairs** of secondary veins prominent beneath. The
upper surface is matte and the fine nervules are not very apparent. Petiole
short.

Flowers are pedicellate, with pedicels about **1 cm** long. Sepals are thick,
yellow-green, and glabrous or nearly so. The corolla is white and about
**1.8 cm** long, with lobes **11 mm** long and tube **6 mm**. Hermaphrodite
flowers have **5-6 stamens per bundle**; in female flowers these are replaced
by subulate staminodes. The ovary is glabrous and has about **30 locules**
arranged around a hollow central part.

Fruits are large, flattened-subglobose, grey-brown, about **15 cm** in
diameter and **8 cm** high, sometimes weighing more than **2 kg** fresh. They
contain many black flattened seeds about **4 x 2.5 cm** with a linear ventral
scar.

## Distribution

**Range**: one of the broad-ranging species, extending from Sierra Leone to
the Congo according to the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Bounzocou and Koulounga; the cited material also mentions Bata in
Spanish Guinea.

## Habitat and ecology

Humid forest. The treatment does not narrow the habitat further for the
Gabonese material.

## Source

Aubreville A. (1961) *Sapotacees*. In: Aubreville A. (ed.), *Flore du Gabon,
Volume 1*. Museum national d'Histoire naturelle, Paris.

## Notes

The species is keyed in the treatment by its **pedicellate flowers**, shorter
corolla, very cuneate leaf base, and the **matte** upper leaf surface with
weakly visible fine nervules.
