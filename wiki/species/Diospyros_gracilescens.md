# *Diospyros gracilescens* Gurke

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

15. Diospyros gracilescens Gurke

Bot. Jahrb.46:155 (1911).

— Diospyros nigerica F. WHIrE, Bull Jard. Bot. Brux. 33 :349,tab.19, A-I;FWTA ed.2, 2 : 12 (1963); Nigerian Trees 2 : 341 (1964),syn. nov. - Diospyros Heudelotii auct. non HIERN : FWTA ed. 1, 2 : 6 (1931)，p.p.

PL.4,I1-19,p. 51,et CARTE 3o,p. 171.

Small tree reaching 20-30 m in height and 40-50 cm in diameter, but sometimes much taller and up to 1 m thick, the bole with slight buttresses or fluted at the base, especially in old trees, with a broad dark crown and somewhat pendulous branches; young stems erect with more or less whorled branchlets; young leaves reddish. Rhytidome black or dark brown, rough, with thick brittle scales that, once shed, reveal a dark chocolate-brown surface beneath; bark section black to dark brown outside and pale pink to dull red inside, with a yellow margin at the cambium; sapwood cream-colored and heartwood probably black, perhaps yielding a commercial ebony; young slender olive-brown branchlets and petioles finely puberulent, later glabrous.

Leaves with petiole 5 mm long, not 2.5-3.5 cm as erroneously given in the diagnosis; blade lanceolate-ovate or lanceolate-elliptic, up to 11 x 5 cm, with cuneate or rounded base and acuminate apex; midrib impressed above; 5-8 pairs of lateral veins, sometimes forked, little distinct, the veinlets difficult to see, the lower surface bearing fine appressed black strigillose hairs that are shed with age; blade bluish green beneath when fresh, drying greenish brown above and yellowish gray below; very small depressed black glands occur beneath the blade on either side of and quite close to the midrib, often about 2-4 between each successive pair of lateral veins.

Male flowers with puberulent pedicel not exceeding 2 mm, in fascicles of 1-7 flowers axillary or on old branchlets; calyx at most 2.5 mm high, finely pubescent outside, glabrous within, with 3 rounded or broadly triangular lobes reaching almost half the depth of the calyx; corolla yellow, bottle-shaped, 7-8 mm high, with 3 very short lobes, very thick and coriaceous, densely pubescent; 6-9 stamens inserted on the receptacle, with filament not exceeding 1 mm, hirsute at the summit, and narrowly lanceolate anther 3-4 mm long, long-apiculate, 1 mm or more, densely strigose; pistillode rudimentary, 2.5 x 1 mm, glabrous, or absent.

Female flowers similar to the male flowers but somewhat larger, with calyx 4 mm long, slightly accrescent, strigose inside, and 3(-4) lobes; 3-6 staminodes about 3.5 mm long inserted at the base of the corolla tube, with densely setulose pseudo-anther; ovary ovoid, 4 x 3 mm, covered with fine strigose hairs, 6-locular, and surmounted by 3 styles united into a stout column 0.5 mm long ending in 3 fleshy stigmas.

Fruits subsessile, globose, about 2.6 cm high and 3 cm wide, almost glabrous except at the depressed summit, with a dish-shaped calyx 5 mm across; 6 seeds, 18 x 10 x 5 mm.

**Syntypes:** Busgen 398 and 556, Cameroon.

This species is known only from Nigeria, Cameroon, and Gabon, in evergreen humid dense forest, or in primary-type semi-deciduous forest, as in the Nanga Eboko region of Cameroon.
