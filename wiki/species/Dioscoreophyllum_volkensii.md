# *Dioscoreophyllum volkensii* Engl.

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Dioscoreophyllum]]

Pflanzenw. Ost-Afrikas, **C**: 182 (1895).

## Synonymy

- *Rhopalandria cumminsii* Stapf, Bull. Misc. Inform. Kew **1898**: 71 (1898).
- *D. cumminsii* (Stapf) Diels in Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 181 (1910).
- *D. jollyanum* Pierre ex Diels, in Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 181 (1910),
  *nom. inval.*
- *D. klaineanum* Pierre ex Diels, in Engl., Pflanzenr. **IV.94**: 179 (1910).

## Description

Slender twining herb; stem **subhispid with erect hairs**. **Leaves**:
petiole **(3–)6–22 cm**, twisted and/or geniculate and/or curved at base,
with hispiduleous hairs, sometimes simultaneously pubescent with ± appressed
hairs; blade ovate or **3-lobed**, ± circular in outline, **(5.5–)8–16 ×
(4.5–)9–13 cm**, blade and/or lobes acuminate over 0.5–1.5 cm; lateral
nerves in 6–8 pairs of which 2 or 3 (or 4) basal pairs, sparsely pubescent
on both faces with subappressed hairs.

**Inflorescence** with axis and especially the peduncle hispiduleous. **Male
inflorescence 17–20 cm long**, with peduncle **4–11 cm**; **female** up to 15
cm long, with peduncle 7–9 cm; bracts 1.5–2 mm long, with hispiduleous apical
hairs. **Male flower**: pedicel 3–5 mm; sepals **reflexed** at anthesis,
ovate-elliptic, 2.5–3 × 1.5–2 mm; **stamens** in a **± hemispherical or
disciform, (sub)sessile synandrium**.
**Female flower**: pedicel and sepals as in male; carpels 1.5–2 mm long.

**Fruit**: mericarp ovoid-ellipsoidal, apiculate when dry, 8–10(–12) × 6–8
mm, glabrous, **orange**, smooth; mesocarp 2–3 mm thick; endocarp subspinose.

## Distribution

**Range**: throughout tropical Africa, from Guinea-Bissau east to Kenya and
south to Angola and Zimbabwe.
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Moyen-Ogooué, Ngounié, Nyanga, Ogooué-Lolo.

## Habitat and ecology

Flooded forest, gallery forest, primary and secondary forest. In Gabon at
25–270 m altitude, elsewhere up to 1140 m.

## Uses

The **rhizome is eaten cooked like a potato**. The fruits are also edible.

## Notes

- Famous as the **"serendipity berry"**: the fruit of African
  *Dioscoreophyllum* species (notably *D. cumminsii*, here treated as a
  synonym) contains the protein **monellin**, one of the sweetest substances
  known. The Gabonese name "Guinea potato" refers to the edible rhizome.
- Distinguished from [[Dioscoreophyllum_gossweileri|*D. gossweileri*]] by the
  much longer hispid male inflorescence (17–20 vs 3–6 cm), the **6 stamens
  in a hemispherical synandrium** (vs 3 in a cylindrical one) and the erect
  (vs ± appressed) stem indument.
