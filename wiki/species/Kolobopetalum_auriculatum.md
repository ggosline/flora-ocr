# *Kolobopetalum auriculatum* Engl.

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Kolobopetalum]]

Bot. Jahrb. Syst. **26**: 410, t. 13, f. 2 (1899).

## Description

Liana up to **12 m** tall; **branches woody, lenticellate, with bark
exfoliating in scales or thin plates**. **Leaves**: petiole (2–)2.5–5(–12)
cm; blade elliptic, **(5–)7–13(–17) cm long**, **deeply and/or broadly
cordate-auriculate**, **obscurely peltate over 1–4 mm at base**, with basal
lobes angular or acute, divergent or overlapping, acuminate over 0.5–1(–1.5)
cm at apex, with entire margin or with a few spaced often inconspicuous
teeth, with (3)4 or 5 pairs of lateral nerves.

**Male inflorescence** axillary, sometimes terminal or on leafless branches,
**up to 65 × 30 cm**, in 2- or 3-flowered fascicles, sometimes solitary;
peduncle up to 3 cm. **Female inflorescence** terminal on leafy branches,
once-branched, up to 50 × 22 cm. **Male flower** yellowish to pinkish;
pedicel 1.5–4 mm; outer sepals ovate, 0.3 mm long, inner elliptic, 1–1.25 ×
0.5–1 mm; petals concave, elliptic, 0.8–1 × 0.2–0.5 mm; **stamens 3–6**, as
long or a little shorter than the petals, with filaments fused at base.
**Female flower**: pedicel 2–3 mm; sepals as above; outer petals
longitudinally rolled, elliptic, 2.5 × 1 mm, ± truncate at apex; inner
elliptic to linear, 1–1.5 mm, truncate to bilobed at apex; staminodes 6,
oppositipetalous, slender, 1–1.5 mm; carpels slightly curved, 2 mm long;
stigmas pointed.

**Fruit**: mericarp ± 1 × 0.8 cm.

## Distribution

**Range**: Ghana, Benin, Nigeria, Cameroon, Equatorial Guinea (Rio Muni),
Gabon, Republic of the Congo, Central African Republic, Angola (Cabinda).
**Gabon**: Estuaire, Ogooué-Lolo, Woleu-Ntem.

## Habitat and ecology

Secondary forest. In Gabon at 10–600 m altitude, elsewhere up to 1000 m.

## Uses

An infusion of leaves and seeds is taken against insomnia, and an infusion
of leaves and twigs against mouth infections.

## Notes

- The species is variable in stamen number — most commonly 3, sometimes 6,
  or any number in between.
- Distinguished from the other Gabonese congeners by the **deeply
  cordate-auriculate, obscurely peltate** leaf base. See [[Kolobopetalum]]
  for the key.
