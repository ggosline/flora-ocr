# *Tieghemella africana* Pierre

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tieghemella]]

## Description

A **very large tree** with glabrous branchlets and leaves clustered at the
ends of shoots, lacking stipules. The blades are obovate, rounded or obtusely
acuminate at the apex, cuneate and shortly decurrent at the base, **8-16 x
4-7 cm**, glabrous, with **16-18 pairs** of rather faint secondary veins.
Petioles are slender, **1.5-3.5 cm** long.

Flowers are white, pedicellate, and borne in small fascicles of **2-3** at the
tips of branchlets. There are **8 sepals** in two whorls. The corolla is about
**5.5 mm** long with a short tube and **8 lobes**; each lobe bears **2 dorsal
appendages**. The **8 stamens** alternate with staminodes that are longer than
the stamens. The ovary is tomentose and has **8 uniovulate locules**.

Fruits are large, ovoid, shiny brown-yellow, about **8 x 7 cm**, sometimes
larger, on thick peduncles. They contain **1-3 seeds** in yellow pulp. Seeds
are elongated-ovoid, **5-7 x 2.5-3.5 cm**, with a ventral scar occupying
almost the whole ventral face; the seed coat is **very hard and thick**.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon and a little into Cameroon according to the treatment.  
**Gabon**: described as one of the great emergents of primary forest; cited
from Libreville, Fernan Vaz, Mayombe, Tchibanga, Sindara, and Koulamoutou.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary humid forest. The bole may reach **20-30 m** under the branches and
**1.5 m or more** in diameter. The bark is longitudinally fissured and exudes
whitish latex; flowering and fruiting are both reported in **December-January**.
