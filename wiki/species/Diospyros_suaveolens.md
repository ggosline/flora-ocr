# *Diospyros suaveolens* Gurke

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

34.Diospyros suaveolens Gurke

Bot.Jahrb.26:68(18g8).-F.WHITE，FWTA ed.2,2:II，tab.202 B,2 (1963)；Nigerian Trees 2:334 (1964). —Diospyros Barteri auct. non HIERN : HUTcH. et DALz.， FWTA ed. 1, 2:4 (1931) p.p. (Mildbraed no 10522). Specimens of this species appear in various herbaria under the following illegitimate name: *Diospyros confertiflora* Gurke ex Kennedy.

PL.25,p. 155, et CARTE 32，p. 173.

Tree reaching 30 m in height and 50 cm in basal diameter, usually with a straight
trunk fluted near the base and a small densely leafy crown with horizontal
branches and more or less pendent twigs. Young twigs are covered with a
brownish-yellow pubescence, sometimes almost felted, later becoming glabrous,
slightly angular, cracked, and dark purplish brown; the youngest leafy shoots are
clothed in a golden pubescence. Rhytidome scaly, dark purplish black; bark black
outside in section, dark yellow turning orange inward. Sapwood white becoming
pale yellow; heartwood pinkish to orange-yellow with black streaks.

Leaves with a short petiole 2-5 mm long; blade oblong or lanceolate, sometimes
slightly elliptic, up to 17.5 × 5 cm but more often about 8 × 3.5 cm, rounded
or slightly cordate at the base, acuminate at the apex. About five pairs of
lateral veins, concentrated toward the base of the blade, depart very obliquely
from the midrib, which is often pubescent above in its lower part. Veins
impressed above and very prominent beneath; lower surface with more or less
appressed hairs, especially on the veins. Blade papyraceous to subcoriaceous,
dark green and shining above, greyish-blue beneath; when dry, dark reddish
brown above and greyish below, with a pulverulent coating dotted with tiny
aggregates. Beneath the blade there are a few sunken glands near the midrib,
especially about five in the acumen that are remarkable for their size and
depressed surface.

Male inflorescences in lax cymes 4-6 cm across, very much branched and many
flowered, about 50 flowers, always borne high on the trunk or on branches, never
in the leaf axils and only exceptionally on old twigs. Axes clothed with
appressed pubescence; bracts ovate-lanceolate, 2-3 mm, pubescent. Pedicels
8-13 mm, broadened and articulate at the summit, likewise pubescent, with
bracteoles similar to the bracts. Calyx reddish brown, tomentellous outside and
densely appressed-pubescent inside, campanulate and often slightly constricted
above the swollen pedicel apex, about 5 mm high, with 5 short broad triangular
or rounded lobes, more or less apiculate. Corolla yellowish white, red-tipped
in bud, fragrant, about 2 cm high, glabrous, with a tube 6-8 mm high and
3-4 mm wide and (4-)5 oblong blunt lobes 10-14 × 6-8 mm. Stamens 22(-24)(-32?)
in two rows at the base of the tube, with filaments about 2 mm and linear
anthers 6-8 mm long, acute at the apex, glabrous or rarely with a few obliquely
erect hairs. Pistillode absent or reduced to a slightly ribbed mound clothed
with a tuft of erect hairs, altogether under 2 mm high.

Female inflorescences similar but usually less branched; pedicels 12-15 mm.
Calyx larger, 6 mm, and more spreading than in the male flower, with 4-5(-6)
lobes reaching half the depth of the calyx, slightly accrescent. Corolla
similar, a little larger and especially with a more inflated tube; no
staminodes. Ovary globose, covered with erect bristles, the whole 6-8 mm in
diameter, topped by a 1 mm style hidden among the bristles and prolonged by
4(-5?) long narrow lanceolate erect stigmas 6 mm long, silky on the outer half
and otherwise glabrous. Ovary 8-10-locular.

Fruits ovoid-conic, about 4 × 2.5 cm, densely clothed with short chocolate-brown
hairs and long yellowish irritating hairs, this pubescence falling rather
quickly. Fruiting calyx about 1 cm long, with 4-5(-6) triangular lobes almost
as long as the expanded tube. Seeds 4(-10?), elongated, somewhat pointed at the
top, about 25 mm long.

**Syntypes:** Staudt 207 and Zenker 951, Cameroon (P).

This species is widespread in Nigeria, Cameroon, and Gabon in evergreen humid
forest, apparently reaching even the limits of that formation where it meets
semi-deciduous forest.
