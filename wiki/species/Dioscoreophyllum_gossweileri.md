# *Dioscoreophyllum gossweileri* Exell

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Dioscoreophyllum]]

J. Bot. **73**, Suppl. Polypet. **I**: 10 (1935).

## Synonymy

- *Dioscoreophyllum triandrum* Troupin, Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles
  **19**: 433 (1949).

## Description

**Twining herb**; stem **glabrous or with sparse ± appressed hairs**.
**Leaves**: petiole 3.5–8.5 cm, geniculate and/or twisted at base, sparsely
hairy to glabrous; blade simple to **3(5)-lobed**, circular to elliptic,
**(9–)12–15 × 6–15 cm**, with lobes acuminate over 5–10 mm, mucronulate or
not, glabrous to sparsely hairy on both faces with ± appressed hairs; lateral
nerves in 4 or 5 pairs of which 2 or 3 basal pairs.

**Male inflorescence** **3–6 cm long**, ± densely flowered, with glabrous
axis; peduncle 0.5–1.5 cm; bracts narrowly triangular to ± linear, sparsely
hairy. **Male flower**: pedicel 5–8 mm; sepals spreading, obovate-elliptic,
1.5–2.5 × 1–1.5 mm; **stamens 3**, 2–2.5 mm long, with anthers in a **±
cylindrical synandrium** 1.5 mm long, **filaments fused into a stipe 1.5 mm**.

**Female inflorescence and flower** unknown. **Fruit**: mericarp subglobose,
6 × 5 mm, pubescent to glabrous; endocarp warty, apiculate.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon, Republic of the Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo,
Angola (Cabinda).
**Gabon**: Haut-Ogooué, Ogooué-Ivindo.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary and flooded forest. In Gabon and elsewhere at 300–430 m altitude.

## Notes

- In his original description, Exell described the male flower of
  *D. gossweileri* with **6 fused stamens**. However, Gabonese, Congolese
  and Kinshasa material consistently shows **only 3 stamens**. Examination
  of a flower from *Gossweiler 7639* (the type) is needed; it is very
  probable that Exell was mistaken. The fruit description follows Troupin.
- See [[Dioscoreophyllum_volkensii|*D. volkensii*]] for the other Gabonese
  congener (which has 6 stamens, a hemispherical synandrium, and a much
  longer male inflorescence).
