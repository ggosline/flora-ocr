# *Diospyros tricolor* "(Schum. & Thonn.) Hiern"

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

35.Diospyros tricolor (Schum.& Thonn.） Hiern

Monogr.Eben.,Trans.Camb.Phil.Soc.12:183,tab.5,r(1873)；FTA3： 521 (1877).— GURKE,Bot.Jahrb.43:203,tab.2 (1909).— F.WHITE, FWTA ed. 2,2:12 (1963).

Noltia tricolor ScHUM. et THoNN., Beskr. Guin. Pl. : 189 (1827), in Kong. Danske Vidensk. Sel. Phys.og Mathem. Skr.3 :2o9 (1828).

Specimens of this species appear in various herbaria under the following name: *Euclea warneckei* or *warneckeana* (Gurke ms.).

PL.26,1-II',p.I59,et CARTE 3o,p. 171.

Very much-branched shrub 0.5 to 2 m tall, sometimes taller, the stem usually not exceeding 3 cm in diameter, with procumbent, sometimes flexuous, divergent, glabrous branches and dense foliage; young zigzag twigs covered with ferruginous tomentum; wood white, very hard.

Leaves distichous, with short tomentose petiole 3-5 mm long; blade broadly elliptic or ovate, 3-9 x 2-5 cm, with obtuse-rounded base, margin edged by a fine cartilaginous rim, and apex obtuse or somewhat acute, sometimes with small apicule formed by a tuft of hairs, never acuminate; midrib and the 3-5 pairs of lateral veins only slightly marked above, somewhat raised and densely clothed beneath with appressed tawny hairs; lower surface also bearing silvery appressed hairs and, beneath that pubescence, a whitish powdery coating; upper surface green and glabrous when fresh, dark reddish when dry; small black glands scattered beneath the blade, especially toward the base and near the midrib; young leaves silky golden on both surfaces.

Male flowers subsessile, grouped 3-5 in the leaf axils with minute pubescent bracts and bracteoles; calyx 2.5 mm long, reddish silky outside, glabrous or with a few hairs inside, divided for half its height into 4 acute triangular teeth; corolla silky, at least outside the calyx, tubular-conical, more or less 4-angled, 6 mm high, with 4 acute erect subvalvate teeth 1 mm high; stamens inserted on the receptacle, about 8, unequal, 2-4 mm long, with filaments 0.5-2 mm bearing curved hairs near the base, and glabrous lanceolate anthers 1.5-2 mm long, pointed at the summit; pistillode rudimentary, conical, with tuft of erect hairs, not exceeding 1 mm.

Female flowers sessile, solitary, axillary, with minute pubescent bracts and bracteoles; calyx scarcely accrescent, somewhat larger than in the male flower, 4 mm, more fleshy and silky inside on the lobes; corolla similar to that of the male flower but somewhat larger, 8 mm, silky throughout; 6-8 glabrous staminodes 3 mm long, with filaments united for a considerable length at the base of the corolla tube; ovary ovoid-conical, 3.5 mm, with erect bristles, gradually tapering into a very short thick style prolonged by 2 erect linear stigmas 1.5 mm long with obtuse tips; ovary 4-locular.

Fruits, edible, ovoid-conical, slightly 4-angled, pointed-apiculate at the summit, 2.5-3 x 1.5-2 cm, orange, with somewhat uneven surface, glabrous or pubescent only at the summit, containing within a papery pericarp 4 seeds in 4 locules, later rapidly reduced to a single locule within a sticky pulp; seeds 1.5-2 cm, crowned by a small pyramidal aril.

**Type :**Thonning s.n., Guinée.

This species, known from Ivory Coast to Nigeria and also found in Gabon, occurs only in littoral shrub thickets.

### Material studied in Gabon
