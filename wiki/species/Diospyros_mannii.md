# *Diospyros mannii* Hiern

**Family**: [[Ebenaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Diospyros]]

## Full treatment

20.Diospyros Mannii Hiern

Monogr. Eben., Trans.Camb.Phil.Soc.12 ：255 (1873)；FTA 3：524(1877).—F.WHITE,FWTA ed.2,2：II,tab.202 A，2 (1963)；NigerianTrees 2:335 (1964). -Diospyros aggregata GURkE,Bot.Jahrb.43: 2O4 (1909). -Diospyros Talbotii WERNHAM,Cat.Pl. Talbot Nigeria :57 (1913). -Diospyros pseudaggregata MiLDBR.， Notizbl. Bot. Gart. Berl. 9 ：1047(1926). Diospyros ivorensis AUBREv.et PELLEGR.,Bull. Soc.Bot.France 83： 621 (1937).

Tree sometimes reaching nearly 30-35 m in height, with slender bole 20 m or more long, fluted for 3 m and star-shaped in cross section, 20-40 cm across, at the base in old trees; branches often somewhat pendulous, at least at the tips; young stems erect with subhorizontal whorled branchlets. Rhytidome reddish brown, more or less longitudinally fissured, peeling in small scales and often mottled with greenish and grayish lichen patches; bark black in section toward the outside and bright yellow within; sapwood lemon-yellow, heartwood black at the center. Young twigs hirsute, covered with long reddish or yellowish hairs, though sometimes with shorter, more appressed pubescence; adult twigs glabrous and blackish. In West Africa the second pubescence type is more frequent than the first, but this character does not appear sufficient to distinguish two specific taxa, though a subspecies might eventually be recognized.

Leaves with petiole 6-8 mm long, hirsute or pubescent, then glabrescent; blade elliptic, more rarely lanceolate-elliptic, reaching 10-12(-20) x 3-5(-7) cm, with some Nigerian specimens up to 28 x 10 cm, base obtusely cuneate, apex acute and often provided with a fairly long acumen, 1-2 cm; midrib impressed above, hirsute or pubescent beneath like the twigs and later glabrescent; likewise the 6-8 pairs of very ascending lateral veins, the lower 2-3 pairs close together and appearing more or less flabellate; tertiary veins subparallel; blade subcoriaceous, glossy green above and grayish beneath, the lower surface sparsely hirsute between the veins then glabrescent; when dry, the blade becomes dark reddish brown above and pink or grayish white beneath, the lower surface with a powdery mineralized epidermal coating; a few depressed glands occur near the midrib, especially at the axils of lateral veins and often on tertiary veins or veinlets.

Male inflorescences in compact cymes bearing up to 20-30 flowers, axillary or on leafless branchlets below the leaves, with pedicels 1-2 mm long, articulated at the summit and pubescent; bracts and bracteoles broadly ovate, pubescent. Calyx campanulate, 1 cm high, narrowed at the base, densely clothed outside and inside with a felt of hirsute reddish or yellowish hairs, later dark brown-black, divided almost to the base into 5-7 triangular lobes; corolla white or pinkish white, browning very quickly during anthesis, funnel-shaped, 18 mm high, fleshy, glabrous except for a band of hairs along the middle of the petals, with tube 5 mm long and widely open at the top, surmounted by 5-6 large oblong-lanceolate lobes with obtuse apex; 15-18 stamens, visible from outside, unequal, at most 8 mm long, with very short filament attached low, midway, or high in the corolla tube and bearing a linear anther densely strigose like the filaments; pistillode formed of a tuft of a few erect bristles.

Female inflorescences in loose cymes of 2-3 flowers on old branchlets and branches, with stout hirsute or pubescent axes 1-2 cm long, bearing at the base broadly ovate bracts pubescent outside; pedicels 1 cm long, likewise stout, articulated, and broadened into a disk at the summit. Female flowers rather similar to the male ones but somewhat larger, with slightly accrescent calyx 10-12 mm long and corolla 20-24 mm long; 6-12-18 linear strigose staminodes, 4 mm long; ovary broadly conical, 4 mm high, densely covered with erect reddish or yellowish bristles up to 3 mm long, easily detached, with 10(-12?) locules and directly surmounted by 5 erect stigmas, very conspicuous from outside, lanceolate, 6-8 mm high, stiff, densely silky on the back, with sinuous glabrous inner surface.

Fruits borne on stout pedicels 1-2(-3) cm long, subglobose-turbinate, about 5(-9?) x 4.5(-8?) cm, orange, at first densely covered with irritating reddish caducous bristles, then glabrous, surrounded at the base by the accrescent calyx 3(-6?) cm in diameter, star-lobed to the middle, and crowned by the style base; seeds 10, perhaps reaching 5 x 3 x 1.5 cm.

**Type:**Mann 924,Gabon (iso- P).

This species occurs in West Africa from Sierra Leone to Nigeria, and also in Cameroon, Gabon, the Central African Republic, both Congos, and Cabinda. It inhabits humid dense forest, whether evergreen or semi-deciduous; in Cameroon it has been collected in the regions of Kribi, Ambam, Edea, Nkongsamba, Deng Deng, and Yokadouma, and in Gabon near Makokou, in the upper Ngounie, and near Bitam.
