# *Triclisia megacarpa* Breteler, spec. nov.

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Triclisia]]

Described in: Breteler F.J. (2022) *Menispermaceae*. Flore du Gabon **60**:
88. **Spec. nov.**

**Diagnosis** (Latin, original): *Triclisiae dictyophyllae* Diels similis,
ab ea differt foliis 3 paribus nervosum basalium, floribus sepalis
interioribus multo brevioribus et fructibus majoribus sine rostro.

**Type**: Gabon, Ngounié, upper Waka area, 13 km on JFL forestry road B2,
primary forest, 1°20.7′S, 10°52′E, alt. 200 m, 28 Mar. 2004, *Wieringa,
Nzienqui, Moussavou & Mbombet 5115* (holo-: WAG [WAG.1102684,
WAG.1102685, WAG.1102686, WAG.1102687]; iso-: BR, LBV, MO).

## Description

Liana up to **at least 30 m** tall; **stem up to 4 cm thick**, finely
striate when dry, with brown tomentum, glabrescent. **Leaves**: petiole
straight or distally geniculate, cylindrical, ± finely striate when dry,
**(4.5–)6–17 cm**, puberulent, glabrescent; blade broadly ovate to ±
circular, **14–31 × (4.5–)13–30 cm**, obtuse to cordate at base, acute to
acuminate over 0.5–2 cm at apex, glabrous above, pubescent and
glabrescent on the major nerves beneath, **with 3 pairs of dominant basal
lateral nerves** and 1 or 2(3) additional pairs higher up.

**Inflorescence**: **male on the main stem and also on second-year,
defoliated lateral branches**, in panicles of cymes, **1–3 cm long**,
tomentose; female unknown. **Male flower yellow**: pedicel 3 mm, tomentose;
**sepals 15**, tomentose outside, of which 12 imbricate, ovate, 0.5–2 mm
long, the **3 inner valvate, obovate, 3 × 2 mm**, with the upper part
spreading to reflexed; petals 6, ovate-elliptic, 1 × 0.5 mm, glabrous;
stamens 1.5 mm long, anther curved and apiculate at apex; pistillode a
tuft of stiff hairs. **Female flower** unknown.

**Fruit**: mericarp obovoid, ± asymmetric, slightly flattened, **3–5 ×
2.5–5 × 1.5–3 cm, ± apiculate** (without beak), puberulent; mesocarp ± 5
mm thick; endocarp bony, 2–3 mm thick; condyle well developed.

## Distribution

**Range**: West Africa from Guinea to Nigeria, Cameroon and Gabon.
**Gabon**: Ngounié, Ogooué-Ivindo.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary forest, degraded forest. In Gabon up to 880 m altitude, elsewhere
up to 300 m.

## Notes

- This is the West/Central-African material **previously placed in *T.
  dictyophylla* by Jongkind (2017)** but distinguished by **3 (vs. 2)
  pairs of dominant basal nerves**, **shorter inner sepals (3 mm vs. 5–6.5
  mm)** and **larger fruits without a beak**. See the taxonomic note under
  [[Triclisia_dictyophylla]].
- Jongkind (2017) had described the drupe as glabrous; in fact it is
  finely puberulent.
