* This species is clearly very close to *D. cinnabarina* (no. 7). The observed differences, involving calyx, corolla, and style pubescence, leaf size and glabrescence, venation pattern, and gland distribution, seem too slight to justify specific rank. They suggest that *D. simulans* should disappear in favor of the earlier *D. cinnabarina*, though field and distributional studies may later justify infraspecific distinctions.

This species is known only from Cameroon, Gabon, and Cabinda, in coastal evergreen humid dense forest, though extending inland as far as Lastoursville in Gabon. It may also have been collected by Mildbraed, no. 5132 not 5312, in Congolese-type evergreen forest between Assobam and Lomie in Cameroon, although that specimen might instead belong to *D. cinnabarina*; likewise, the Lomie-region specimen Letouzey 3666 seems referable here.

Note: *Maba tenuifolia* Gurke, described from Ledermann 984 from the Kribi region and apparently now destroyed, may be close to *Diospyros simulans*. It was a shrub 6-8 m tall, with leaves on 6-8 mm petioles, glabrous lanceolate blades 10-14 x 4-5 cm narrowed onto the petiole above and acute at the apex; inflorescences on old wood in compact fascicled cymes; male flowers with pedicels 2-3 mm; calyx very short with 3 acute triangular lobes 2-2.5 mm; yellowish tubular to ventricose corolla 1 cm long with 3 narrow triangular teeth 3 mm high; and 9 glabrous stamens with 1-2 mm filaments and linear, long-pointed anthers 4-8 mm long.
