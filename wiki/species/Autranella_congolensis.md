# *Autranella congolensis* (De Wild.) A. Chev.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Autranella]]

## Description

A **very large forest tree** with thick branchlets and leaves grouped near the
ends of the shoots. The blades are oblong to slightly obovate, rounded and
obtusely acuminate at the apex, cuneate at the base, **10-14 x 4-5 cm**, and
glabrous; about **15 pairs** of secondary veins are present but are not
strongly differentiated from the fine reticulum. Petioles are slender and
reach **4-5 cm**.

Flowers are fasciculate in the leaf axils at the ends of branchlets, on
pedicels about **20 mm** long. There are **8 sepals** in two whorls, a corolla
tube about **7.5 mm** long, **8 lobes**, and **16 dorsal appendages** about
twice as long as the lobes. The **8 stamens** are inserted on the rim of the
tube and alternate with short staminodes. The ovary is tomentose and has
**8 uniovulate locules**.

The fruit is greenish yellow, ovoid, about **7 x 5 cm**, with rough matte
surface and yellow pulp. It usually contains **1 seed**, occasionally **2-3**.
Seeds are flattened-ovoid, pale brown and glossy, about **4.5 cm** long, with
a thick, hard testa and a **subrectangular ventral scar** about **2.5-3 x
1 cm**.

## Distribution

**Range**: equatorial Africa, including Gabon, Cameroon, and the Congo basin.  
**Gabon**: cited from Tchibanga and Doubagni; the treatment describes the
species as widespread in equatorial Africa but nowhere abundant.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary humid forest. The species is described as an emergent tree with a
straight cylindrical bole **20-30 m** long and more than **1.5 m** in
diameter, without buttresses, exuding sticky white latex from the bark.

## Notes

The treatment discusses floral variation among different collections and stops
short of deciding whether more than one Central African species should be
recognized. It notes that the Gabonese material corresponding to *Mimusops
Le-Testui* is the only element then considered sharply defined.
