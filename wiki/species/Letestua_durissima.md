# *Letestua durissima* (A. Chev.) H. Lec. emend.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Letestua]]

## Description

A **very large primary-forest tree**, with thick branchlets and leaves crowded
toward the ends of the shoots. The blades are obovate-oblong to oblong,
rounded at the apex, cuneate at the base, **16-22 x 5-10 cm**, glabrous, with
**12-16 pairs** of secondary veins linked by a very characteristic dense
network of transverse nervules and veinlets visible in the dry state. Petioles
are long, **3-4 cm**, and channelled near the blade.

Flowers are fasciculate in the axils of fallen leaves. Pedicels are glabrous,
about **2 cm** long. The **6 sepals** are arranged in two whorls of three and
are ferruginous-scaly outside. Petals are **12-18**, each with **2 dorsal
appendages**; stamens are **12-18**; the ovary is tomentose and surmounted by
a long glabrous style.

The fruit contains **1 seed**. The seed is flat, attenuate at both ends,
about **3.7 x 1.2 cm**, with a long narrow ventral scar; the endosperm is
thick and the cotyledons are foliaceous.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon and the Mayombe according to the treatment.  
**Gabon**: stated to occur across Gabon, though not abundantly; cited from
Tchibanga, Bouzimbi, Remboue, Liby, and Lonangue.

## Habitat and ecology

Primary humid forest. The species is described as a canopy tree with a bole
up to **30 m** under the branches and about **1 m** in diameter.

## Notes

The treatment explicitly sinks *Letestua floribunda* into this species. It
also emphasizes the remarkable hardness of the wood and records several
vernacular names built around that property.
