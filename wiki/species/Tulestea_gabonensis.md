# *Tulestea gabonensis* Aubrev. & Pellegr.

**Family**: [[Sapotaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Tulestea]]

## Description

Terminal buds are slightly pubescent; young branchlets soon become glabrous and
reddish. Leaves are variable in shape, oblanceolate in the holotype or oblong
to elliptic in other specimens, attenuate to obtusely acuminate at the apex and
sharply cuneate at the base, sometimes slightly decurrent onto the petiole.
Blades are **10-22 x 3.5-5.5 cm**, glabrous and coriaceous. Petiole **0.5-1 cm**
long. There are **6-10 pairs** of secondary veins, depressed above and raised
below; fine nervules are indistinct.

Flowers are greenish in dense leaf-axil fascicles. Buds are ovoid and apiculate.
Pedicels are **4 mm** long. The calyx has **5** lobes, slightly fused at the
base and glabrous or nearly so. The corolla is about **4 mm** long, with very
short tube and oblong lobes attenuate at the apex. The strong lanceolate
anthers are **3.5 mm** long, apiculate, and pubescent on the back. Staminodes
are reduced to scales. The ovary is pubescent and **5-locular**; the style is
slightly pubescent.

Fruit was unknown to the treatment.

## Distribution

**Range**: known only from Gabon in the treatment.  
**Gabon**: Mogonzo near the Offoue, Oyem, and Mocaba.
