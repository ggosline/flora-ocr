# *Triclisia riparia* Troupin

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Triclisia]]

Bull. Jard. Bot. État Bruxelles **19**: 414 (1949).

## Description

Twining liana with **stem and petiole bearing brown tomentum, glabrescent**;
stem finely wrinkled when dry. **Leaves**: petiole cylindrical, straight or
distally geniculate, **1.5–3(–3.5) cm**; blade oblong-elliptic,
**(9–)13–17(–20) × (4.5–)7–11(–12.5) cm**, obtuse to rounded at base,
rounded to obtuse or acute to shortly acuminate over 0.5 cm at apex,
slightly coriaceous, glabrescent to glabrous; **very young leaves entirely
covered with brown tomentum**; with **4 to 6 pairs of lateral nerves**.

**Inflorescence**: male unknown; **female ramiflorous**, in cymules with
up to 10 flowers, tomentellous; peduncle ± 1 cm. **Male flower** unknown.
**Female flower**: pedicel 1–2 mm; sepals 12, the outer face entirely
covered with **silvery-brown hairs**, glabrous on the inner face; the 9
outer bracteoliform, ovate, up to 3 × 2.5 mm; the 3 inner ovate-elliptic,
**5 × 4 mm**; petals 3, obovate to subspatulate, ± 2 × 0.5–1 mm, glabrous;
**carpels 30 to 40**, 3.5–4 mm long; ovary 1.5 mm, covered with
silvery-brown hairs.

**Fruit**: mericarp globose-ellipsoidal, truncate at base, **2 × 1.5 cm**,
finely puberulent, smooth; mesocarp pulpy, yellowish-white; endocarp bony.
Seed curved, 1.5–2 cm long.

## Distribution

**Range**: Cameroon, Gabon, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Central
African Republic.
**Gabon**: Ngounié, Woleu-Ntem.

## Habitat and ecology

Riverine forests. In Gabon up to 300 m altitude, elsewhere up to 450 m.

## Notes

- Distinguished from the similar [[Triclisia_gabonensis]] by **tomentose
  (not stiff-appressed-hairy) indumentum** and by **30–40 carpels** in the
  female flower (vs. ± 15 in *T. gabonensis*).
- Male flower **unknown**.
