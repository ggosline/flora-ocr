# *Kolobopetalum synsepalum* Breteler

**Family**: [[Menispermaceae]] · **Genus**: [[Kolobopetalum]]

Pl. Ecol. Evol. **154**: 287 (2021).

## Description

**Twining herb or subligneous liana**, up to **7 m** long, with slightly
milky sap; branches wrinkled when dry. **Leaves**: petiole 2.5–8 cm; blade
ovate, **6–10 × 3–4(–6.5) cm**, obtuse to slightly cordate at base, acuminate
over 0.5–1 cm and finely pointed at apex, with 5 or 6 pairs of lateral
nerves, ± prominent beneath.

**Male inflorescence** a raceme **generally branched in the basal part**, up
to 17 cm long, with flowers fascicled by 2 or 3; peduncle 0.5 cm; bracts
narrowly ovate-triangular, 1–2 mm. **Male flower**: pedicel (1–)2–4 mm;
**outer sepals triangular, fused at base**, 0.8–1 × 0.7–0.8 mm; **inner
sepals fused over two-thirds, forming a ± urceolate calyx**, broadly ovate
to elliptic, 2.5–3 × 2.8–3 mm, thickened at base; petals oblong, 0.8–1.5 ×
0.3–0.6 mm, with involute margin; stamens 6, ± free or slightly fused at
base, ± 1.5 mm.

**Female flower** unknown. **Infructescence** a raceme up to 5 cm long.
**Fruit**: immature mericarp globose to ellipsoidal, **7–10 mm** in diameter,
green; endocarp with pointed spines.

## Distribution

**Range**: Gabon and Republic of the Congo.
**Gabon**: Ngounié, Nyanga.

## Habitat and ecology

Forest edge, secondary forest and gallery forest, at 40–100 m altitude.

## Notes

- A recent (2021) description by the same author. The defining feature is
  the **fusion of the inner male sepals** into a ± urceolate calyx — unique
  in the genus, hence the species epithet.
