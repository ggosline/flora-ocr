# Anacardiaceae

**Authority**: Lindl. (1830), nom. cons.  
**Order**: Sapindales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Trees, shrubs, or lianas with resin canals and resinous, gummy, or milky
exudates. Leaves are usually alternate and pinnate or trifoliolate, flowers are
small and often functionally unisexual, and the fruits are drupaceous. Bark,
sap, and fruit structure are major diagnostic characters in the treatment.

## Distribution

A cosmopolitan but chiefly tropical and subtropical family of about 81 genera
and 800 species. The Vol. 50 treatment reports six indigenous genera with 31
species in Gabon, plus three introduced genera with four species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol50]]  
**Year**: 2017  
**Author in treatment**: Frans J. Breteler  
**Source**: `sources/Anacardiaceae_vol50_liteparse`

This is one of the larger family treatments among the first fifteen ingests. It
covers the indigenous Gabonese core in detail, while also discussing introduced
fruit trees and clarifying that some introduced taxa are keyed but not fully
treated.

## Notes

The treatment explicitly rejects the previously reported Gabonese occurrence of
*Haematostaphis*. It also notes that *Mangifera indica* is widely naturalised
and treated, whereas some other cultivated anacards are only mentioned in the
key context.
