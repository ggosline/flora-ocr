# Arecaceae

**Authority**: Bercht. & J.Presl (1820), nom. alt.  
**Alternate family name in source**: Palmae Juss. (1789), nom. cons.  
**Order**: Arecales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Palms are arborescent, shrubby, or lianescent monocots with spiral leaves,
sheathing leaf bases, highly structured inflorescences, and drupaceous or
fibrous fruits. The family combines acaulescent understorey forms, canopy palms,
and climbing rattans, making it one of the most architecturally diverse lineages
represented in the Gabon flora.

## Distribution

A mainly tropical family of 186 genera and more than 2,500 species, distributed
through the tropics and subtropics. The Gabon treatment recognises 11 genera and
34 species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol53]]  
**Year**: 2019  
**Authors in treatment**: Thomas L.P. Couvreur, Terry C.H. Sunderland, with
contributions by Suzanne Mogue Kamga  
**Source**: `sources/Arecaceae_vol53_liteparse`

This is a large, single-family volume. It treats both rattans and non-climbing
palms and uses the traditional family name **Palmae** as the heading while also
giving **Arecaceae** as the alternate conserved name.

## Notes

The treatment is a major structural expansion for the wiki because it adds a
large monocot family dominated by palms rather than climbers, shrubs, or small
herbs. It also makes clear that the Gabonese palm flora includes both wild palms
and some economically important introduced or cultivated taxa.
