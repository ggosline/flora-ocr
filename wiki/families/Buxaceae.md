# Buxaceae

**Authority**: Dumort. (1822), nom. cons.  
**Order**: Buxales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Trees or shrubs with simple usually coriaceous leaves and small unisexual
flowers, the female flowers with a superior 3-locular ovary and the fruits
usually capsular. The family is morphologically understated, but structurally
distinct once the inflorescences and fruits are examined closely.

## Distribution

Buxaceae comprises six genera and around 125 species, especially in temperate
regions and tropical uplands. In Africa only *Buxus* is recognised, and in
Gabon the treatment records a single species.

## Notes

The treatment explicitly notes that Buxaceae is the sole family of the order
Buxales and is not especially close to Euphorbiaceae or Celastraceae despite
older morphological comparisons. It also adopts a broad *Buxus* concept that
includes names once segregated as *Notobuxus* and *Buxella*.
