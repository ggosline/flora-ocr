# Apiaceae

**Authority**: Lindl. (1836), nom. cons.  
**Order**: Apiales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Usually aromatic herbs with sheathing petiole bases, often compound leaves, and
characteristic compound umbels. Flowers are small, 5-merous, epigynous, and the
fruits split into two mericarps suspended from a carpophore. These characters
make the family easy to recognise even though the Gabonese representation is
very small.

## Distribution

A large cosmopolitan family of roughly 445 genera and c. 3,500 species, centred
mainly in temperate regions. In Gabon the treatment recognises only the genus
*Centella*, represented by *Centella asiatica*.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol38]]  
**Year**: 2009  
**Author in treatment**: Marc S.M. Sosef  
**Source**: `sources/Apiaceae_vol38_liteparse`

The Gabon treatment is short and centred on a single species. It is notable for
explicitly separating *Hydrocotyle* from Apiaceae and aligning it instead with
Araliaceae on molecular grounds.

## Notes

The treatment flags that additional Apiaceae may still be found in Gabon,
especially in montane and inselberg habitats. At present, however, the family is
represented in the flora by a single widespread species.
