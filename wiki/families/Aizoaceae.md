# Aizoaceae

**Authority**: Rudolphi (1830), nom. cons.  
**Order**: Caryophyllales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Annual or perennial herbs, sometimes shrubs, often distinctly succulent.
Leaves are usually opposite, sometimes alternate or falsely whorled, simple,
with stipules present or absent. Flowers are bisexual and actinomorphic, with
tepals free or basally fused into a tube; the ovary ranges from superior to
inferior and the fruit is usually a loculicidal capsule.

## Distribution

Aizoaceae is a predominantly arid-zone family with about 127 genera and around
2,500 species, strongly centred in southern Africa. In Gabon the treatment
recognises only one genus, *Sesuvium*, represented by a single species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol42]]  
**Year**: 2011  
**Source**: `sources/Aizoaceae_vol42_liteparse`

The Vol. 42 treatment is very small and focuses on a single coastal species,
*Sesuvium portulacastrum*. It notes that *Glinus*, previously treated in
Molluginaceae, is no longer accepted in Aizoaceae for the Gabon flora.

## Notes

The treatment explicitly flags historical instability in the family limits,
especially around *Glinus*. In the current Gabonese treatment the family is a
small, herbaceous, largely littoral element of the flora.
