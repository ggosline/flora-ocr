# Alismataceae

**Authority**: Vent. (1799)  
**Order**: Alismatales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Aquatic or marsh herbs, often perennial and stoloniferous, sometimes annual,
with a basal rosette of emergent, floating, or submerged leaves. Flowers are
actinomorphic, usually with 3 sepals and 3 petals, numerous free carpels, and
achenial fruits. The family is strongly tied to wet habitats.

## Distribution

Alismataceae is cosmopolitan, with about 12 genera and roughly 80 species. In
Gabon the treatment recognises only *Ranalisma*, represented by a single
species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol38]]  
**Year**: 2009  
**Author in treatment**: Marc S.M. Sosef  
**Source**: `sources/Alismataceae_vol38_liteparse`

The Gabon treatment is narrow and centred on *Ranalisma humile*, a marsh and
shallow-water species. It presents Alismataceae as a small aquatic family in
the country relative to its broader cosmopolitan range.

## Notes

The source emphasises the family’s aquatic specialisation and the strongly
reduced Gabonese representation compared with the worldwide diversity of the
group.
