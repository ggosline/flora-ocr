# Begoniaceae

**Authority**: C.Agardh (1824)  
**Order**: Cucurbitales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Mostly fleshy herbs or subshrubs, terrestrial or occasionally epiphytic, often
with strongly asymmetric stipulate leaves and unisexual flowers. Male flowers
have a variable androecium, female flowers an inferior usually winged ovary, and
the seeds are minute. The family is instantly recognisable in the field once the
leaf asymmetry and flower dimorphism are understood.

## Distribution

Begoniaceae contains only two genera worldwide: the enormous pantropical
*Begonia* and the Hawaiian endemic *Hillebrandia*. In Gabon the family is
represented only by *Begonia*, but with a large treated species set in Vol. 39.

## Notes

Although monogeneric in Gabon, Begoniaceae is species-rich there. The treatment
is morphologically broad, covering acaulescent, rhizomatous, suffrutescent, and
occasionally epiphytic forms within a single genus.
