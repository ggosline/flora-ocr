# Ancistrocladaceae

**Authority**: Planch. ex Walp. (1851), nom. cons.
**Order**: Caryophyllales (APG IV)

A small, **monogeneric** family of evergreen forests. The single genus
[[Ancistrocladus]] contains about 16 species ranging from tropical Africa
through southern India and Sri Lanka, across Southeast Asia (via Hainan) to
Sumatra. Four species occur in Gabon.

## Diagnosis

Lianas or shrubs that become sarmentose at maturity, glabrous. Juvenile growth
erect, shrubby, monopodial, little or not branched, **without hooks**; adult
growth with sympodial main stems bearing **curved to helicoidal hooks** —
ordinarily arranged in a single plane along the branch and branchlets. Stipules
absent or small and caducous.

Leaves spirally arranged, simple, sessile but often pseudopetiolate; juvenile
and adult leaves usually heterophyllous; blade entire-margined; both surfaces
bearing small foveoles (= crypts), each containing a single wax-secreting hair.

Inflorescences extra-axillary or terminal on the lateral branchlets,
paniculate, usually bracteate. Flowers bisexual; sepals and petals 5, free or
shortly connate at the base; stamens 10, with filaments shortly connate at the
base; anthers basifixed, dehiscing by longitudinal slits. Ovary inferior or
semi-inferior, 1-loculate, with basilateral placentation, with 1 ovule; styles
1 or 3, with 3 stigmas.

Fruits indehiscent, dry, **a nut, with the persistent style base and accrescent
sepals**, 1-seeded. Seeds subglobose, with ruminate endosperm and folded
cotyledons.

## Distribution

Pantropical Old World: tropical Africa, southern India and Sri Lanka,
Southeast Asia through Hainan to Sumatra. Approximately 16 species in total,
of which **four occur in Gabon** (all in the genus *Ancistrocladus*).

text alone.

## Notes

The family is currently classified within the order **Caryophyllales** and is
**closely related to the Dioncophyllaceae**, with which it shares the
distinctive habit (lianas with hooked branchlets) and several floral
characters. Both families have been the subject of phytochemical and
phylogenetic interest because of their carnivory-related affinities within
Caryophyllales (the Dioncophyllaceae include true carnivorous lianas).
