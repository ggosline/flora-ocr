# Aristolochiaceae

**Authority**: Juss. (1789)  
**Order**: Piperales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Perennial herbs, climbers, lianas, and more rarely shrubs or trees, with simple
often cordate leaves and flowers lacking a true corolla. The petaloid calyx is
the main showy floral structure and may be strongly tubular, inflated, or
zygomorphic. Fruits are usually capsular, though the Gabonese genus treated here
has indehiscent drupaceous fruits.

## Distribution

The family contains about 12 genera and roughly 450 to 500 species worldwide. In
Gabon the treatment recognises a single genus, *Pararistolochia*, with six
species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol42]]  
**Year**: 2011  
**Source**: `sources/Aristolochiaceae_vol42_liteparse`

The Vol. 42 treatment is centred entirely on *Pararistolochia* and includes one
species described as new and one new combination. It is one of the clearest
examples in the present batch of a family page built around a single Gabonese
genus.

## Notes

The treatment emphasises the distinction of *Pararistolochia* from
*Aristolochia*, especially the woody indehiscent fruits, absence of
pseudostipules, and the placement of the glandular areas in the utricle.
