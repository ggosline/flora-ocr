# Burmanniaceae

**Authority**: Blume (1827)  
**Order**: Dioscoreales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Tiny annual or perennial herbs that are either chlorophyllous and autotrophic or
wholly mycoheterotrophic and achlorophyllous. Leaves are reduced to scales or
small green blades; flowers are 3-merous, tubular, and often winged in
*Burmannia*. The family combines some of the most delicate autotrophic wetland
plants with highly reduced forest-floor mycoheterotrophs.

## Distribution

Burmanniaceae comprises about eight genera and roughly 90 pantropical species.
In Gabon the treatment recognises three genera and five species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol41]]  
**Year**: 2010  
**Source**: `sources/Burmanniaceae_vol41_liteparse`

The treatment includes both the main family account and an appendix with a new
nomenclatural combination and a newly described species. It is therefore one of
the more taxonomically active small-family treatments among the first fifteen
ingests.

## Notes

The Gabon flora includes both green *Burmannia* and non-green
mycoheterotrophs (*Campylosiphon*, *Gymnosiphon*). That contrast makes this one
of the clearest examples in the flora of a family spanning radically different
nutritional strategies.
