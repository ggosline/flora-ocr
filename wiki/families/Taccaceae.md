# Taccaceae

**Authority**: Dumort. (1829)  
**Order**: Dioscoreales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Terrestrial perennial herbs with globose or elongate rhizomes and no aerial
stem. Leaves are large and basal, while the inflorescences arise with the
leaves on erect radical peduncles and form involucrate umbels. Flowers are
bisexual, epigynous, and actinomorphic in general outline, though their
prefloration is zygomorphic; the 6 tepals are fused into a tube, 6 stamens are
inserted on that tube, and the inferior ovary is 3-carpellate with many ovules.
The fruits are fleshy and many-seeded, with distinctly ribbed seeds.

## Distribution

A monogeneric family of 12 species, centred mainly in Southeast Asia but also
extending to Africa, Madagascar, the Mascarenes, Australia, the Pacific, and
South America. In Africa, and in Gabon, the treatment recognises a single
species.

## Treatments

### Vol 38 (2009)

**Author in treatment**: Marc S.M. Sosef  
**Pages**: 59-64  
**Source**: `sources/Taccaceae_vol38_liteparse`

The treatment is compact but morphologically rich, centred entirely on
[[Tacca_leontopetaloides|*Tacca leontopetaloides*]]. It gives a full account of
the species' large dissected leaves, filiform floral bracts, and edible but
toxic-when-fresh rhizomes, and records the plant as especially widespread in
Gabon's coastal zone.

## Notes

Within the wiki this family adds a very different growth form from the
rainforest shrubs, trees, and lianas that dominate many other treatments:
a robust stemless geophyte of open and disturbed habitats. The source also
highlights its ethnobotanical importance as an arrowroot-yielding plant whose
rhizomes require careful preparation before consumption.
