# Huaceae

**Authority**: A.Chev. (1947)  
**Order**: Oxalidales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Trees or shrubs with bark and leaves that smell strongly of garlic. Leaves are
alternate, simple and entire, with stipules usually soon caducous and a
distinctive pennate venation whose minor veins form closed, rectangular meshes.
Flowers are axillary, solitary to fasciculate, bisexual and actinomorphic, with
4- or 5-merous perianths, 8-10 stamens, and a superior unilocular ovary.
Fruits are ovoid, either indehiscent or opening by 5 valves, and the seeds are
light brown, albuminous, and likewise garlic-scented.

## Distribution

A very small family confined to humid tropical Africa. The treatment recognises
2 genera and 4 species worldwide, all 4 recorded from Gabon.

## Treatments

### Vol 38 (2009)

**Author in treatment**: Yves Azizet Issembe  
**Pages**: 25-34  
**Source**: `sources/Huaceae_vol38_liteparse`

The treatment covers the complete Gabonese representation of the family: the
3-species genus [[Afrostyrax]] and the monotypic [[Hua]]. It is especially
useful for its clear generic key based on indumentum, calyx structure, petals,
anthers, and fruit dehiscence, and for documenting the repeated use of the bark
and seeds as garlic substitutes in Gabonese communities.

## Notes

The family account stresses how tightly restricted Huaceae is, both
geographically and taxonomically. All Gabonese species are woody rainforest
plants with alliacous bark or seeds, but the two genera differ sharply in
indumentum, floral construction, and fruit type.
