# Anthericaceae

**Authority**: J.Agardh (1858)  
**Order**: Asparagales (modern placement)

## Diagnosis

Perennial geophytic herbs with rhizomes or corms, basal leaves with parallel
venation, and terminal racemose to paniculate inflorescences. Flowers are
actinomorphic, 3-merous, with 6 free tepals and a superior 3-locular ovary;
capsules are loculicidal and the seeds are black, albuminous, and
phytomelanin-bearing.

## Distribution

The family contains about nine genera and c. 200 species globally, especially
in tropical and subtropical regions. In Gabon the treatment recognises only
*Chlorophytum*, represented by six species plus one treated infraspecific taxon.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol41]]  
**Year**: 2010  
**Source**: `sources/Anthericaceae_vol41_liteparse`

The Gabonese treatment is entirely organised around *Chlorophytum*. It is a
moderate-sized monoceneric treatment and gives a useful family-level discussion
of the old Liliaceae/Agavaceae/Asphodelaceae placement debate.

## Notes

The source highlights the unstable historical circumscription of the family,
especially its treatment within broad Liliaceae and later association with
Agavaceae or Asphodelaceae. The Gabon flora uses Anthericaceae as a distinct
family.
