# Ebenaceae

**Authority**: Gurke
**Order**: Ericales (modern placement)
**Common name (FR)**: *Ebenacees*

## Diagnosis

Tropical trees and shrubs with alternate, simple, entire leaves and usually
dioecious flowers. Flowers are actinomorphic and hypogynous, with a persistent
calyx, a fleshy corolla, and a superior ovary divided into 2-8 locules by
false partitions; the fruits are berries, often seated in an accrescent calyx,
and several species produce ebony or edible fruits.

The Vol 18 treatment repeatedly stresses the importance of foliar tannin
pockets ("glands"), calyx form, corolla indument, and floral sex for
identification. In Gabon the family is represented only by [[Diospyros]].

## Distribution

Pantropical. The family comprises two genera globally, but in tropical Africa
the flora treated here is overwhelmingly centered on *Diospyros*. Vol 18
recognises **38 species** in the Cameroon-Gabon treatment area, of which **30
are recorded from Gabon**. The wiki also includes one later article-based
species page, [[Diospyros_kupensis]], a Cameroonian endemic described in 1998.

## Treatments

### Vol 18 (1970)

**Authors**: Rene Letouzey, Frank White
**Pages**: whole volume; family treatment with keys, plates, maps, introduced and excluded species
**Source**: `sources/Ebenaceae_vol18_mineru`

Volume 18 is a single-family treatment devoted to Ebenaceae, specifically the
African species of *Diospyros* occurring in Cameroon and Gabon, with
supplementary Cameroonian material. It provides four identification keys
(vegetative, male flowers, female flowers, fruits), 27 plates, distribution
maps, and extensive specimen citations. The treatment recognises 30 Gabonese
species but discusses 38 species overall, several of them outside Gabon.

### Kew Bulletin 53(2) (1998)

**Authors**: George Gosline, Martin Cheek
**Pages**: 461-465
**Source**: `sources/articles/diospyros_kupensis/liteparse`

This short article describes [[Diospyros_kupensis]], a localized Mt Kupe
species in Southwest Cameroon. It is not part of the *Flore du Gabon* proper,
but it is relevant to the regional circumscription of *Diospyros* because it is
compared explicitly with [[Diospyros_conocarpa]].

## Notes

The source consistently links species delimitation to ecology: some taxa are
restricted to evergreen coastal forest, others to semi-deciduous forest,
river margins, or littoral scrub. Ebony production is concentrated in
[[Diospyros_crassiflora]], but several other species are noted as yielding dark
heartwood or edible fruits.

Because the Vol 18 OCR is whole-volume and figure extraction is not mapped
cleanly to species-page image paths yet, this ingest prioritises textual
coverage and specimen-based distribution. Plate references are preserved on
species pages via the source citation and notes.

The family remains represented in Gabon by a single genus, [[Diospyros]], but
post-volume articles continue to add regional context and new non-Gabon species
that may matter for comparison and identification work.
