# Sapotaceae

**Authority**: Juss. (1789)  
**Order**: Ericales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Mostly trees, more rarely shrubs or even lianescent forms, almost always with
latex and usually with simple alternate leaves. Flowers are generally
hermaphroditic, often fasciculate on leafy or defoliated branchlets, branches,
or trunks; the calyx may be simple or double, the corolla is sympetalous, and
staminodes are often taxonomically important. Fruits are berries or, in a few
Gabonese genera, dry dehiscent capsules; seeds are typically glossy brown or
yellow-brown and marked by a distinctive ventral or basiventral scar.

## Distribution

The source presents Sapotaceae as one of the best represented forest families
in Gabon. For the accepted/present taxa materialized on this wiki, Vol. 1 is
now represented by **23 genera and 48 species pages**. The monograph itself
uses a somewhat broader historical circumscription and repeatedly notes that
the family remained under-collected and likely to grow with further prospecting.

## Treatments

### Vol 1 (1961)

**Author**: A. Aubreville  
**Pages**: full volume  
**Source**: `sources/Sapotaceae_vol01_paddle`

This is the opening fascicle of the whole *Flore du Gabon* series and a full
family monograph rather than a short treatment. It combines a broad essay on
Sapotaceae morphology, ecology, wood use, and biogeography with generic and
specific keys, plates, and species accounts. The text repeatedly emphasises the
importance of Sapotaceae in primary humid forest and the unusually high
Gabonese diversity of the family.

## Notes

The source predates APG concepts and uses an older generic framework in places,
but it is still one of the foundational synthetic treatments in the corpus.
Many of the genera are defined as much by seed scar morphology and fruit type as
by floral structure. The monograph also stresses ecological contrasts between
terra-firme giants such as *Baillonella* and *Tieghemella*, riparian and
marsh-edge lineages, coastal thicket species such as [[Manilkara_lacera]], and
small understory shrubs.

This family page marks the start of a **chunked ingest** of Vol. 1. The
monograph is now ingested across all accepted Gabonese genera treated in the
volume, including [[Manilkara]], [[Letestua]], [[Autranella]],
[[Tieghemella]], [[Baillonella]], [[Lecomtedoxa]], [[Neolemonniera]],
[[Gluema]], [[Tridesmostemon]], [[Omphalocarpum]], [[Englerophytum]],
[[Wildemaniodoxa]], [[Zeyherella]], [[Tulestea]], [[Afrosersalisia]],
[[Pachystela]], [[Synsepalum]], [[Vincentella]], [[Pseudopachystela]],
[[Gambeya]], [[Delpydora]], [[Donella]], and [[Aningueria]].
