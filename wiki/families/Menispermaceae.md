# Menispermaceae

**Authority**: Juss. (1789), nom. cons.
**Order**: [[Ranunculales]] (APG IV)
**Common name**: moonseed family

## Diagnosis

Woody lianas, climbing herbs, **shrubs or shrublets** (rare in this family
— see [[Anisocycla]] and [[Penianthus]]); without tendrils. Wood with
medullary rays in cross-section. **Stipules absent**. Leaves alternate,
peltate or not; blade simple and pinnatinerved, or palmatilobate and
palmatinerved, sometimes 3-foliolate (only in [[Syntriandrium]]). Flowers
**3-merous, unisexual, dioecious**. Male inflorescences very diverse:
axillary, supra-axillary, on defoliated branches or on the trunk; solitary
or fascicled, sometimes uniflorous, often pseudopanicles or pseudoracemes
of cymules. Female inflorescences usually similar to the male, generally
smaller and/or simpler. **Male flowers** usually pedicellate, rarely
sessile, actinomorphic (rarely zygomorphic), generally very small; sepals
(1 or)3 to 12 or more, free (rarely fused), in 1 or several whorls; petals
1 to 6 or absent, free or fused; **stamens 2, 3 or 6, rarely 4 or 5**, free
or fused, sometimes synandrous, with introrse or extrorse anthers,
longitudinally or transversely dehiscent. **Female flowers**: sepals usually
like the male; petals like the male; staminodes present or absent;
**carpels (1 or)3 to 6 or more, free**, with 2 ovules of which 1 abortive;
style ± terminal or absent, with entire to ± lacinate or lobed stigma.
Fruits generally apocarpous, with **drupaceous mericarps**, sessile or
stipitate; the style/stigma scar **near the base**; exocarp membranous to
subcoriaceous, smooth to rugose or tuberculate; mesocarp ± pulpy; **endocarp
cartaceous or bony**, rugose, tuberculate, wrinkled or echinate, sometimes
smooth, with or without a **condyle** (a ventral intrusion of the endocarp
wall into the locule). **Seeds often horseshoe-shaped**; albumen present
or absent.

## Distribution

A family of **67 genera and ± 400 species**, the majority in the tropics.
**In Africa: 25 genera and ± 100 species.** **In Gabon: 22 genera and 34 to
36 species**, of which **16 genera are represented by a single species**.

## Treatments

### Vol 60 (2022)

**Author**: Breteler F.J.
**Pages**: 29–89
**Source**: `sources/Menispermaceae_vol60_liteparse`

Breteler's treatment in vol 60 is a major reorganization of African
Menispermaceae, with **5 species described as new**:

- [[Albertisia_badia]] — Breteler, spec. nov., type from Ogooué-Lolo
- [[Albertisia_mouilaensis]] — Breteler, spec. nov., type from Ngounié
- [[Synclisia_oligogyna]] — Breteler, spec. nov., type from Woleu-Ntem
- [[Triclisia_gabonensis]] — Jongkind & Breteler, spec. nov., type from
  Haut-Ogooué
- [[Triclisia_megacarpa]] — Breteler, spec. nov., type from Ngounié

Plus one undescribed entity:

- [[Albertisia_sp_nov]] — single sterile collection from Ogooué-Lolo
- [[Kolobopetalum_spec_nov]] — single sterile collection from Moyen-Ogooué
  (alternatively *Rhigiocarya*)

Breteler also notes that "about ten species remain poorly known, mostly
because of missing female (or sometimes male) flowering material."

Notable taxonomic acts: synonymy of *Chasmanthera welwitschii* under *C.
dependens*; synonymy of *Tiliacora odorata* and *T. ovalis* under *T.
klaineana*; reinstatement of *Triclisia dictyophylla* in a wider sense
(against Jongkind 2017) coupled with description of *T. megacarpa*.

Bibliography cited: Diels (1910); Jacques (2009); Kessler (1993); Ortiz et
al. (2016); Troupin (1962).

## Notes

- **High generic diversity, low per-genus diversity**: of the 22 genera in
  Gabon, **16 are monospecific** in the country and several are
  monospecific worldwide ([[Beirnaertia]], [[Chasmanthera]], [[Dialytheca]],
  [[Leptoterantha]], [[Limaciopsis]], [[Sarcolophium]], [[Syntriandrium]]).
  This reflects the family-level pattern of small, often relictual, central
  African genera.
- **Habit dominated by lianas/twiners**: Only **two non-climbing genera**
  occur in Gabon: [[Anisocycla_jollyana]] (a 30–70 cm shrublet) and
  [[Penianthus_longifolius]] (a true shrub up to 4.5 m). All other species
  are woody lianas or twining herbs. **No species is a tree.**
- **Many described as new in this volume**: 5 spp. nov., placing
  Menispermaceae (along with [[Dilleniaceae]]) among the most actively
  studied families in vol 60.
- **Sister to [[Ranunculaceae]]** and [[Berberidaceae]] within the
  [[Ranunculales]] (APG IV).
