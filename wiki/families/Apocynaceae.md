# Apocynaceae

**Authority**: Juss. (1789), nom. cons.  
**Order**: Gentianales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Trees, shrubs, lianas, and a few herbs, usually with conspicuous white or pale
latex, opposite or whorled simple leaves, and sympetalous flowers. The family is
morphologically diverse but is held together by its floral architecture,
frequent colleters, paired or syncarpous ovaries, and characteristic fruit and
seed syndromes including comose seeds in many lineages.

## Distribution

Apocynaceae is a large cosmopolitan family of tropical and subtropical regions.
The Vol. 59 treatment is explicitly only the first part, covering the
subfamilies Apocynoideae and Rauvolfioideae rather than the whole family across
all its major clades.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol59]]  
**Year**: 2022  
**Author in treatment**: Carel C.H. Jongkind  
**Source**: `sources/Apocynaceae_vol59_liteparse`

This is the largest family treatment among the first fifteen ingests and is
currently represented on the wiki only at family-page level. The source is
born-digital liteparse output and covers a very broad woody and lianescent
component of the Gabon flora.

## Notes

The treatment treats Apocynaceae broadly, explicitly absorbing the historically
separate Asclepiadaceae and Periplocaceae. It also notes that some ornamental
escapees, such as *Allamanda cathartica* and *Catharanthus roseus*, are probably
naturalised in Gabon.
