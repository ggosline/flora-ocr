# Aloaceae

**Authority**: Cronquist (1981)  
**Order**: Asparagales (modern placement)

## Diagnosis

Perennial herbs, shrubs, or arborescent succulents with thick yellow roots,
rosettes of fleshy toothed leaves, and usually showy racemose or paniculate
inflorescences. Flowers are tubular, 6-tepalled, nectariferous, and usually
bird-pollinated in the broader African radiation; fruits are loculicidal
capsules with many seeds.

## Distribution

The treatment recognises nine genera in the family as then circumscribed, with
the great majority centred in southern Africa and Madagascar. In Gabon only the
genus *Aloe* is recognised, with two treated species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol47]]  
**Year**: 2015  
**Author in treatment**: Charlotte Sletten Bjorå  
**Source**: `sources/Aloaceae_vol47_liteparse`

The Vol. 47 treatment covers the Gabonese *Aloe* element only. It retains the
older family concept Aloaceae, while also discussing APG-style merger into a
broader asphodeloid/xanthorrhoeaceous circumscription.

## Notes

The treatment is taxonomically transitional: it still uses Aloaceae, but
explicitly notes the modern shift toward inclusion in a broader
Xanthorrhoeaceae/Asphodelaceae concept. For the wiki, the historical treatment
name is preserved while the order placement is kept modern.
