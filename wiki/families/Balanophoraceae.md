# Balanophoraceae

**Authority**: Rich. (1822)  
**Order**: Santalales (modern placement)

## Diagnosis

Holoparasitic, fleshy herbs lacking chlorophyll and true roots, arising from
subterranean tubers attached to host roots. Leaves are absent or reduced to
scales, and the inflorescences are dense, often colourful heads or spikes of
strongly reduced unisexual flowers.

## Distribution

Balanophoraceae contains about 18 genera and 40 to 45 species in tropical and
subtropical regions. Continental Africa has six genera and six species, but the
Gabon treatment recognises only *Thonningia*, with a single species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol40]]  
**Year**: 2010  
**Source**: `sources/Balanophoraceae_vol40_liteparse`

The Gabon treatment is small and focused on *Thonningia sanguinea*, a striking
root parasite of forest and plantation systems. It also deliberately includes a
second African genus in the key because that taxon could yet be found in Gabon.

## Notes

The treatment explicitly flags *Chlamydophytum aphyllum* as a taxon still worth
searching for in Gabon. Ethnobotanical notes are unusually prominent for such a
small parasitic family treatment.
