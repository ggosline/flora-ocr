# Annonaceae

**Authority**: Juss. (1789)  
**Order**: Magnoliales (APG IV)

## Diagnosis

Woody tropical plants ranging from shrubs and medium-sized trees to robust
lianas, with simple alternate distichous leaves and usually fragrant bark.
Flowers are typically trimerous, with a calyx and two petal whorls, numerous
stamens, and numerous free carpels in the common annonoid condition. The family
is one of the classic lowland tropical rainforest lineages.

## Distribution

The source treatment reports about 120 genera and c. 1,100 species worldwide in
the tropics. In Gabon it recognises a very large flora: 29 genera and 119
species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol16]]  
**Source**: `sources/Annonaceae_vol16_mineru`

This is a major early-series treatment and one of the largest family accounts in
the local OCR corpus. The mineru family split begins with a broad family essay
on habit, bark, inflorescence architecture, cauliflory, floral structure, and
generic affinities across tropical Africa.

## Notes

The treatment stresses Gabon as a stronghold for the family in lowland humid
forest and repeatedly compares the flora with southern Cameroon and the Congo
Basin. It also presents Annonaceae as one of the most natural and homogeneous
families in the flora, both morphologically and ecologically.
