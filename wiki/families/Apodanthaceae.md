# Apodanthaceae

**Authority**: (R.Br.) Tiegh. ex Takht. (1987)  
**Order**: Cucurbitales (modern placement)

## Diagnosis

Highly reduced holoparasites living inside the tissues of their hosts, with the
vegetative body reduced to a thalloid or mycelioid endophytic system. The only
visible structures are the small unisexual flowers and berry-like fruits. The
family is one of the most morphologically extreme parasitic lineages in the
flora.

## Distribution

Apodanthaceae comprises three genera and roughly 23 species, distributed in the
Americas, southwest Asia, Africa, and southwestern Australia. In Gabon the
treatment recognises a single genus with a single species.

## Treatment

**Volume**: [[vol40]]  
**Year**: 2010  
**Source**: `sources/Apodanthaceae_vol40_liteparse`

The Gabon treatment is small and focused on *Berlinianche aethiopica*. It is a
useful example of how the flora handles highly reduced parasitic plants in a
standard family-treatment format.

## Notes

The treatment states that Apodanthaceae was long submerged in a broad
Rafflesiaceae but that molecular evidence showed that concept to be polyphyletic.
The family is therefore treated as distinct in the modern flora.
