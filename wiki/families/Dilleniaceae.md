# Dilleniaceae

**Authority**: Salisb. (1807)
**Order**: Dilleniales (APG IV — sole family in the order)

A pantropical family of about **550 species in 10 or 11 genera**, with the
greatest diversity in **Australia**. In Gabon, Dilleniaceae are represented
by a single pantropical genus, [[Tetracera]], with **five species** of lianas.

## Diagnosis

Lianas or sarmentose shrubs, more rarely trees or suffrutices. Stipules
absent or sometimes present as narrow wings adnate to the petiole. Leaves
alternate, simple, petiolate or rarely subsessile; blade with entire or
dentate margin, **penninerved**.

**Inflorescences**: terminal or axillary, in racemes, cymes, thyrses, or
fascicles, rarely with solitary flowers; bracts and bracteoles often present.

**Flowers**: bisexual or rarely unisexual, **actinomorphic**; sepals (3)4 or
5(15), free, imbricate, **± accrescent**, often persistent, sometimes becoming
fleshy and enveloping the fruit; petals (1)4 or 5(7), free, imbricate or
crumpled in bud, yellow or white; stamens (1)15 to numerous, free or arranged
in fascicles. Ovary **superior**, with 2–20 carpels free or ± fused at base,
each with 1–20 ovules; styles as many as the carpels.

**Fruits**: follicular or bacciform, syncarpous or apocarpous, coriaceous or
fleshy, with 1 to numerous seeds.

**Seeds**: generally with a **lacerate or crested aril**, covering the seed
or not; endosperm abundant and fleshy; embryo small.

## Distribution

Tropical and subtropical regions worldwide, with the greatest diversity in
Australia. In Gabon, only the pantropical genus *Tetracera* is represented,
with five species of lianas.

## Treatments

### Vol 60 (2022) — Niangadouma R., Lachenaud O. & Sosef M.S.M.

**Authors**: Raoul Niangadouma (Herbier National du Gabon, IPHAMETRA/CENAREST,
Libreville); Olivier Lachenaud (Jardin botanique de Meise, Belgium / Université
Libre de Bruxelles); Marc S.M. Sosef (Jardin botanique de Meise).
**Pages**: 9–20 of Vol 60 (Sosef et al. eds.).
**Source**: `sources/Dilleniaceae_vol60_liteparse`.

The treatment recognises five species in Gabon (all in *Tetracera*), describes
one as new ([[Tetracera_breteleri]]), provides a comprehensive key
(incorporating two further species likely to be sought), and re-circumscribes
the *T. alnifolia* / *T. podotricha* complex with the latter as the widespread
Central African taxon and the former restricted in Gabon to coastal regions
along the Gulf of Guinea.

## Notes

The genus *Tetracera* is the **only Dilleniaceae** present in Gabon, despite
the family's much greater overall diversity worldwide. All Gabonese
Dilleniaceae are lianas of the genus *Tetracera*, often substantial enough
to be locally important "water lianas" — see the discussion on the
[[Tetracera]] page.
