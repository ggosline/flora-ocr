# Flore du Gabon — Overview

A rolling synthesis of what the wiki currently knows about the flora of Gabon
as a whole. Updated when an ingest changes the high-level picture, not on every
ingest. The detail lives on family/genus/species pages — this page is the bird's
eye view.

## Scope

The **Flore du Gabon** is a long-running monographic series treating the
vascular plants of Gabon, published in 60+ volumes. Volumes 1–37 (1961–2001)
were edited at the Muséum national d'Histoire naturelle, Paris under Aubréville
and successors; volumes 38+ (modern era) are co-edited by an international team
including Naturalis (Leiden), Meise Botanic Garden, and the Herbier National
du Gabon, and published by Margraf Publishers. Each volume covers one or more
plant families, treated by specialist authors.

The wiki ingests these treatments family-by-family and builds a structured
view of Gabon's flora as the corpus grows.

## Phylogenetic coverage

| Order | Families ingested | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-------|
| Alismatales | [[Alismataceae]] | Aquatic family represented in Gabon only by *Ranalisma*. |
| Apiales | [[Apiaceae]] | Extremely reduced in Gabon: only *Centella asiatica* is treated. |
| Arecales | [[Arecaceae]] | Major monocot expansion of the wiki: 11 genera and 34 species of palms and rattans in Gabon. |
| Asparagales | [[Aloaceae]], [[Anthericaceae]] | Succulent aloes plus geophytic *Chlorophytum*-centred herbs broaden monocot coverage. |
| Boraginales | [[Boraginaceae]] | A small but mainly woody Gabonese family, treated in the broad APG sense. |
| Buxales | [[Buxaceae]] | Represented in Gabon by a single species of *Buxus*. |
| Caryophyllales | [[Aizoaceae]], [[Ancistrocladaceae]] | Succulent coastal herbs in Aizoaceae contrast with hooked woody lianas in Ancistrocladaceae. |
| Cucurbitales | [[Apodanthaceae]], [[Begoniaceae]] | Includes both highly reduced endophytic parasites and the large fleshy-herb genus *Begonia*. |
| Dilleniales | [[Dilleniaceae]] | Sole family of the order; in Gabon represented only by [[Tetracera]] (5 spp.). |
| Dioscoreales | [[Burmanniaceae]] | Mix of green and mycoheterotrophic herbs. |
| Ericales | [[Ebenaceae]] | In Gabon represented only by [[Diospyros]]. Vol 18 treats 38 species overall, 30 recorded from Gabon; the wiki also carries regional comparator taxa and later article addenda, including [[Diospyros_kupensis]]. |
| Gentianales | [[Apocynaceae]] | Very large latex-bearing family; currently represented on the wiki at family level from Vol 59 part 1. |
| Magnoliales | [[Annonaceae]] | One of the largest rainforest family treatments in the corpus; 29 genera and 119 species reported for Gabon. |
| Piperales | [[Aristolochiaceae]] | In Gabon centred on *Pararistolochia*, a lianescent lineage with petaloid calyces. |
| Ranunculales | [[Menispermaceae]], [[Ranunculaceae]] | Both Gabonese Ranunculales families now ingested. Menispermaceae is the diversity hotspot of the order in Gabon (22 genera, 34–36 spp.); Ranunculaceae is represented only by [[Clematis]] (2 spp.). The two are not sister to each other — Ranunculaceae is sister to Berberidaceae, and Menispermaceae is the next branch out (APG IV). |
| Santalales | [[Balanophoraceae]] | Represented by root holoparasites, chiefly *Thonningia* in Gabon. |
| Sapindales | [[Anacardiaceae]] | Resin-bearing woody flora including both indigenous taxa and some naturalised fruit trees. |

The wiki now spans a much broader structural and phylogenetic range than the
initial liana-heavy seed set. The new family-level ingest adds aquatics
([[Alismataceae]]), succulents ([[Aizoaceae]], [[Aloaceae]]), palms
([[Arecaceae]]), woody resin-bearing Sapindales ([[Anacardiaceae]]), broad-sense
woody Boraginales ([[Boraginaceae]]), and several parasitic lineages
([[Apodanthaceae]], [[Balanophoraceae]], partly also [[Burmanniaceae]]).
Detailed genus/species coverage is still concentrated in the earlier deep
ingests (Vols. 18 and 60), but the family layer now gives a much more
representative map of the flora.

## Recurring habitats

These habitat types appear repeatedly in distribution data on ingested
species pages and are likely to recur as the wiki grows:

- Riverine forest (galleries, periodically flooded forest)
- Terra firma forest (often on sandy soils)
- Forest edges and gaps (heliophilic colonisers)
- Coastal forest behind mangroves and savannas
- Inselbergs and ridges

## Patterns observed

*(emerging from the ingested treatments — refined as more families come in)*

- **Many monospecific genera in Menispermaceae**: of 22 Gabonese genera,
  **16 are monospecific in the country** and several (*Beirnaertia*,
  *Chasmanthera*, *Dialytheca*, *Leptoterantha*, *Limaciopsis*,
  *Sarcolophium*, *Syntriandrium*) are monospecific worldwide. This reflects
  a family-level pattern of small, often relictual, central African genera.
- **Active novelty production in Vol 60**: 6 new species described in a
  single volume — 1 in [[Dilleniaceae]] ([[Tetracera_breteleri]]) and 5 in
  [[Menispermaceae]] ([[Albertisia_badia]], [[Albertisia_mouilaensis]],
  [[Synclisia_oligogyna]], [[Triclisia_gabonensis]],
  [[Triclisia_megacarpa]]). All 6 type localities are in Gabon.
- **Ebony and tannin-pocket morphology**: the Vol 18 Ebenaceae treatment adds
  a very different diagnostic toolkit to the wiki: bark and heartwood colour,
  accrescent calyces, and the recurrent leaf tannin pockets ("glands") are now
  central characters across 38 species pages.
- **Post-volume article addenda matter**: the first journal-article ingest,
  [[Diospyros_kupensis]], is a 1998 Mt Kupe endemic from Southwest Cameroon.
  It is regionally relevant because its protologue compares it directly with
  [[Diospyros_conocarpa]] and shows that species discovery in Central African
  *Diospyros* continued after Vol 18.
- **Female flowers unknown**: a recurring data gap in Menispermaceae —
  reported for [[Dialytheca_gossweileri]], [[Syrrheonema_fasciculatum]],
  [[Tiliacora_gabonensis]] and others. Breteler explicitly notes "about ten
  species remain poorly known, mostly because of missing female (or
  sometimes male) flowering material."
- **Endemism**: at least 9 endemic Menispermaceae species in Gabon
  (*Albertisia* spp., *Synclisia oligogyna*, *Tiliacora gabonensis*,
  *Triclisia gabonensis*, *T. hypochrysea*, plus undescribed entities) —
  the wiki should eventually spin out a [[topics/endemics_of_gabon]] page.
